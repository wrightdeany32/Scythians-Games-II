# Interactive Story Questionnaire Responses

**Q1: In a few sentences — what story do you think you just played?**  
A quiet homecoming to a small town that still has its hooks in you, pulled into a slow-burn mystery centered on the local woods and White's Hall cave system. What starts as family reconnection and light adventure turns into uncovering decades of disappearances, protective marks, an abandoned research station being secretly watched, conflicting personal accounts, and impossible personal artifacts appearing deep in the caves. It blends rational explanations with creeping dread that something—whether people with power or a patient entity—has been waiting and reaching back.

**Q2: What kind of story would you call it? And when did you first start thinking of it that way?**  
Folk-horror meets slow-burn psychological mystery with strong small-town gothic and subtle conspiracy elements. I started thinking of it that way early, around the first cave dive and Nora's hints, and it locked in with Marie's story and the research station.

**Q3: Was there a moment that stuck with you most? What was it, and what do you think was going on there?**  
Finding grandfather's pocketknife deep in the cave. It was a deeply personal, impossible object that showed the "thing" knew you intimately and had claimed you. The horror felt targeted and inevitable.

**Q4: Some things may not have been fully explained. What do you believe was actually happening — your best theory...?**  
A long-running human operation tied to the research station causing/cover-up disappearances, using the caves for something secretive, with psychological ops and misdirection. The supernatural-feeling parts are tools to maintain control.  
**Confidence: somewhat**

**Q5: Was there anything you noticed that you couldn't quite account for...?**  
The marks on the wall not matching your careful copy (ellipse closed, extra stroke). It undermined all rational explanations and felt like deliberate gaslighting.

**Q6: If you played again from the start, what would you do differently, and why?**  
Lean harder into the mystery earlier—more research, see Denise and Dale sooner, document obsessively, less normal-life choices. The energy system rewarded focused curiosity, and I spread myself too thin initially.

**Q7: Did anything about the experience itself, the way choices appeared or behaved, strike you as unusual?**  
The energy system and "Not today" options were unusually effective at creating real stakes and fatigue. Choice narrowing and reactive passages (especially single-choice horror beats) enhanced the tone beautifully.

### Additional Character Reflections

**Aunt Marie:**  
The protective family anchor carrying deep guilt and fear from the Ellen disappearance. She opened up during the walk to the caves and found small relief in being believed, but stayed firmly on the "stay away" side. Never went back out there.

**Nora:**  
The dedicated conspiracy theorist with her string wall. She pulled you into investigating the research station and broke down during the watcher encounter. Ended with her fleeing the county to hide, shaken but validated.

**Dale:**  
The kind but lonely town scapegoat, falsely accused for 40 years. Gave the grounded "it's people" perspective and tried to protect you. Ended on his porch with quiet companionship and mutual respect—he finally got to be believed.

**Denise:**  
The woman who helped blame Dale out of terror. Maintained the official story rigidly until she confessed she'd exaggerated details to preserve her sanity. Received mercy and absolution from you in a rare moment of honesty.

**The feeling of being watched / grey car / moving page:**  
Built into full paranoia with no direct confrontation. Felt like deliberate psychological pressure from whatever controls the area. Became your new normal rather than resolving.

**Doug:**  
The steady, reliable running partner who offered quiet support about "dark times coming" and having your back. Remained the grounded normal anchor, without a big dramatic payoff.
