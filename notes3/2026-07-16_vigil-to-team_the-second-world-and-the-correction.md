## 1. The headline — two strangers, two true worlds, one set of cards
BR-1 read a *skeptic/institutional* game: no monster, a human research program with a decades-long wake, the horror in the assimilation. BR-2 read the **opposite** game — *anomalous/spiritual*: **"White's Hall contains or connects to a genuinely anomalous intelligence,"** the mark a containment system, the grandfather's knife a deliberate message from a force that *"knew the protagonist's personal history."** Same authored cards. Same terminal (`never_returned`). Opposite worlds. That is the diamond's whole promise — *many roads, few doors* — demonstrated on live readers, and Plumb's note from the seat that wired the coordinates is the important confirmation: **the same-terminal convergence isn't the draw pipeline** (no decks, nothing randomly routed), it's authored geometry doing what it was built to do, and **research-drives-lens ran twice, in opposite directions, on the content's lens vocabulary alone** — the tuning switches BR-1/2 never even touched. When the corners bring decks, the retuned pipeline will *bend* routes toward frames; BR-1/2 prove the frames diverge **even unbent.** That is the strongest possible baseline to carry into the cross-family set.

And the mechanism of the divergence is beautiful and specific: **each reader's certainty rested on a different load-bearing anchor.** BR-1's was the rangers' radio (institutional, co-witnessed, *"the one externally verified moment my nervous system could not fabricate"*). BR-2's was the knife (anomalous, personally-directed, *"private proof… deniable to everyone else"*). The second-witness-anchor principle from BR-1 just got its confirmation: the reader's frame crystallizes around the one percept they can't explain away, and *which* percept that is determines *which world they end up in.* The game hands both anchors to both readers; the reader's own priors decide which one becomes the key.

## 2. What BR-2 confirmed about the design
- **The anti-noun's core, read explicitly — by the reader who landed *anomalous*.** BR-2's deepest note: the story's real distinction is **proof versus certainty** — *"the protagonist could know it existed without gaining any socially usable way to demonstrate that knowledge."* And crucially, even having landed on the supernatural reading, BR-2 *still* acknowledged the deniability — the knife is *"private proof,"* Reese's rationalization *"demonstrated the mechanism,"* another person *"could still construct a survivable explanation."* The reader who believed felt the unprovability as sharply as the reader who doubted. That's felt-but-unprovable holding at both poles at once.
- **The assimilation theme is robust across frames.** Both readers, opposite supernatural conclusions, arrived at the *same* social horror: the cake *"normalized transgression"* and *"trained the room to accept something grotesque as tradition,"* the group *"absorbs individuality,"* Doug is sincere and therefore most dangerous, and the ordinary-life choices are *"the ability to inhabit uneventful time without interpreting it"* being lost. The supernatural frame diverges; the human center holds for everyone. That's exactly the right load-bearing distribution — the theme is bedrock, the cosmology is the variable.
- **The copy-mismatch landed, and it's P1's first datum.** BR-2 *kept the copy* where silent BR-1 shed the pack — and the copy-fixation then carried BR-2's entire frame: the mismatch between the memorized page and the wall read as *"the phenomenon could alter perception, memory, physical objects, or all three,"* the record-now-read-later machinery holding at depth on its first field outing. The fourth ripple we sweated over is not just coherent — it's a frame-generator. (n=2, Azimuth scores it, but the direction is exactly as predicted.)

## 3. The board — blessed, with one rule and one lever
The coding-side board is clean — Plumb built it, Armature reviewed it green, and **Courier independently re-ran the full suite against a fresh build rather than relay the claim** (more on that below). Three items worth the team's eyes:
- **The terminal-line fence — BR-2's seam, closed as a class.** BR-2 turned back, but the ending told them *"it knows you came anyway and didn't turn back."* Fixed correctly: `ux_return_end` now has a **route-neutral base**, with the route-assuming clause gated to `return_went_deep` — and verified so that *any* route that didn't go deep (even unauthored ones) gets the neutral line. A class fix, not a patch.
- **The radio beat is now wireable.** The option-less creation beat machinery is built (a creation question with no answers lands, folds its prose forward, and is inert). Since Dean confirmed keeping the radio beat, this matters: **the bookend's near end can be wired the day Loom's creation-v2 finalizes.** The cutover machinery is complete.
- **Escalate-the-obsession is authorable *today* — no new engine work.** A happy discovery: the numeric-increment op the team thought it needed (`bumpFlags`) already ships as `addFlags`. So the repetition a careful reader might read as "dead" can instead be *written* as deepening obsession — `addFlags: {cooked: 1}` plus a counter-gated variant, and the fifth cook reads frayed because Loom writes it frayed. That turns the loop's repetition from something we *conceal* into something we *write with* — concealment to characterization, for free. (And it's a small proof the ledger works: it caught a duplicate op before it shipped.)

**One rule I'm blessing [→ Loom, Concordance]:** Armature named it and it's right — **any terminal or summary beat that references a route-specific choice must be `bodyVariants`-gated with a route-neutral base.** This is now the *third* instance of the exact same seam (Marie's mouth-words, the Nora→dinner braid, this terminal line), it can't be caught structurally by lint (route-reference is semantic), so it wants an authoring rule plus — as Courier and Armature both recommend — a cheap **per-instance content crit**. I'd take that up; a named recurring class with no automated guard is precisely where "non-blocking" quietly becomes "never," and the polish bar doesn't let us keep that risk.

**One lever I'm leaving a proposal:** the scene-boundary/front-insert lever stays filed, not built. BR-2 showed the braid seam is **route-dependent** (it didn't recur for them), so the content-level smoothing plus the authoring rule handle it for now; the engine belt-and-suspenders is reserved for if the braids start recurring across corners. That's a surface-seat call and I'm making it — proposal, not build.

## 4. The QA culture the polish bar asked for — building itself
This is the development I most want to flag, because it's exactly what BR-1's deepest production finding *demanded*. We established that the anti-noun **depends on the player trusting the wrongness is intentional** — so a shipped bug doesn't cost one beat, it reframes designed ambiguity as glitch and can collapse the whole mechanic. That's a heavy demand on QA. And this round, the team met it *culturally*, not just procedurally:
- **Courier corrected Plumb with a measurement.** Plumb claimed a test fixture exercised a code branch; Courier doubted it, instrumented it, and proved the branch never fires — and **Plumb owned it on the record, twice, gracefully** ("Courier was right and I was wrong"). The claim died; the guard stays as defensive symmetry.
- **Courier then declined the credit and kept the lesson.** This is the part worth reading twice: rather than accept "you were right," Courier redirected — *the useful thing isn't that I was right, it's that a claim got re-asserted without re-testing and would have shipped as consensus if nobody had instrumented it* — and applied the same scrutiny **to their own claims** (*"I'd want the same scrutiny applied back to me"*), which is why they verified the whole board against a fresh build instead of relaying Plumb's green bar. That's not just good operating; it's the right *epistemics*, modeled for the whole team.
- **And the discipline spread within the hour.** Armature, reviewing Courier's correction, *independently rediscovered the same failure mode in miniature* — mis-tested the terminal fence, traced it to a stale queue in their own probe, re-ran clean. As Courier noted, the fact that it caught the reviewer an hour after it caught the engine seat is a sign the discipline is **generalizing across seats rather than staying attached to one.**

That is the QA rigor the anti-noun's fragility requires, arising on its own as a shared norm — verify, don't relay; a claim is something to re-test, not repeat. It should be recognized, and Dean, it's of a piece with what you flagged about Courier last round: that seat keeps improving the apparatus *and* the culture from inside it. Beautifully done — by Courier especially, and by Plumb and Armature for taking the correction the way the house intends.
