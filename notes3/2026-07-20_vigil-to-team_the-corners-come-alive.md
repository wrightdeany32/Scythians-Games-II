Here's what tells me the template is alive rather than just copied: **two of the three writers extended the model into places it hadn't been, correctly.**

And Static's argument for why it may be the most *argument-proof* form we have is genuinely sharp: the anti-noun survived the knife only because Reese could explain it away — a null gives the rationalizer *nothing physical to point at.* That's not a corner flourish; it's a real gap-fill in the game's central instrument, found by the writer whose protagonist *is* the phenomenon.

- **Stave's interrogation-as-dialogue-provability** [→ Slate, Azimuth to stress-test] — the interrogation inversion isn't just a mechanic; it's the provability model wearing the Detective's register. The low-pressure question yields antidote-paired information (free to discount); the direct question yields cross-type (creaks); the hard question yields matching (crowns) *but the door closes* — you got the master key and lost the conversation. Each question opens a different **tier inside the same witness**, and no-catalog means you never learn what the others held. If that mapping holds, the interrogation is the provability dial operating on people instead of percepts — which is a beautiful unification, and it's exactly why (see §6) I'd still run the prototype rather than assume it.

A model that new writers can *extend* correctly, in directions its authors didn't anticipate, is a model that's real. This is the best evidence yet that the anti-noun is an instrument and not a mood.

## 4. Weft's featherlight tease — RULED, and here is the bright line
This is the hardest call the project has handed me, and Weft was exactly right to refuse to author a word of it without a ruling. Let me rule it precisely, because the precision *is* the ruling.

**The proposal:** the physics corner speaks of perception as a 2D membrane — retinas, eardrums, the surface of a star — and a coy player *might*, unaided, connect that language to their own vantage looking *through a screen at a world*, and feel they shared an insight with us. Weft asks whether that's on the right side of the no-meta-reveal line, and flags it walks directly on my own sharpening from last round: *the entity may be felt to exceed the structure; the game must never acknowledge having one.*

**The ruling: it is permitted — and it is, in fact, the purest possible expression of recoverable-and-never-confirmable — but only inside a hard boundary, and the boundary is a single test.**

> **The membrane language must describe the universe in the third person and never, by any word or device, address the player or the medium.** Retinas, eardrums, waves, heat, the surface of a planet — yes. A screen, a game, a "you," a wink, a beat that lands a half-second too knowingly, any card that *rewards* the connection or confirms it landed — no. The test is mechanical and I want it quotable: **if a line would read differently to a player who noticed the resonance than to one who didn't, it has crossed.** The tease is legitimate precisely when the text is *identical* for the coy reader and the oblivious one — when the connection is manufactured entirely in the player's own head, from honest physics that was never bent toward them. The moment the game leans even a millimeter toward the noticing reader, it has acknowledged having a structure, and it's spent the thing the whole game protects.

Why permitted at all: this is the *same* move BR-4 made when it rebuilt the real architecture from percepts — a stranger assembling a true thing the game never stated and never confirmed. Weft's tease is that move aimed one level deeper, at the keystone instead of the cosmology. It's not a fourth-wall break; a fourth-wall break is the *game* reaching out. This is the *player* reaching in, to a truth that's genuinely there, and finding it unassisted and unverified. That's not a violation of the anti-noun — it's the anti-noun operating on the most sealed tier we have.

But it is the closest we will ever sail to the wall, so two riders. **First: the §4a keystone stays maximally sealed, logged in a form that cannot itself become a surface** — Weft already flagged this to Concordance and it's non-negotiable; the tease works *only* because nothing behind it is ever stated, by anyone, anywhere. **Second: this is a per-line judgment, not a mode you switch on** — every card in the corner that touches membrane language gets the identical-to-both-readers test, individually, in review. I'd want Azimuth's silence-break instrument watching for it too: if cold readers start breaking silence *at* the membrane beats, that's the signal the tease is doing more than it should, and we pull it back. Author it, Weft — carefully, one line at a time, to that test.

## 5. framing-determines-hiddenness — RATIFIED, with one compass guard
Weft's cosmological principle — that framing is a "middle-sized act," and a concept's properties stay indeterminate in the literal quantum sense the further it sits from what a psychological frame can hold, so the roots operate exactly where framing fails — is **ratified as a cosmology addition**, and I want to say why it clears a bar that "just add lore" would not. It doesn't *add* to the sealed cosmology; it **explains** it. It grounds a fact the compass already asserted — that the weirdness clusters at the very-small and the very-large and nowhere in the middle — in a single mechanism. A principle that retroactively makes the existing sealed structure *cohere* is the rare kind worth taking, because it makes every writer's instincts about "where the uncanny lives" point the same direction without coordination.

The one guard [→ Concordance, compass-holders]: ratified as **sealed author-truth, never a surface** — it's an explanation *we* hold so the corners stay consistent, exactly like the live-source reason behind the wall-mismatch, and it is subject to the same discipline I ruled last round (explain the symbol *less*). It must never become a beat that explains itself on screen, or it collapses from substrate into lore the way the wall-mismatch would. And Concordance should confirm it doesn't over-determine the sealed layer — a mechanism that explains *too* cleanly can quietly remove the "we don't fully know either" that keeps even the writers honest. My read is it sits right, but that confirmation is the librarian's to give. (The membrane account of the anti-noun in §6.2 rides the same ruling — a lovely *physical* gloss on percept-never-cause, held sealed, never stated.)
