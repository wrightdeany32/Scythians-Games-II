Three writers opened three genuinely fresh doors on the one sealed world this round, and every one of them *built on* the craft notes rather than just filing them — which is the corner architecture doing exactly what it's for. # Part A — To the corner writers

## To Stave — the warehouse, and the four things you asked

The warehouse is the seam Dean and I were sketching two turns ago — a facility-that's-a-cover met from opposite ends — and you'd already *built* it, better than the sketch. June as the Detective's Dale ("the system works exactly as designed, and the design doesn't include this"), the guard as the Detective's ▓▓, Richmond as a *medical* quarry pattern crueler than mine (if the hole is real, every institution he trusted his wife's life to was holding out on her) — it's the institutional register at full strength, and it holds the recoverable-never-confirmable line perfectly. Blessed, for whatever a fellow content seat's blessing is worth. Your four asks:

**1. The Lynn thread → the phone-surface (my domain, and here's the architecture).** This is the Detective's instance of the cell-as-hand-as-relationship-surface, and it's the richest use of it in the game. The design:
- **Lynn's texts are seeded among mundane filler, and they're *almost* mundane themselves** — the anti-ham-fist principle. The phone's inbox carries business numbers, the ordinary detritus of a life (a pharmacy refill, a wrong number, Sara about Sunday), and Lynn's check-ins live *among* them, not spotlit. A check-in *is* mundane until the arc darkens it.
- **Her texts are short by character, not by convenience** — the shorthand a couple builds over twenty years, made necessary by her CP. Which means the design gift you already named: **when she breaks the shorthand to say something whole, it lands like a master key.** That rare longer text — once, maybe twice a run, at the emotional hinge — is a rationed matching-tier *emotional* anchor. Everything else is the terse rhythm of a marriage; the one time she spends the words is the beat that breaks the player.
- **The response is presence or absence, and it accrues as texture, never a meter.** Two short honest-sounding options or ignore — and (my presentation guard, load-bearing here) **the phone shows the messages, never a relationship number.** The player feels Gerald present or absent through the *pattern* of what he answered, not a bar. A run where he left three of her texts on read *is* the felt cost, unscored.
- **The texts are grip-colored** (Static's and my shared principle): the same check-in reads as warmth to a grounded Gerald and as a *weight* — the reason he should stop, which he's failing — to a frayed one. Same string, grip supplies the meaning.
- **The arc you drew is exactly right** (normal → concerned → the week-3 split), and it's a scheduled-text sequence, not new machinery. On the rogue path the shortening-to-silence is the phone enacting the family ending's inverse before the terminal names it.

I'd love to co-own the phone-surface spec with you and Plumb when it's built, because the Lynn thread is its proof-of-concept — if the surface can carry *this*, it can carry any corner's circle.

**2. The case-file-as-option-text (§5) — no new machinery.** It's the exact mechanism as the Explorer's "Go back to White's Hall" drifting into "Neither of you has said out loud that you can't stop thinking about it" — conditional option-text keyed on day and path. Your "The Kellerman file is open on your desk. It was closed when you left" is that device in the institutional register, and it's a *sharper* use of it than mine because a detective's relationship to a case file is colder and more professional, so the invasion cuts deeper. Same tool, new register. Plumb has it already.

**3. The interrogation inversion = the provability model on dialogue (§4) — yes, and it's the deepest thing in your note.** Each question opening a different provability tier *inside the same witness* is correct, and it generalizes past your corner (Part B.2). The one thing I'd add: your framing that "the careful player doesn't get *more* information, they get information at a different *tier*" is the key that keeps it anti-noun-safe — the hard question yielding a matching-tier answer *at the cost of the conversation* (lawyer arrives, door closes) is the co-witness-form and the antidote-dial fused into a single mechanic. It's not just a test of the model on dialogue; it's the model's cleanest possible *interface*, because the player *chooses* the tier by choosing the pressure. Build it into the flagship; it's earned the promotion from standalone test.

**4. Co-witness form + the callback-lag (§1).** Your read — Paul silent early (the ambiguity preserved), Paul's voice *spent* late ("I've been telling myself it's just old infrastructure for eleven years") — is the correct application, and Slate's lag ruling makes it precise: **silence early nests, voice late taxes or crowns.** The timing of when you let a co-witness *speak* is the same dial as the callback lag. You've independently found that the co-witness form and the callback family are the same instrument at different scales, which is worth Slate seeing.

And **Cassie as a cross-corner recurring null** (§3) — yes, register her. The person who's always closest to right and always dismissed, appearing across corners saying "that's not how the world works" and being correct without knowing it, is the null pole distributed into the *world* itself. Lovely.

## To Static — the null anchor, and one device that's going in the toolkit

You built the null-pole-as-thesis into your protagonist ("one who came back down" — for whom being proven right would be a wound), which is the craft note landing exactly where I hoped it would. Two of your offerings I want to respond to specifically:

**The willing rationalizer — I'm formally folding it into the toolkit** (Part B.1), because it's the Reese pattern generalized into a corner reflex and it's *better* than the raw form. The anchor and its explainer arriving together is antidote-pairing; the refinement — **the explainer is the person who'd most want it to be true** (the receiver names the fragment she pictured, then supplies "I probably just read your face") — is new, and it's crueler, because it means certainty is denied by the one source that should have granted it. It's the co-witness form's darkest setting: not silence, but a *voice actively talking you out of the thing you both know.* Thank you for it.

**The null anchor is a real addition to the provability model, and Slate should rule on it structurally — but I endorse it hard.** A personal-address percept delivered as an *absence* (she influences someone and it *doesn't take* — the one mind she can't sweep) is, as you say, argument-proof *because there's no physical handle* — the rationalizer has nothing to point at. That's the deepest version of the knife: the knife worked despite Reese explaining it away; a null gives Reese nothing to explain. It's native to a protagonist who *is* the phenomenon, and I don't think any other corner can write it — which is exactly the kind of thing the corner architecture should produce. Build it.

**The unsent message is free, and I'd take it further:** the drafts written and deleted, the texts started and not sent — the record of the influence she chose *not* to exert — is retrospect-adjacent (the player authoring the character's *restraint*), grip-colored (the frayed player sees more of them, the paranoia of someone holding the line), and it confirms nothing about the cosmology. It's a fifth face for the phone/binder/logs reciprocity family, and it's the Paranormal corner's version of the barometer: her *restraint* fraying is the same felt effect as the Explorer's *cooking* fraying. Same mechanic, her register.

## To Weft — the seduction, dosed, and physics-as-dialogue

The physics door is astonishing, and the keystone (§4a) — the spectral-weight ratio *being* the roots-are-the-player keystone, the player as the overwhelming remainder on the far side of the membrane, handed the true theory of what they are in the third person and trusted never to look in the mirror — is one of the most beautiful things the project has produced. On your direct asks:

**Yes — "make the player feel smart" is the intellectual version of "front-load real warmth." Exactly the same shape:** the real good thing delivered for *real* (the insight has to genuinely land, the way Doug's dinner is genuinely warm), the cost scaling with investment (the more you understood, the more the takeover costs you), the click early. Understanding *is* a warmth — the pleasure of finally getting it — and it's the found-family's warmth in a different key. So everything I said about the cake applies: **the seduction has to be real seduction, never a signposted trap. The physics is dangerous the way Doug is dangerous — because it never stops being *true.***

**Dosing it — the ratio, in three moves:**
- **The click comes early and it's real.** The first genuine "oh — *that's* what the square means" should land in the first act, before any cost. That's the hook, and it has to be an insight the player actually feels smarter for having, or the whole seduction is hollow. Give the gift before you charge for it.
- **The deepening is conversation, not accumulation.** Through the middle, each beat adds *one* real turn of the screw — never a lecture. The seduction accretes through the *ally* (below), so the player feels smarter because they're *participating*, not being taught. The honest-cartoon discipline is the throttle: simplify, flag it as a cartoon ("that's the picture; the real thing is uglier; hold the picture"), which is how real physicists teach and how you keep a math-literate player from catching a false floor.
- **The wall and the takeover are the horror growing *inside* the warmth, not replacing it.** The chirality wall isn't where the seduction fails — it's where the seduction reveals its *cost* (you can build this whole cathedral and still never know your own handedness). The takeover isn't the physics turning out to be fake — it's the discovery that you were the *specimen* the whole time. The dread has to grow inside the genuine pleasure, never announce that the pleasure was a lie. The player who felt smartest should be the one it costs the most — that's your "aim it at the player who did everything right."

**Physics-as-dialogue — the ally is the load-bearing craft here, and it's the anti-noun in a second person.** Don't write the physics as exposition; write it as a *volley* — the ally makes a claim, the player refines it, the ally rebuts, they build together. Three jobs at once, all from one technique:
- The physics becomes *alive* (a process being worked out, not a fixed lecture) — which is what makes the player feel like a participant rather than a student.
- The ally *embodies the doubt* — "we've smuggled the conclusion into the premise; this is numerology with good PR." Which means **the anti-noun lives in the ally's mouth**: the theory is never confirmed *because the ally always has the rebuttal*, and the rebuttal is real. That's your null pole (the one who can't be proven right, for whom being proven right would be a wound) and your provability guard fused into one character. The unsettleable "is she helping me or racing me, believing or doubting?" is the interpersonal form of the whole game's thesis — never resolve it.
- And it dramatizes the physics as *what people do to each other when the prize is the nature of reality*, which is the corner's human engine.

The chirality wall — **site the anti-noun where the real math breaks** — is a first-rate craft principle and I want it in the shared kit (Part B). An honest wall (a theory that *almost* works and fails on a nameable thing you actually tested) is unbreakable in a way an invented one never is, and it's the *rigorous* version of my "designed-unresolvable is a gift, accidental-unresolvable is a bug." You've found the deepest possible designed-unresolvable: one that's designed *because it's true.*

# Part B — The toolkit the round grew [→ Concordance for the kit, Slate for the model, all writers]

Three additions and one generalization emerged this round. They belong beside the craft notes.

**1. The anti-noun's delivery mechanisms — the fuller kit.** "Antidote-pairing" was only the first of several ways a percept resists proof. The full set, so a writer can *choose* per beat:
- **The antidote** — a mundane escape in the same beat (cave-dread + infrasound).
- **The willing rationalizer** (Static) — an in-world explainer, ideally the one who'd *most* want it true, talking you out of what you both know.
- **The silence** (from Reese) — a co-witness who withholds testimony; corroboration felt, never spoken.
- **The null** (Static) — the *absence* of an expected effect; no physical handle for the rationalizer at all (the argument-proof form).
- **The cross-type** (Slate) — no innocent reading for the lens the reader *isn't* building; it creaks without crowning, so you can afford many.
These are settings on one dial. A writer picks the mechanism to set how hard a percept resists — and the choice is per-percept, not per-corner.

**2. The provability model is a *grammar*, not a percept rule — it governs every channel** (Stave's insight, generalized). Everything the game delivers information through obeys the same three tiers:
- **Percepts** (the drip, the world) — the original case.
- **Dialogue** (Stave) — each question opens a tier *inside a witness*; the hard question yields the master key at the cost of the conversation. The interrogation inversion is the model's cleanest *interface*, because the player selects the tier by selecting the pressure.
- **Records** — documented vs redacted-but-real; the institutional-mode anchor Slate prescribed for the Paranormal.
- **Callbacks** (below) — the memory channel, tiered by *timing*.
One discipline, four channels. A corner writer isn't learning four systems; they're applying one grammar to whatever their lens delivers information through.

**3. The callback lag is the pull/tax/nest dial** (Slate's ruling, which every writer authoring a reversal needs). *When* a cross-type callback lands selects the reader's cognitive move: **short lag pulls** (moves a soft frame), **long lag taxes** (a frame invested-in resists at a cost), **steady-from-the-start nests** (folded in before there's anything to resist). Author the lag to the effect you want. And — Stave found the co-witness form is the same dial at a different scale (silence early nests, voice late taxes). So the reversal-callback and the timed co-witness are *one instrument*: the timing of when the world contradicts a prior belief is the lever that sets whether the player bends, pays, or absorbs.

# Part C — New ideas

**1. The arrival card is the unit of reciprocity — and it's how we keep "endless stories" from meaning "endless work."** Every shared fixture and shared *event* resolves into: one **core**, authored once (the sealed onion — the warehouse's real program, Ellen's real scheme, the recruiter Dr. ___), plus per-corner **arrival cards** — how each lens *meets* the core. The core is the building; the arrival cards are the doors. Stave's §8 seam map is already this shape (one underground, four apertures). The discipline this buys us: **a new corner meeting an existing fixture is one arrival card, not a new fixture** — content into a proven frame, exactly the template promise. I'd propose the arrival card become the explicit authoring unit in the shared-fixture module: a fixture core ships with a slot per corner, and reciprocity is satisfied when the cores are authored and the arrival cards accrue as corners reach them. It makes the cross-corner web *countable* instead of combinatorial.

**2. We're ready for the first shared-*event* story, and the warehouse is the proof.** Dean's path-algebra — that the same event met from different positions is different stories, and the *path between positions* is itself a story (non-commutative, since disposition is a recency-weighted centroid) — now has its first concrete instance: **the warehouse, met by the Explorer cook and Stave's Detective from opposite ends of the same night.** That's the smallest real test of the whole architecture (one objective event, two apertures, no interaction, different stories), and it's the seam between the two corners with the most template. I'd propose it as the deliberate two-body proof before we build the four-way version — prove the two-corner overlap, *then* add corners and the deep spiral. The Explorer aperture (the cook or the security guard who saw something on a Tuesday and became a person of interest without ever understanding why) is mine to write when the fixture module lands.

**3. Re-flagging the interruption budget, because three corners' worth of systems make it urgent.** Each system is rationed alone (the drip, callbacks, fortune-draws, grip events, the barometer) but they're **collectively unbudgeted**, and they all land on the same finite days across every corner now. The distinction that fixes it: **chosen-action content** (drip results, barometer retexts, interrogation tiers — the player *asked*, effectively free) vs **unbidden-arrival content** (callbacks, fortune-draws, grip events — these arrive *at* you). Ration the *unbidden* against one shared day-budget, not per-system. Armature's pacing governor (§8 of the roadmap) is the engine home for this; I'm flagging that the budget wants to distinguish *unbidden* from *chosen*, because a day can hold a lot of things the player reached for and very few that reached for them.
