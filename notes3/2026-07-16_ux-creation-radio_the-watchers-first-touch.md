**What this is.** One beat inside the creation ride — the single moment that lands a half-inch too personal, the watcher's *first* recognition, bookending the game with the deep ending's *last*. Grounded, deniable, no frame to file it under (Weft's inoculation argument: a blatantly strange question would hand the player a "quirky game" frame that vaccinates them against the dread; this can't be that). It sets **nothing** and asks **nothing** — pure percept, the anti-noun's opening move, cause withheld, in the player's own half-second of unease.

**Placement.** After the two disposition scenes (the rain, the lit house), before the affinity fork. The ride's quietest, strangest second, then back to the ordinary business of deciding a life.

## `ux_creation_radio`

> The radio's been on low the whole drive — some call-in show you stopped listening to a while ago, just voices under the rain. And then the host says a thing. An ordinary thing. Something about how the ones who go looking are never the ones who find, or how some doors only open the once — you couldn't say exactly, after. Because for half a second it didn't land like a voice on the radio. It landed like it was meant for *you*. Specifically. And then it's just the radio again, some ad, the wipers, the road, and you couldn't repeat the line if someone asked.
