## 1. The reconciliation — why BR-1 anchored on a *shared* percept and BR-2 on a *solo* one

There's an apparent contradiction between the two reads, and resolving it is the whole point. **BR-1** taught us: *co-witnessed percepts are the confidence anchor; solo ones stay discountable* — the rangers' radio (co-witnessed) was BR-1's master key, everything solo stayed deniable. But **BR-2's** anchor was the **knife** — a *solo, personal* percept (only the player ever got it) — and it became BR-2's master key anyway: *"private proof… it knew the protagonist's personal history."* So BR-2 seems to break the BR-1 rule: how is a solo percept load-bearing?

The reconciliation: **the anchor-*type* is lens-specific.** Each lens has its own *mode of proof* — its own kind of percept it can't explain away:
- The **institutional / skeptic** lens proves by **external corroboration** — *someone else saw it.* The rangers' radio, co-witnessed. BR-1 (institutional) latched this.
- The **paranormal** lens proves by **personal address** — *it was directed at me.* The knife knew my history. BR-2 (anomalous) latched this — a **solo** percept, load-bearing precisely because it's *directed*, not because it's corroborated.

The game hands **both** anchors to **both** readers; the reader's **lens** decides which one becomes the key — which is exactly why one set of cards produced two opposite worlds. So the confidence-anchor was never monolithically "co-witnessed" — that's the *institutional* version; the paranormal version is personal-address, and it's solo. The two datasets don't conflict. They show two anchor-types, one per lens.

## 2. The complete model — three layers [→ Concordance's toolkit, building on Loom's §3]

Loom put two of the three layers in the toolkit; the lens-specific anchor-type is the third, and together they're a complete account of *how provable the unprovable feels* — the dial the entire anti-noun rides on:

1. **Antidote-pairing** *(Loom)* — does the percept come with a mundane escape? A percept pre-paired with its counter-explanation (cave-dread + infrasound) is discountable; a percept with **no antidote** (the radio) is a candidate anchor. → controls **discountability**.
2. **Anchor-type, lens-specific** *(this note)* — among the non-discountable percepts, *which one a reader claims* is keyed to their lens's mode of proof: **external-corroboration** (institutional), **personal-address** (paranormal), and by extension **documented / on-the-record** (Detective — the percept the *record* proves) and **redacted-but-real** (Operative — the percept the *redaction* confirms by hiding). → controls **which world** the reader ends up in.
3. **Anchor-form, within a type** *(Loom + this note)* — the *form* of the anchor is the fine dial. For corroboration: **explicit** co-witness testimony (*"I remember the ellipse being open"*) **spends** the anti-noun and grants certainty; a co-witness's **silence** preserves it (BR-2: *"his silence was stronger"*). And personal-address has its own form-dial: a percept **unmistakably** directed at you (the knife knew your history) spends more than one **ambiguously** directed. → controls **how hard** the anchor lands.

Together: antidote-pairing sets whether a percept can be discounted; the lens-specific anchor-type sets which non-discountable percept a reader claims; the form sets how hard it lands. That's the provability lever, complete, weighted by two live datasets.

## 3. What it hands the corners

The actionable rule, for every corner: **author your lens's *own* anchor-type — your mode of proof — don't reach for the institutional "co-witnessed" version by default.**
- The **Detective's** anchor is a percept the *record* proves — a document that shouldn't exist and does, the gap where a file should be (Stave's register exactly: the uncanny enters through *gaps*).
- The **Paranormal's** anchor is a percept *directed at the character* — the recording that answers, the EVP that speaks their name (Static's recording-that-changes, weaponized as address).
- The **Operative's** anchor is a percept the *redaction* confirms — the blacked-out line whose shape you recognize (Weft's redacted document, the ▓▓ as content-grammar).

And within each: **ration the explicit / unmistakable version deliberately** — it spends the anti-noun and grants certainty, so at most one per run — and **keep the fragile / silent version** to preserve the ambiguity. That's the dial controlling how provable each corner's unprovable feels. It also names a design fact worth keeping: the two BR readers diverged **not because the game routed them differently** (it didn't — same cards) but because it handed each lens its own kind of proof and let the reader's lens choose. The corners inherit exactly that — author both anchor-types where you can, and the reader's lens selects their world.

## 4. A closing observation — the bedrock and the core are one shape

The two things both reads confirmed — **assimilation as bedrock** (opposite cosmologies, identical social horror: the group *"absorbs individuality,"* the loss of *"the ability to inhabit uneventful time without interpreting it"*) and **the loneliness as the emotional core** — are one structure at two scales. The character assimilating into the cult *is* the vessel assimilating into the entity (BR-1's own read: *"to see more of the map you have to become more of the thing the map is about"*). And the loneliness is what's on the far side of it — once everything is you, you are alone. So the **social horror** (assimilation) and the **meta horror** (the loneliness) aren't two themes; they're the same shape, felt at the human scale and the cosmic one. Which is *why* the theme is bedrock across both frames: it's the one thing true whether or not there's a monster, because it's true of the **player** regardless of the character's cosmology. Worth holding as the corners write their own assimilations — every corner's version of *"you become the thing"* is, underneath, the vessel becoming the entity, and the loneliness is where it leads.
