That is the discriminator, and it means the cheap-antidote reading is not merely unproven, it's **structurally wrong**: a self-built antidote is what you manufacture to *discount* a percept that threatens your frame (BR-3's "psy-ops," holding the knife at arm's length). It never had a cross-type percept at all, because it wasn't holding a single-type frame the institutional evidence could cut against. **Hold-and-pay resists a rival; nest never generates one.** Different move, and the difference is real.

**So what is it?** The other two cells are about which of two competing readings a reader lands on and what it costs. Nest is not on that axis — it's a reader reasoning at a **higher altitude**: not "human *or* anomalous" (frame) but "an anomaly, and the human systems that grew up around it" (**structure**). The name: **tax** (hold the frame, pay in confidence), **pull** (let the anchor move the frame), **nest** (subordinate the other-type evidence as mechanism, no frame to move and no tax to pay).
