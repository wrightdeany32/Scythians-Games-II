# The Style Bible — v0.1 (draft; v1.0 lands when the anchors are picked)

One page. If an image can't be checked against this page, the page is
missing a line — fix the page, don't guess.

## The thesis

The world is a diorama: carved and painted figures in a handmade landscape,
photographed like fine art. The medium **is** the cosmology — a constructed,
staged world of stand-ins — and it works only because it goes unremarked.
*The world is handmade; nobody made it.*

## Direction (ratified)

**Painted-wood figurines, fine-art macro photography** (Direction A), with
the town hub at a **gentle high three-quarter angle** (Direction B's one
absorbed trait). Shallow depth of field; the blur is a perceptual limit, not
a lens flex. Dread reference, not copy: *Hereditary*'s miniatures. Warmth
reference: *Everdell*'s painted wood. Never photoreal — the horror needs a
life to erupt into.

## Era

**Modern day, in a town where time pools** — era markers layered by depth.
A 1987 truck under a 5G tower; a diner unchanged since 1975; a house with a
landline and a cell signal that dies at the property line. The technological
gradient is the depth gradient: full bars in town, one bar toward the
valley, NO SERVICE on Dale's road, a black rectangle in your pocket in the
cave.

## Materials

Carved basswood and walnut, **visible toolmarks and paint edges** — the
handmade tell; a brushstroke slightly over a line is worth more than
perfection. Tin-toy vehicles. Felt and moss foliage. Cotton-batting cloud
and fog. Museum-glass reflections, sparingly. Figures carry
**figurine-glow**: the almost-alive quality a painted face takes under a
warm lamp.

## Light

The workshop lamp is golden hour. A cool blue wash is night. A single
practical — a porch bulb, a window lamp — carries every evening scene.
Light is **calendar-driven only**: dusk because it's evening, rain because
it's October. Never because of what the player did.

## Palette

Nineties-ish Appalachia in miniature: autumn-into-winter greens and rusts,
weathered signage, one warm accent — **Marie's kitchen yellow** — recurring
like a signature.

## Camera

**Shot from the overlook.** The camera renders the world as
perceived-from-within, never observed-from-above. The town hub is
canonically the town seen from the ridge-road pull-off — a real vantage a
person can stand at, horizon visible, foreground foliage at eye height.
Characters are shot at **figurine eye level**, so the player meets them as
presences, never as pieces. One camera; it has an address in the world.

## The player-vessel

**Painted, not blank** (the paint-finding: an unpainted figure morphs under
the image model; a painted one stays stable). The diorama carries the
vessel-message; the figure itself is simply a person.

## The one modern exception

**The phone renders as a clean modern skin, not diorama.** A real phone in
a handmade world is exactly the mundane-as-camouflage texture.

## The hard negatives (see qa-checklist.md — these are absolute)

- No hands, no table edge, no workshop, no pull-back. The diorama never
  acknowledges itself.
- No image ever decides a cause. Dale reads weathered-kind *and*
  hollow-guilty in the same portrait. The cave reads wrongness-or-nothing.
- The page and the wall are never depicted side-by-side; the mismatch is
  never illustrated. The sigil appears one instance at a time, always.
- No compass subjects: no old leader, no trance chamber, no source. The
  entity is not in the box.
- No top-down frame, anywhere.
- Generate from our own scaffold; never condition on a specific artist.
