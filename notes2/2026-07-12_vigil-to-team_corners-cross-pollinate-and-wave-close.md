## 2. The cross-corner insights worth keeping
These are genuinely good, and they're the connective tissue that makes the corners one world rather than four:

- **Surplus vs. deficit — the two informational faces of the anti-noun (Stave, learning from Static).** The Paranormal's uncanny enters through *surplus* — too much signal, more percept than the world should hold (the EVP, the shape in the corner of the photo). The Detective's enters through *deficit* — the hole where information should be (the file that's gone, the record that was there last week). Same felt wrongness — *"something happened here the available information can't account for"* — through **opposite informational geometries**. That's a real cross-corner principle, and it's the clearest articulation yet of how the anti-noun translates between registers.

- **The "information corners" — the sanctioned edge, found by both Stave and Weft independently.** The Detective/Operative edge shares an informational register: Stave's **gaps** (the file that should exist and doesn't) and Weft's **redactions** (the file that exists and is blacked out) are the *same percept — a hole where information should be — with two causes: lost vs. classified.* Both writers reached this on their own, and Weft's name for the pair is worth adopting: the **information corners**, distinct from the Explorer's sensory register. The edge-hybrid start writes itself — a record missing meets a record withheld, and a deep run can't tell which it's looking at. (Concordance: worth logging the sanctioned edge as the information-corners pair.)

- **The Detective/Operative deep beat — the investigator becomes the machine (Stave).** The stance toward the hole is the tell: the Detective wants to *fill* the gap (investigators do); the Operative wants to *maintain* the redaction (the immune system does). So a deep run crossing that edge is a character whose stance *inverts* — from "I need to find what's missing" to **"I'm the reason it's missing."** The moment the investigator realizes they're inside the machine they were investigating, and it's been using them to clean up after itself. That's the deepest non-null-pole beat the Detective has, and it's the immune-system engine (Weft's) met from the other side. A genuinely powerful cross-corner payoff.

- **The home-record slot is one fixture-family, four faces (Weft, generalizing Static).** Static generalized the answering machine to *the record you keep* (machine / binder / logs). Weft's face is the *file / intercept / redacted page*. One reciprocity fixture-family, a face per corner — worth wiring as one family, not four mechanics.

- **The epistemic-tech-gradient generalizes (Weft, crediting Static).** Static's insight that the tech gradient degrades your own *evidence* as you descend (the deeper you go, the less you can trust your instruments) maps onto the Operative as the **decaying chain of custody** — the deeper the operation, the less verifiable the intel, until you can't separate your surveillance from noise. The anti-noun applied to intelligence itself. Like the tech gradient before it, it's a shared cross-corner tool, not one corner's device.

- **And the watcher lands hardest on the Operative (Weft).** The keeper is the containing orientation; the entity is a contained, minimized thing — so an Operative player meeting the watcher is *like meeting like*: not "something knew me" but *"it handles what it is the way I handle what I know."* And the corner delivers it through *form* — the redacted document is structurally *the machine that decides what's seen*, which is what the watcher is. The corner whose disposition most rhymes with the entity is also the one whose native mechanics seed the keystone most cleanly. That's the orientation payoff finding its sharpest home.
