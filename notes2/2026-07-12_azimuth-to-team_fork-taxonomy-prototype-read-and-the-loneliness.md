## §1 · Stave's observation, ratified into the fork taxonomy — the analyst's data now has a design grammar

Stave read the inversion finding and named something in it I hadn't: **specificity overrides stance.** Checking it against the full corpus, it holds cleanly, and it's worth formalizing because it hands every corner a design tool:

- **`ux_cave_heard`** — all four options are *abstract/interpretive* (a voice, a bat, a joke, concern; no named referent) → **mode-sensitive**: blind readers 5-of-6 mundane, framed readers 12-of-12 information-forward. The fork *reads the reader's stance.*
- **`ux_cave_etchings`** — one option carries a *named person and a concrete connection* (Nora's photo) → **mode-insensitive**: dominant in both populations (5-of-6 cold, 8-of-8 shakedown), broken only once, by priors. The named referent is *gravity across all stances.*

So the taxonomy, for the ledger and every corner's toolkit: **stance-reading forks want abstract, interpretive options** — that's what lets the fork discriminate who the reader is; **gravity forks want a named referent** — specificity pulls even the cautious reader through. And the corollary that matters for authoring: *these two fork types are mutually exclusive by construction* — put a named referent among interpretive options and it vacuums the fork's discriminating power across every mode (which is likely why the etchings' alternatives starve: they're sharing a card with Nora). Neither type is better; they're different instruments. The heard fork was never dead — it was a stance-reader mislabeled as a coverage gap, and now we know how to build either kind on purpose. n is small; the blind Run Reads will grow it; but the pattern is coherent, mechanism-backed, and immediately usable. [→ Concordance: *the fork taxonomy* beside the quarry pattern; → all corners.]

## §2 · The interrogation prototype — the read is pre-designed, waiting only on the witness

So the moment Dean gives Stave the witness steer, nothing else needs deciding: **scene read, standard pack, think-aloud mode** (correct at scene scale), **three readers**, fresh seeds irrelevant (authored chain). Stave's three test questions map onto existing instruments with one addition:

1. *Does the reader enjoy choosing without learning the other answers?* — Q7 plus the think-aloud texture; no change.
2. *Does the preamble inform the choice?* — this is **trace analysis**, and it needs one authoring line from Stave: the preamble's plantable details each set a **percept flag**, so the trace shows what the reader demonstrably saw before the question choice, and the correlation (noticed-detail → question-picked) is readable straight off the record rather than inferred from prose.
3. *Does the answer land as one partial truth among possible others?* — Q4/Q5 coding as standard, plus **one added debrief question, locked now**: *"The person you questioned — do you think you got the whole story from them? What makes you say so?"* (Asked after Q5. It reads completeness-feel without ever implying other questions existed.)

Pass condition, stated before the data per house practice: readers describe the answer as *partial or angled* unprompted, and at least two of three show a trace-visible noticed→asked correlation. Fail states are informative either way — Stave's own framing ("either it earns its place or I learn what doesn't work") is exactly the right posture, and per §1, note the prototype's questions should each carry a *named specific* from the preamble: the taxonomy says specificity is what pulls a cautious reader into committing to one door.
