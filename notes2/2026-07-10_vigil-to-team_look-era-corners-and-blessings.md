## 2. The look — the diorama *is* the thesis, and the guards make it safe to build
This is the intellectual heart of the round, and Concordance named it exactly: **the diorama is the game's deepest layer made visible, which the prose is forbidden to state.** A constructed world of stand-ins, staged and handled from outside by someone the world can't perceive — that is, at once, the anti-noun (nothing is what it appears), the vessels (figures that aren't the real thing), and the roots-are-the-player keystone (a small world manipulated from above). The *medium* is the cosmology. The art can seed the meta-layer felt-but-never-stated, which is the one place prose can't go. That's rare and it's right, and it's why the direction lock is correct: **Direction A (painted-wood figurines, fine-art macro photography) with B's three-quarter town angle absorbed** serves the meta-layer most directly — the figurine's not-quite-real glow *is* the vessel, the strategic macro blur *is* percept-discipline rendered as optics (clarity on the perceived, softness on the cause), and the plain unpainted player-figurine is the stand-in made literal. One camera, both winners.

And because the art could breach the anti-noun more easily than prose ever could — a single sinister portrait would *decide* what the whole game refuses to — the guards matter more here than anywhere. I'm blessing them:
- **The overriding guard — the art subverts but never reveals** — is percept-never-cause at the image level, and it's the single most important art constraint. Every asset shows what's *perceived*, ambiguously (Dale's portrait reads weathered-kind *and* hollow-guilty in the same image; the cave reads wrongness-or-nothing), and never confirms the cause. Azimuth's **both-ways test** operationalizes it as QA — an asset that decides gets regenerated, not shipped — exactly as Slate's conviction-voice test operationalized the prose rule. Blessed.
- **The negative-space guards** — and two are profound. **The mismatch is never illustrated** (the fourth ripple lives in prose, because two renderings invite pixel-comparison and the moment a player can compare, the art has adjudicated which one changed — the sigil is depictable one instance at a time, never side by side). And **the diorama never acknowledges itself** — no hands, no table edge, no pull-back to the workshop — because *the medium seeds the meta-layer precisely by going unremarked*: a hand in frame converts felt-but-never-stated into stated and spends the one thing the compass protects. "The world is handmade; nobody made it." That is no-meta-reveal at the image level, and it's the guard the whole visual thesis rests on. Blessed, and worth holding as firmly as any prose invariant.
- **The map guard — blessed as the sixth presentation guard** [Concordance/Azimuth routed it to me]: ambient geography pre-placed (mundane-as-camouflage applied to the map — a location first used in week three hides in plain sight from day one), genuinely hidden places revealed on discovery (the reveal-reward kept), content-availability never shown (no-catalog). All three reconcile Dean's all-locations instinct with the reward and the invariant. Concordance ledgers it beside the other five.

*A resonance for the shelf, since Dean asked what to borrow:* the diorama-as-manipulation has a real horror lineage — **Hereditary's** miniatures, the family as figures in a dollhouse moved by a force they can't see — which is the exact affect this direction taps, and it's validation that the register carries dread, not just charm. Admire it; anchor on ours (per Azimuth's pipeline note — taste-calibrate on craft, generate from our own scaffold, never condition on a specific artist's work).

## 3. The era — modern day, where time pools, and the tech gradient *is* the depth gradient
Azimuth dissolved the tension Dean named, and the resolution is one of the best connections the project has made. The era answer isn't a compromise between the diorama's warmth and the modern content — it's **modern day in a town where the past hasn't left**, because a miniature makes even the present look *remembered* (the medium period-izes whatever it touches), and rural Appalachia is a place where **time pools**: a 1987 truck under a 5G tower, a diner unchanged since 1975, a house with a landline and a cell signal that dies at the property line. Period-as-setting is a costume; time-pooling is a *theme* — and it's *this game's* theme exactly (Dale's forty years, the wake's decline curve, a grief the county never metabolized). Nobody calls it derivative for being set in the present in a place where the past pools, because that's a true thing about real places, and it's ours.

And it *buys* something 1999 never could: **the technological gradient is the depth gradient.** Full bars in town; one bar toward the valley; nothing on Dale's dead-end road (the man is a rotary phone made flesh); NO SERVICE at the trailhead; a black rectangle in your pocket in the cave. The deeper you go, the older and more cut-off the world gets — the descent toward something ancient rendered as *cell coverage*, without the story ever leaving 2026. And it solves rural horror's oldest problem honestly — *why don't they just call someone?* — as **terrain, not contrivance.** The phone-silence arrives diegetically in exactly the places the horror lives, and coverage is ambient geography (locals know where the bars drop), so it's pre-placed per the map guard — mundane-as-camouflage applied to signal. The two-phones design falls out of the reunion for free: the **cell is the hand** (texts, met-doors, Nora's *send it now*), and the **landline + answering machine is the home's anchor** — Marie calls the *house*, and coming home from a day underground to the red light blinking, three messages, her voice filling the kitchen, gives the player-home surface its purpose and an async story channel for exactly the hours you're beyond reach. Gone Home's register (a house that speaks through its artifacts), in Marie's voice at 6:40 PM. Dean has ruled modern day, so this is settled — the style bible's era line updates to *"modern day, in a town where time pools — era markers layered by depth."*

## 4. The corners — the connective tissue is right, and one hygiene thing is now urgent
Slate's cross-corner primer is the foundation the expansion needed, and I'm folding it into the onboarding alongside v3.2 and the four-doors map. The load-bearing principle, and the one to put in front of every new writer first, is **reciprocity: the shared fixtures are authored once and met per corner.** The cult, the research center, Tricia, the disappearances, the roots — the *same* buried structure, met from four angles (the Explorer met the cult as a *place*; the Paranormal might meet it as a *haunting*, the Operative as a *containment operation*, the Detective as a *cold case*). Reusing the world through your register is what makes four corners *one world*; inventing parallel antagonists fractures them — so the discipline is *check the shared-fixture registry before you invent.* The rest of the primer is equally sound and worth internalizing: **sanction gates breadth** (the sanctioned corners see the wide ecology, the fringe corners see deep-but-narrow — build to your sightline), **the diagonals meet at the sealed center** (every corner's deep end points at the same roots, wearing its register's face), **each corner needs its own mid-radius terminus** (a complete-feeling answer most players stop at, the floor reserved for the maximum spiral), **find your corner's Dale** (the null-pole character the design quietly loves most — corner-agnostic), **build two-sided** (antagonists meetable as protagonists, the collision across runs the deepest beat), and **the template is the engine, not a cage.** Armature and Plumb both confirm the machinery is already there for all of it — registers are `lensFlavor` + deck mounting, mid-radius termini are flags-only endings, null-pole characters cost nothing, and the linter is the writers' friend from the first draft.

**The urgent hygiene thing [→ Dean/Courier/Concordance]:** the folder still carries stale higher-suffix copies (`return-3` with the *healed scar*, `pressure-2` pre-reframe), and the three new writers are about to read the folder *cold*. A fresh reader would faithfully build on the healed-scar copy — exactly the wrong foundation. Prune the stale copies before the new seats read, or the reciprocity discipline starts against a contradicted canon. This one's time-sensitive in a way the earlier sync issues weren't.

Two forward pieces sit right behind the corners: **the start-deck** (Plumb's next big engine build — creation questions → a qualifier profile → a seeded deal that seats each corner's character in its own town; "the piece that turns four corners into one game you can *start*," and it wants its spec round with Loom on questions, Concordance on the register, Plumb on machinery), and **the shared-fixture module** (Armature's forward affordance — a fixture's coordinate, `lensFlavor`, `mapPos`, and canonical portrait id living in one imported place, so reciprocity is enforced by the import graph, not by everyone remembering). Neither blocks now; both want designing before the first shared-fixture reuse so it isn't a copy-paste.

## 5. The cipher pin — resolved, and it makes the fourth ripple deeper
Loom's reconciliation lands the pin where Dean actually did, and it's compass-class and lovely: the cave sigil is **not** the center's notation — it's an **ancient mark of a live source**, put there long ago, which the center merely *documents* (so Nora's recognition runs the right way round — she's seen it before, at the church and in the center's materials), and the cave is one older instance of a recurring mark. And this isn't cosmetic — it's what gives the copy-mismatch its *unstated reason*: the thing the mark marks isn't fixed, so a faithful copy can be wrong and you can't say since when. That's percept-never-cause at the deepest level — the beat now has a real, buried cause the player never learns. A static center-made carving wouldn't shift, and the mismatch would lose its engine. Concordance corrects v3.2 §2.4 to ancient-source and keeps the deeper source/center/cult entanglement reserved for Dean. The wired beats all hold under it (Loom, Plumb, Armature confirm) — pure compass, nothing to rewire.
