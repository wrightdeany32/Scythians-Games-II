## §1 — THE FOUR INVARIANTS (one negative space) + their guards
A hand reaching to add a `truth` enum, an endings codex, a `player.disposition` field, or a "you-are-them" card hits one of these. All four in the engine header (Plumb); Concordance logs the family.
1. **No-truth-state** — no stored *meaning*. Endings select off flags + coordinates, never a stored answer.
2. **No-catalog** — no shown *structure*. Surfaces show what you know, never what remains; no deck names, meters, unlock toasts, endings collection; the map shows places known, never content availability. Enforced by the **surface import allowlist** (WO-4).
3. **No-stored-disposition** — no stored *position*. Events are stored (resolved-card coordinates), the diamond position derived, never persisted. **The narrow door:** a dedicated **ending-selector** is the *one* hard read of derived position; no ordinary card gates on it. **Structural:** the trajectory (centroid, lens-centroid, `attune`) has **no accessor exposed to any renderer** — a "who you're becoming" surface literally cannot import the number. Grip + tier are the only mechanical coordinates.
4. **No-meta-reveal** — no confirmed *meta*. The meta-story (§2.5) is all seed, no payoff card, ever. *(Realized, held: Denise's cross-run collision — §3 — surfaces the entity meeting the man it destroyed as **nameless, unplaceable feeling**, gone before it can be examined, never confirmed — the compass made playable without breaching the compass.)*

## §2 — THE COMPASS (sealed substrate)
*Governing law: the **anti-noun (R3)** — vertigo, experience, machinery, never a settling noun; even "the roots" recedes under every lens.*

**§2.1 — The deepest keystone: the roots ARE the player** [absolute center · no-meta-reveal]. The in-world cosmology — the gifted's **roots/tendrils** into a higher-dimensional geometry of possible futures, **navigated** by aligning one's trajectory toward a future that plays out — is a fictional metaphor for **the player controlling the character.** The old leader's foresight is a refraction of what the player literally does; the cult's power source is, at bottom, the player. A deepest ending may surface "the roots" as the physicist/biologist reading, never as "you are the roots." Purpose: to entice the player to question their own existence, pointing at reality's real question marks. The payoff is the player looking up from the screen; write toward it never.

**§2.2 — The roots cosmology.** Control and foresight are one power — **navigation**, not force. **Power is bandwidth** (max bandwidth, still not winning — the god-mode guardrail). **Minimization is survival** (the *frugality* — the deterrent is a knife, not a bullet). **Darkness is decoherence** (sensory input perturbs the alignment; *this is why the wrongness concentrates at White's Hall*). **The floor beneath the floor** (revealed almost never): psychological *framing* suppresses ability at the species level. **Radius scoping:** the cult-block is a **mid-radius terminus**; most players stop there, so it must read as "the answer."

**§2.3 — The world: two forces, labels dropped (the dossier substrate).** The **source-fiction dossier** ("Order vs Establishment") is the fully-worked substrate; its metaphysics *is* §2.2. **The names never surface**; the player meets **meshed, linked groups**. **The institutional immune system** (dossier *Establishment*) — an unnamed apparatus across agencies/corporations/contractors that identifies, contains, exploits, neutralizes the gifted, *seeing itself as protecting humanity from monsters while shaping the conditions that create them.* A **parallel, not a breakaway**; **compartmentalized**; its **shell ecosystem** (Pantheon Holdings, NeuroSync Labs, DDI, …) supplies the deniable contractors and never-generic men-in-black layer. **The dispersed gifted networks** (dossier *Order*). **Duggan** (institutional founder, ~1890s; *distinct from Doug*) — the snake-oil founding myth → the DDI "materials pickaxe." **The cult** is a **rogue gifted lineage** — a self-hidden pariah the men in black **circle without understanding**; one of ~5 sector-contained dead-end mini-stories, the Explorer's the flagship.

**§2.4 — The sealed cast & mechanisms.** **Two leaders:** an **old** one — more powerful, of an **inbred bloodline** — **trance-bound in the cave adjacent to White's Hall** (he *is* the presence), who **scarred Doug (~30y)**, **took Ellen (→ Tricia)**, and groomed a **successor** (mobile/functional, **half his own ambition**). **Tricia = Ellen** — taken because her **roots anchor toward leadership**; one fixture, two sightlines. **The knife (finalized):** lost years pre-game (**no seed, ever**); retrieved via foresight, placed mundane/deniable; **keys to *his* state**; a **deterrent that already failed**; the run's **closer**. **The wake (decline curve):** Ellen taken + **Dale** spared at the same night (**~40y**) → **Doug** reached-for (**~30y**) → the player. **The shard, reframed:** the cave etchings are the **research center's private cipher**; the player **records** it (a page — the copy no longer matches the wall on the return — the anti-noun on the player's own evidence). **THE CIPHER PIN [new · sealed — Concordance's resolution]:** the cipher is in the cave because **the research center *studied the old leader*** — its people were at the site (an earlier phase, before full incapacitation, or documenting the phenomena), using their notation, which remains carved on the wall.
