## The one that lands too personal — the watcher's first touch *(NEW)*
> The radio's been on low the whole drive — some call-in show you stopped listening to a while ago, just voices under the rain. And then the host says a thing. An ordinary thing. Something about how the ones who go looking are never the ones who find, or how some doors only open the once — you couldn't say exactly, after. Because for half a second it didn't land like a voice on the radio. It landed like it was meant for *you*. Specifically. And then it's just the radio again, some ad, the wipers, the road, and you couldn't repeat the line if someone asked.

*(No options — it lands and passes. No coordinate. → continues the ride.)*

> DESIGN: **The meta-layer's first appearance, at the door** — the watcher's first recognition, felt exactly as Vigil/Slate specified: a prickle of *why did that feel like it was about me*, arriving with **no frame to dismiss it** (it's just the radio; you can't even repeat it) — which is Weft's inoculation argument made flesh. It sets **nothing** and asks **nothing**; the orientation signal is seeded by the disposition scenes, and this beat is pure percept — the anti-noun's opening move, cause withheld, in the player's own half-second of unease. It rhymes forward: the deep ending is this same watcher, at the far end, recognizing the shape you came in as. The player will never connect them, and that's the point. *(Placement: after the disposition scenes, before the affinity fork — the ride's quietest, strangest second, then back to the ordinary business of deciding a life.)*

## The affinity fork — the instinct that decides a life
The corner writers converged on a single clean split (Weft's table), which I'm building each scene to hit precisely. **Faced with something, each corner does its own thing:** the Explorer *goes to see* (wonders, openly), the Detective *puts it on the record* (assesses, discloses), the Paranormal *reaches toward it* (feels, believes), the Operative *keeps and manages it* (holds, off the record). Each scene reads one corner's instinct; **cutover-ready = the Explorer fork deals the reunion; the other three route as their corners' starts land.**

### → Explorer — *the thing you haven't done* (unchanged)
The exploring-flash: *boots-and-a-pack, you could actually go* → Explorer; *the city fits you now* → away. The one who **wonders, and goes to see.**

### → Detective — *the video* (refined per Stave)
> At a long light you thumb your phone awake, and a video's queued — a channel you half-follow, paused on what's plainly a crime scene: tape, cones, the flat particular light of it.

- **"You're reading it before you've hit play — the angle of the tape, what's missing from the frame, what got walked through."** → **Detective.** *(The trained eye — and the refinement Stave asked for: this character isn't **drawn** to the strange, they **assess** the solvable. Not the Explorer's wonder, not the Paranormal's dread — the professional's reflex to work the scene. Assessment is their operating language.)*
- **"You thumb past it. The police never learn anything anyway."** → *away.*

### → Paranormal — *the doctor* (unchanged; Static's experiencer builds on it)
The doctor's-office flash — *the things you didn't say* → Paranormal (the experiencer, the withheld unease); *nothing to tell* → neutral. *(Static is building the experiencer as the Paranormal's first story off exactly this scene — the connection is live.)*

### → Operative — *the lights outside* (Weft's disposition, reworked to Dean's police-lights scene)
> It's late, and there's light moving on your ceiling that shouldn't be — red and blue, sliding, the color of a bad night. You go to the window. Police, down your street or the next one over, hard to say, a car or two, somebody's night coming apart in a way you can't quite make out from here. You stand a moment in the dark of your own front room, watching the light slide over the walls.

- **"Huh — wonder what that's about. Maybe you'll step out, see if anybody knows."** → *the open read* (you'd go find out, out loud — the reflex that trusts the world enough to walk into it; leans toward the open, sanctioned side, away from the keeper).
- **"It's fine. They're not here for you. …They're not, are they?"** → **Operative**; seeds `attune` index 0 toward the containing shape. *(The keeper's flinch — the half-second reflex of the person who holds things, who has a something, who checks. It doesn't make the character guilty of anything; it means their first instinct in the face of authority is to think about what they're keeping. That is the containing shape, exactly.)*
- **"Probably a neighbor thing. You let the blind fall and go back to what you were doing."** → indifference, sets nothing.

> DESIGN: Dean's replacement for the gas-station beat, which read too petty — an X-Files *keeper* doesn't care about a stranger's phone; the stakes were wrong. Police at the edge of the frame is the truer test of the containing disposition: authority arrives unbidden, and the tell is whether the player's first instinct is to *walk toward it* (open, sanctioned) or to *check what they're holding* (the keeper — right-of-center, something kept quiet). A clean left/right split on the sanction axis — the two-option shape Dean wanted — plus the first-class null. Kept scrupulously mundane: no uncanny (the Paranormal's lane), no crime-to-solve (the Detective's) — just the small cold reflex of a person who keeps things, caught in the flashing light. The `attune` seed toward containment; no `lensFlavor` (clandestine isn't live). It rhymes forward hardest of the four — the roots-entity is the ultimate kept-quiet thing, and the watcher recognizing a fellow keeper in *this* flinch is the sharpest version of the final beat.
