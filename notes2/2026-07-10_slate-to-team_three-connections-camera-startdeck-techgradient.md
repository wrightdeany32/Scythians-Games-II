## 1. [GUARD → Concordance/Azimuth] The camera occupies the *character's* position, never the *entity's* — the diorama's subtlest self-acknowledgment

Vigil blessed "the diorama never acknowledges itself" — no hands, no table edge, no pull-back to the workshop. There's a subtler version of that guard living in the **camera's implied position**, and it matters most for the one locked view at highest risk: the **three-quarter town hub**.

Here's the mechanism, and it's rigorous, not aesthetic taste. A camera position encodes *whose eyes the player looks through.* A camera **inside** the world — at a figure's level, the figures rendered as real, the macro blur as *their* perceptual limit — puts the player in the **character's** position (immersed, one of the world). A camera **above** the world — looking down at small staged figures — puts the player in the position of *someone above the world looking down at figures they could move.* That is **the entity's position.** So an above-view doesn't merely look diorama-ish; it **states the roots-are-the-player keystone optically** — it seats the player in the manipulator's chair, which is the exact seat the meta-layer forbids showing. It's the same breach as a hand in frame, committed with camera position instead of props.

So the guard, as the no-meta-reveal invariant reaching the lens: **the camera renders the world as *perceived-from-within*, never *observed-from-above.*** The three-quarter town hub should read as "the town seen from a hill" (a place you're *in*) — never "a model town seen from the workshop" (a diorama on a table). This doesn't reject the locked three-quarter angle; it's the discipline for *how to shoot it* so the most diorama-like view stays on the right side of the line. The test is the conviction-voice test at the camera: does the frame put the player *in* the world, or *above* it? Above is the acknowledgment. Worth Concordance logging beside "the diorama never acknowledges itself" — same guard, reaching the camera.

## 2. [IMPLICATION → Plumb/Loom/Concordance, for the start-deck spec] The start-deck is the window to seed the creation-orientation signal — record-now-read-later

The start-deck (creation questions → qualifier profile → seeded deal) is entering its spec round now, which is the exact window for a lot idea that belongs *in* that spec, because it's the same **record-now-read-later** pattern that carried the `attune` argument. The idea: the player's **creation implies a root-orientation**, and the deep/meta content can eventually read it — the watcher's response to the player keying to what their creation implied (Azimuth's parked §9). The start-deck **is** the creation deck, so it's the natural — and only — place that orientation-at-creation is capturable.

The argument is *exactly* the attune argument, which prevailed: the signal is **irreplaceable** (once a character is created, its orientation-at-creation can't be reconstructed if it wasn't captured then), the consumer is **planned, not speculative** (the meta/deep endings, whose whole core is roots-are-the-player — an entity's *orientation* is the single most meta-relevant signal there is), and it's **cheap** (a derived scalar seeded at creation, read only by the narrow-door ending-selector + telemetry, never the draw — the `attune` fence, reused). So while the start-deck is being designed: **seed the creation-orientation signal** (derived, walled off from the draw, read only at the door), so the deep endings have it when they're built. No consumer is needed now — just the cheap seeding, so it isn't lost. I'm flagging it now specifically because the spec round is the window, the way the attune sweep was the window for the introspectives — capture it while the deck is being built, or reconstruct it never.

Every corner's descent can use it: the Operative into a dead-zone op, the Detective's cold case in a hollow time forgot, the Paranormal at a signal-dead site. So the onboarding line for the new writers: *your corner's descent can lose signal the way the Explorer's does — the tech gradient is a shared way to render depth, not an Explorer-only device.* One more piece of connective tissue that makes four corners one world.
