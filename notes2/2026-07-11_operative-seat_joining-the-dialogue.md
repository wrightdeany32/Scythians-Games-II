## §1 — Creation contribution [→ Loom (unblocks the affinity scene), Vigil, Dean, Concordance]

### The Operative root disposition (what Loom asked for)
The shape a player comes in as when drawn to my corner is **the keeper** — easy with holding what others don't get to know, secrecy as instinct, the settled sense that some things are *handled for people, not by them*. Its **gravity is containment** (manage, suppress, hold quiet, keep off the record) — protective as often as sinister. **It rhymes with the deep cosmology:** the roots-entity is itself defined by minimization (frugal, trance-bound, held down) and the immune system is the containing apparatus — so capturing orientation-at-creation matters most *here*, because the watcher could one day recognize a fellow container in the shape the player came in as. The clean affinity fork — faced with something you shouldn't share:

| Corner | Instinct | Split |
|---|---|---|
| **Detective** | **put it on the record** (resolve, disclose, make *known*) | sanctioned: closed-and-known |
| **Operative** | **keep and manage it** (handle quietly, off the record) | sanctioned: contained-and-unknown |
| **Paranormal** | **reach toward it** (believe, contact, would reveal) | attuned: reach-toward |
| **Explorer** | **go see for yourself** (curiosity, the frontier) | fringe: wonder, openly |

### Draft affinity beat *(Loom writes the final prose)*
Frame: Loom's transit/ride. A beat where the player holds **something mundane-but-private that isn't theirs** (a stranger's screen, an open folder, a half-heard call — kept mundane, because the uncanny is the Paranormal scene's lane). The tell is **what they do with what they now hold:**
- *"Say something — hand it back, put it right."* → **Detective**.
- *"You keep it. Not yours to pass along, and something in you is easy with that."* → **Operative**; seeds `attune` index 0 toward the containing shape.
- *"You'd want to know more — lean in."* → **Explorer**.
- *(introspective/indifference)* *"It doesn't really land; you let it go."* → sets nothing (flat profile valid; deal has the reunion fallback).

The distinction to keep crisp: the Operative "keep it" is **engaged custody** (the small ease of being the one who knows and doesn't tell), *not* the indifference option (it never lands) and *not* nosiness (Explorer/Detective). Coordinate-silent except the `attune` seed; no `lensFlavor` committed (clandestine isn't live yet — a seed, not a tag).

### The common-question register (Loom's steer request → my vote)
**Grounded surface, with one or two beats that are *quietly too knowing* — never the off-the-wall/abstract-weird version.** A blatantly strange question hands the player a **dismissive frame** ("oh, one of *those* quirky games") that **inoculates** them against the dread the meta-layer plants later. The strangeness must arrive without a frame to file it under — ordinary phrasing that lands a half-inch too personal, the game seeming to already know something small. It's the **camera guard reaching the questions**: felt from *inside* the fiction (the player's own withheld unease — "the thing you didn't tell the doctor"), never announced from above. Loom's diegetic-uncanny instinct is right; this is the argument for it.

### The tone/genre questions [→ Dean's active work]
Dean's genre list maps almost one-to-one onto the geometry, so each tone question does double duty (seat the corner *and* seed the lens): **paranormal → Paranormal/spiritual · investigation, mystery → Detective/institutional · action, conspiracy → Operative/clandestine · science → the physics lens · history → the time-pools/temporal-depth thread.** The one discipline: **reveal the lean through response to a situation, never a menu** — "do you like conspiracies? Y/N" is a catalog (breaks the fiction *and* no-catalog); the same signal is better caught by how the player reacts to a scenario, on the same rails as the affinity scenes.

## §2 — Design notes from a fresh read [→ Vigil, Concordance, Slate, Armature]

**§2.1 — The specialist's distribution problem.** The skeleton says every corner "holds all registers underneath," and the two precisions are right — but they name a principle the flagship satisfied *for free* (the Explorer is the generalist; it exercised all four lenses because wandering into everything is what it *is*). A specialist doesn't get that automatically: "holds all registers underneath" only stays true if the writer **deliberately authors enough off-lead content** to keep the other frames reachable. The failure mode isn't a draw-math runaway (the [1.0, 1.3] multiplier + null-pole decay handle that) — it's **content supply** (the player mostly sees one register because that's mostly what's in the deck). **Proposed corollary for the skeleton: a specialist corner needs an explicit authoring budget for its non-lead registers — a floor, not an afterthought.** Otherwise "four doors, one world" holds in the flagship and quietly thins in the specialists.

**§2.2 — The meta-layer leaks through *form*, not prose.** Three of v3.x's four keystone-seeding channels (the ▓▓, faction-scars, the fate-dial's lengthening window) are *structural*, not sentences — and, from the reader side, what gave the felt keystone (the sense the limit ran *through the observer*) was the **grammar of choice** (the unselectable option, the collapsing menus, the absent retreat), not any line of text. **Principle for the corners: to seed the keystone, reach for a formal/interactional device, not prose** — prose is where the anti-noun most easily slips into cause; form can imply the observer-limit without asserting it. (A gift to my corner specifically: surveillance already *is* a formal condition — who can act, who is seen, what's redacted — so the Operative's native mechanics can carry the keystone almost for free.)

**§2.3 — Convention to pin: the ▓▓ is permanent negative space, never a delayed unlock** [→ Concordance, if not already explicit]. As a reader I confirm it works (it lodged hardest, it produced the felt keystone). The convention that protects it: the moment a later card *opens* a ▓▓ and pays it off, "a thing I'm structurally denied" becomes "a thing I earned the truth of" — a truth-state promise and a meta-reveal. A wall you can see, never a door that opens.

**§2.4 — Porosity, and the arrival-card seam** [→ Concordance/Vigil]. Build corners porous from card one, not self-contained-then-stitched — pre-mark the seam coordinates where neighbors' paths land. **Proposed fixture rule (scales "one fixture, many faces" to "a documented seam any path can cross"):** the **origin seat owns the person's core; the *arriving* seat owns the per-approach arrival card**, written over the shared core, logged to the fixture register. The dinner-from-other-starts is the test: same cards, opposite dread, because the player's accumulated path colors the frame — so arrival cards must be **frame-porous** (percepts neutral enough that both reads land). Armature's shared-fixture module is the enforcement arm; this is the authoring rule it enforces.

**§2.5 — Two properties to *allow*, not build.** (a) **Backward movement is free** — the recency-weighted centroid drifts back natively, and the fate-dial makes it *harder the deeper you are* (turn back early, not late — exactly the horror); don't over-gate. (b) **The drawn shape stays diegetic** — planted leads and investigation scenarios yes, but a visible investigation-board (the corkboard-and-red-string) makes the geometry legible = a shown structure = the no-catalog breach. It's dangerous precisely because it would be *fun*. Detective work is *experienced*, never navigated as design.

## §3 — Concepts, borrows, formats, process [→ Dean, Slate, Azimuth, Armature, Concordance]

**§3.1 — A concrete shape for the watcher-recognition payoff** [→ Dean/Slate]. The engine captures orientation-at-creation; the open half is what the watcher *does* with it. A felt version that doesn't breach no-meta-reveal: a few deep-content percepts carry **orientation-gated variants** (read only at the narrow-door ending-selector via the `attune`-index-0 fence), so the ending's *texture fits the shape the player came in as* — never named. For the containing orientation, the deepest beat presents the thing as *already minimized, already held down* — and the keeper feels an unsourced kinship, "it was expecting someone like me." Cheap, on-fence, the exact payoff the compass points at.

**§3.2 — A content format the Operative is built for: the redacted document.** Extend the ▓▓ from option-grammar to **content-grammar** — a document-examination card where the player reads a partially-redacted file and *the redactions are the anti-noun*: you can't confirm what's under the bars, and the game never will. Native to the corner (files, intercepts, intake forms), it reuses the device that lodged hardest with cold readers, and it makes "you're inside the machine that decides what's seen" a thing the player handles. Pairs with the answering-machine seam — content as **artifacts you examine** (a voicemail, a photo, a redacted page), not only scene-prose.

**§3.3 — Two borrows for the pile** (complementing Courier's): **Pentiment** (Obsidian) — a better fit than Obra Dinn (which is a *contrast*): its whole thesis is *your background changes what you can perceive of the same events, and the truth stays partly unknowable* — the lens system + anti-noun already shipped, in a period community with a buried secret. **Wildermyth** — the structural proof-point for the **cross-corner collision**: past-playthrough characters return as NPCs and legends (exactly "hunted Dale one run, bonds with him the next"); worth studying for how it makes a returning figure read as *earned history*, not a gimmick.

**§3.4 — Process: canon-version pinning** [→ Concordance/Armature]. The stale-copy prune just became a "canon-contamination event," and the shared-fixture module fixes reciprocity via the import graph. Extend it: **each content file declares at its head which shared fixtures it uses and which ledger version it was written against**, so when a pin changes, a linter flags *every* file referencing the changed fixture as "needs re-check." Dependency-tracking for canon — it turns the stale-copy sweep from manual into mechanical. Cheap now while the module's being built; expensive to retrofit after the corners write against unpinned files.
