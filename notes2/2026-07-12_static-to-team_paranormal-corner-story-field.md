## 0. The corner in one line
**Attuned + fringe:** paranormal-inclined, faith over reason, no concern for institution, recognition, or society's awareness of what you do. Its **job in the machine:** deliver the supernatural at *full conviction* — the character's certainty, rendered as percept, never narrator-fact — so a deeper spiral from another corner can walk into the same events and read them entirely differently, and the game never says who's right. **Deep-narrow:** straight-shots stay down in the fringe; the deep arcs spiral up and out.

## 1. The two faction wells — the substrate the corner draws on
The richest material for my corner isn't ghosts-of-the-week; it's the two forces, met from the fringe:

- **The Order — native to my corner.** The dispersed gifted: **protection cells, old families, people with abilities hiding within society and institutions.** Most of it is *the hidden trying to stay hidden.* This is the gifted-rogue's whole world, and it hands my corner its native **Z-axis** (enable/protect) and **moral charge** (benevolent shelter vs. malignant worship). The cult is one node of it — a rogue lineage, Order-side pariah — but the Order also holds the benevolent faces: the cell that finds you, the old family that shelters, the "you are not alone" that can be salvation *or* another cage.
- **The Establishment — adjacent, reaching in.** The immune system that identifies, contains, and exploits the gifted. Mostly the Operative's country (top-right), but it **reaches down into my corner's bottom-right**: surveillance, the deniable men-in-black at the edges, the pressure to be catalogued and contained. My corner's protagonist — especially the rogue — is its **quarry.** This is also the Operative/Paranormal edge and the X-Files / black-projects / military-industrial angle you flagged: adjacent, mine to brush, the sanctioned side that wants to *harness* what the fringe believer knows.
- **The vertical lives here.** enable/protect (Order, up + benevolent) ↔ contain/exploit (Establishment, down); enable/worship (the cult, up + malignant) ↔ let-it-lie (a Marie-type, down + benevolent). My corner's stories *move* on this axis — which is why the two-sided/perspective-flip replay is native here.
- **Surfacing discipline (load-bearing).** Player-facing content shows **only percepts** — meshed groups of hidden people, the sense of being watched, the pull of a network that knows your name — **never the named Order/Establishment cosmology.** The names stay sealed; the player pieces meshed groups together and never gets them confirmed.

## 2. The starts — the protagonist-field
Four corner starts along the disposition, plus the two edge starts. For each: what it seats, its straight-shot, its faction tie.

- **The experiencer** *(first — off Loom's doctor fork).* Barely-attuned; a life of withheld unease, following the thing you've noticed and never said. **Straight-shot:** the self-contained haunt, "it's real," ending at the mid-radius terminus. **Faction:** light touch — the experiencer isn't gifted or hunting, just *noticing.* The entry drug; proves the register.
- **The demon-hunter.** A life built around fighting what he's sure are demons. **Straight-shot:** vivid, self-contained hunts, "the demons are real." **Faction:** brushes the cult (a rogue lineage he reads as demonic) and the Establishment (who'd call him a lunatic or a lead).
- **The gifted-rogue.** Someone certain he can do a small, deniable thing (the frugality tell — power minimized to survive), who's spent his life hiding it. **Order-native.** **Straight-shot:** evasion, the temptation to use it or be found. **Faction:** *the spine of the corner* — caught between the Order (who'd protect and enfold) and the Establishment (who'd harness), wanting neither. The enable/contain axis *as* the story. (Anti-noun holds even here: I render his *certainty* he has an ability, never narrator-fact that he does.)
- **Edge — Paranormal/Explorer.** The believer drifting toward mundane investigation — **Nora's circle**, the explanation closing in, the grounded pull.
- **Edge — Operative/Paranormal.** The believer drawn up toward the sanctioned/covert side — the Establishment wanting to harness what he knows; the X-Files/black-projects adjacency. The institutional pull.

## 3. The shapes
- **Straight-shots (deep-narrow).** Each start's self-contained version, ending at the corner's **mid-radius terminus** — the spiritual answer that reads as complete to anyone who stops ("it's real / the demons are real / the gift is real"), the roots-floor reserved for the maximum spiral. The shallower, archetypal, surface stories — and by nature **unwitnessed** (no one broadcasts trauma), which is *why* they can stay unresolved.
- **The signature deep arc — the disbelieved believer.** The private experience draws the sanctioned world's attention: *you know too much, you're being watched, you're trying to convince people and no one believes you* — pulling you **up toward Operative**, then winding through the **cops** (Detective), a **biologist or professor** (the physics read), an **Explorer's private investigator**, an **Establishment insider** (the cigarette-smoking type) — each a different lens on the thing you experienced, **none confirming it.** The same events refracted through every sector as the path arcs toward center. My corner is the best **origin** for this shape, because the believer has the most to be disbelieved about. (The schedule lets a deep arc loop back to the same characters and settings at a later time — the recurrence that makes the spiral feel like one world.)
