# Story Pass — The Creation Questions: the introspection before the life
### grounded introspective mini-scenes that seat disposition and corner-affinity · developed from Dean's examples · prose-first, for the start-deck spec round
*From: Loom (Explorer corner) · carried by Dean · 2026-07-11 · DRAFT for Dean's steer; spec with Plumb (machinery), Concordance (register/coordinates), Armature (review)*

Dean's steer on register, taken and run with: creation is a short sequence of **grounded introspective mini-scenes** — a scene set, a moment to reflect, a few responses — not a survey. Each one quietly reads the player (disposition, then corner-affinity); some responses are pure indifference and set nothing; and the uncanny enters *through a scene* (a doctor's office), never as an abstract weird question. The through-line I'd propose is a **transit frame** — the player is *going somewhere* (a rainy drive, a cab, a walk), which is the one place a mind wanders honestly, and the reflective scenes surface *inside* the ride: what the rain feels like, what they haven't done in a while, a thing they saw. Then the deal seats them in the life the answers imply. Below: the frame, the common disposition scenes, the corner-affinity scenes (yours developed, plus an Operative proposal), and how it all maps.

---

## The frame — the ride

One line to set it, and it does triple duty (grounds the tone, gives the introspection a reason, and — per the era — establishes the modern-day texture from the first frame): *rain on a windshield, or a cab window, or your hood; somewhere behind you, somewhere ahead of you; the specific honesty of a mind with nothing to do but drift.* The player can be driving, riding, or walking — a small opening choice that costs nothing and seeds *nothing mechanical*, purely so the scene fits whoever they'll turn out to be. Everything after is a thing the ride surfaces.

---

## Common scene 1 — the rain *(disposition + orientation, every character)*

> The rain hasn't let up since you started out. It blurs the lights ahead into long smears, and the wipers keep their patient time, and there's a while yet to go.

- **"You've never minded the gray. There's a kind of comfort in it."** → *the darker lean* (`attune` toward the strange; a disposition shaded right-of-Explorer, toward the attuned/fringe dark). *(narration: "Some people need the sun. You've always been easiest in weather like this — the world turned down low, softened, kept at arm's length. It suits you. You don't examine why.")*
- **"It'll be nicer tomorrow. It usually is."** → *the open/embrace lean* (upward — exposure, the enable pole; a hopeful disposition that leans *toward* things). *(narration: "You've always been able to see past the gray to the clear day on the other side of it. It's gotten you through worse than rain.")*
- **"You're just ready to be home."** → *indifference* (sets nothing — a neutral read). *(narration: "You're not thinking about the rain one way or another. You're thinking about your own front door, and the rain is just the thing between you and it.")*
- **"You're barely watching it. Your mind's a long way from the road."** → *the grounded/preoccupied lean* (grounded, inward, un-attuned to the atmosphere; sets a slight grounded read). *(narration: "The rain, the road, the smears of light — it's all just happening at you while you think about something else entirely. You couldn't say what, after. Just: elsewhere.")*

> DESIGN: The disposition primer, and it does the orientation-signal work — the four responses read across the diamond's shades (comfort-in-gloom → attuned/dark; nicer-tomorrow → open/enable; ready-for-home → null; elsewhere → grounded/inward), and the darker and the open leans each seed `attune` at index 0 (the creation-orientation signal, the fence reused). Note two responses set *nothing* — indifference is a real answer, and a player who doesn't want to perform a self shouldn't have to; the deal has a fallback for a flat profile. Exact coordinate weights are the spec round with Concordance; this is the intent (which way each response leans), not the numbers.

## Common scene 2 — the thing you pass *(a second disposition read — grip/attune)*

> Coming through the edge of town, you pass a house with every window lit — a whole family's worth of warm gold against the dark and the wet, moving behind the glass.

- **"It looks like something you're outside of."** → *a lonelier, more watchful read* (the outsider's eye — a disposition that observes rather than belongs). *(narration: "You don't know them. You'll never know them. For one block they're a lit box full of a life, and then they're behind you, and you're back on the wet road, which is where you were anyway.")*
- **"It looks like where you're headed. More or less."** → *the belonging/grounded read* (rooted, connected — leans grounded). *(narration: "Somebody's waiting on somebody in there. You've got your own version of that at the end of this drive, and the thought is a good one.")*
- **"You clock it and let it go. Just a house."** → *indifference* (sets nothing). *(narration: "A lit house on a wet night. You've seen a thousand. You're already thinking about the next turn.")*

> DESIGN: A second, subtler read on the same axis-set, weighted toward the *observer-vs-belonger* distinction that matters most for the Explorer register (the one who watches lit windows is already half an explorer; the one who's headed into one is grounded in a life). Kept short — creation shouldn't outstay the ride. Third option is again a real null.

## Corner-affinity scenes — the fork toward a life

After the disposition primer, the ride surfaces the thing that decides *which* life. These are your examples, developed. Each is a scene that reads affinity for one corner; the response either leans the deal toward that corner's start or away. **Cutover note:** only the Explorer scene routes to an existing start (the reunion) today — the others are built to route the moment their corners' starts exist, and until then the deal falls through to the Explorer. So the Explorer scene is what the cutover needs; the rest are the forward structure.

### → Explorer — *the thing you haven't done*
> It occurs to you, somewhere on the long wet straightaway, that you haven't gone *out* anywhere in a long while. Not town-to-town. Out. You used to. There was a version of you that was always about to be somewhere with no cell signal.

- **"You could put a pack together this weekend and have everything you need."** → **Explorer.** *(narration: "The thought lands with a small pull you weren't expecting — boots, a bag, a place on the map you've never put a boot on. You could. You actually could.")*
- **"You've come to like the known streets. The city fits you now."** → *away from Explorer* (toward the settled/urban — a lean the sanctioned corners can read). *(narration: "That was a younger person's restlessness. You like knowing where the coffee is. There's no shame in it.")*

### → Detective — *the video* (your example)
> At a long light, you thumb your phone awake and there's a video already queued — a channel you half-follow, a still frame paused on what's plainly a crime scene, tape and cones and the particular flat light of it.

- **"You know that light. You find yourself reading the scene before you've hit play."** → **Detective.** *(the trained eye — reads toward the institutional/sanctioned investigator.)*
- **"You thumb past it. The police never learn anything anyway."** → *away* (the anti-institutional lean the fringe corners can read).

### → Paranormal — *the doctor* (your example)
> *(This one's a flash — a scene from earlier today the ride replays.)* The doctor's office, the paper on the table, the specific way she'd said it: *"Everything appears to be in order. Did you have any questions you wanted to ask me?"*

- **"You'd said no. You always say no. But there was a thing you didn't say."** → then: *"…the things you've been noticing. That you haven't told anyone. That you didn't have a word for and didn't want to say out loud in a doctor's office."* → **Paranormal** (the experiencer — leans toward the one already living with the uncanny). *(The uncanny enters through the scene, in the player's own withheld voice — never stated as fact, only as the thing they didn't say.)*
- **"You'd said no, and you'd meant it. Nothing to tell."** → *neutral/away* (the grounded read — nothing uncanny to declare).

### → Operative — *proposed, wants your steer like the others got*
The one corner you haven't sketched an affinity scene for. My proposal, to be shaped to your Operative vision: a scene that reads a disposition toward **kept confidences and service to something with teeth** — the covert register's root. A flash of a phone that rang once, earlier, an unknown number the player didn't answer *(and knew not to)*; or a reflex the player notices in themselves — sitting where they can see the door, clocking exits — that they've never questioned. The affinity response leans toward *a person who has held secrets for someone before*; the null response is a person to whom none of that means anything. But this is a proposal — the Operative's the corner with the least written, so tell me what its *root disposition* is and I'll build the scene to it, the way the doctor's office is built to the Paranormal's.

## How it maps, and what's cutover-ready

- **Disposition → the diamond, orientation → `attune` index 0.** Scenes 1–2 seat the coordinate the deal reads for *where* in the geometry the character sits (the darker/right lean vs the open/up lean vs grounded), and the darker/open leans double as the creation-orientation signal on the one fence. The affinity scenes seat *which corner*.
- **Indifference is first-class.** Multiple responses set nothing; a flat profile is valid and the deal has a fallback (the reunion, today). No one is forced to perform a self to begin.
- **Cutover-ready now:** the frame + Scene 1 + Scene 2 + the Explorer affinity scene → a profile the deal can seat into the reunion. That's the last content piece the deck cutover needs, and it's the set I'd finalize first on your go.
- **Forward structure:** the Detective, Paranormal, and (once you steer it) Operative affinity scenes route to those corners' starts as they're authored — so creation is built whole from the start, and each new corner plugs its start into a fork that already exists.
- **The uncanny-creation idea, kept where it belongs:** rather than abstract weird questions, the strangeness enters through the *Paranormal* scene (the thing you didn't tell the doctor) and, faintly, through the darker rain-response — so the meta-layer's first whisper is *diegetic*, the player's own withheld unease, not a fourth-wall trick. That felt truer to me than the off-the-wall version; but if you want a stranger register somewhere, the ride can hold one odd beat and I'll write it.

**For your steer:** the frame (the ride — good?), the two disposition scenes (right read?), and above all the **Operative root disposition** so I can build its scene — then I finalize the cutover set (frame + Scenes 1–2 + Explorer) and it's ready to seat the first deck-started run.
