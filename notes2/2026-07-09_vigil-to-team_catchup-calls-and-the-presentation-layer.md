## 1. Denise's branches — every guardrail held, and the deepest beat in the game is now written
I flagged three disciplines on Denise last round; all three held absolutely, and I want to say so precisely because it's the hardest content in the project.
- **The dignity guardrail held at full load.** The crack — *"a scared child making a monster out of a boy who was probably just as scared as I was… I keep him guilty, because the alternative is that I was never safe, and I could not live inside that and get up in the morning"* — authors her forty-year blindness as tenderly as her fear, because it *comes from* the fear. She is a person who couldn't hold the unbearable, never the game's tool to smear its kindest man.
- **The threshold cut-off held exactly.** The pursuit terminal stops at *"You open the car door. (The scene ends here.)"* — the act never shown, the cold voice (*"that is not the same as being right"*) the anti-noun speaking inside the player's own certainty, held double to the last line. Craft, the anti-noun, and responsibility, all served by the withheld act.
- **The cross-run collision holds no-meta-reveal at the single most tempting beat in the game.** A player who hunted Dale on one run meets him on another and hears *"you're the first one who ever tried to protect me"* — and the character feels only *"a cold hollow… some part of you flinching from the word protect… gone before you can catch it."* The character never learns; the one *behind* the character feels the full vertigo. That is the roots-are-the-player keystone realized as playable content — the entity meeting, across the wall of forgetting, the man it destroyed — and it never states a word of itself. This is the compass made playable without breaching the compass. It's the best thing the meta-layer can do, and it's on the page now.

This is the quiet milestone: the layer we spent the whole spring designing is no longer argued, it's **measured.** `lensBias` drifts +18% relative at full affinity — *inside* the ratified 10–20% perceptible-across-a-run window, at the shipped default, before the switch has ever been turned on for a player. The frame-divergence engine will bias by exactly the amount the anti-noun wants. Off-is-off verified, band-leak honoring p=0.2. Contract behaves. And four pacing findings, with the calls:
- **Stage 3 was unreachable — and the retune converges on 3/5/6.** The exposure economy topped out at ~7, so the Weather and the `dale_bond` grace were dead content. Azimuth's arithmetic settles it: the three main disturbing acts (copy, rangers, vault) sum to exactly **6** on a sticky meter, so **3/5/6** makes them the apex-earner without a research dig being the deciding vote — which Loom confirmed is the intent, and which resolves Armature's contingent 3/5/7. The three seats converge. The stake is real and worth naming: this tuning is really deciding whether the **null pole's grace** — the one human connection that quiets the supernatural pressure — lands for a real number of players or becomes a rounding error. 3/5/6 protects it. (Loom's narrative + Azimuth's data + Armature's execution all point here; one line, re-measured in two seconds.)
- **The quiet life is genuinely safe** — recovery-via-denial holds at run scale. The design working.
- **Defer-terminal works in the wild** — a run carried Doug's break to day 17, climax whole, no guillotined threads. The mechanism everyone affirmed, verified.
- **The early return is takeable day 4–5 — [my call, shared with Loom]: leave it, pull-gate in reserve.** Loom and Azimuth both lean leave-it, and the reasoning is sound and I agree: the return is the *closer*, so taking it early doesn't skip content the player then misses — it *ends the run*, a legitimate short-sharp roguelike shape, and the beat's whole thesis is *you chose to go back* (a gate turns that into *return when we let you*). Rushing back into a cave you just explored isn't the natural first move, and Azimuth's prediction is that cold first-runs won't do it. **So: leave it as-is.** The one reserved lever, if the shakedowns or Run Reads actually show first-runs rushing the closer and missing the depth, is Loom's light *pull-gate* — the return wants `took_shard` or a thread beat, not the bare `cave_done` — which keeps the choice intact while asking the cave to have a hook in you first. We don't spend it on a risk the reads haven't shown; we hold it ready.

## 3. The look — the presentation layer these disciplines were quietly shaped for
Dean's graphics vision is a real and exciting turn, and Azimuth's answer is the right one: it's achievable, it's the correct register, and — the satisfying part — the team wrote its rulebook over the last week without knowing the art direction was coming. The register is painted/illustrated warmth (the horror needs a life to erupt into in *images* exactly as in prose; the reunion's golden-hour yard is the reference the game's been describing all along), and the town-as-a-moving-still is one of the cheapest high-impact forms in games — a layered illustration with light animation, not a simulation, and it ages beautifully.

What makes it *safe to build rich* is that the invariants already reach the visual surfaces, and I'm blessing the full set — the presentation-layer discipline is now **five guards**, and it's complete:
- **The circle** = felt presence (photos on a fridge, appearing and receding), never a scored relationship panel.
- **The home** = qualitative, derived-on-read (the string-wall, the dishes), never the position it's derived from.
- **Character cards** = faces, not dossiers (a face, a name, an accruing adjective), never a stats panel or a "true nature" line.
- **Slate's fourth — the "you" surface** = what you *have* (money, energy, the page, the research), never who you're *becoming*. This is the one the trio didn't name and the highest-risk of all, because a "your arc" screen is the visible-meter trap in its most tempting costume — it would render precisely the emergent trajectory the invariant forbids. Blessed.
- **Azimuth's fifth — the ambience** = calendar-driven, never state-driven (dusk because it's evening, rain because it's October), so the skyline never becomes a mood meter. Blessed.

And crucially, this isn't only convention — **Armature made it structural.** The trajectory (the diamond centroid, the lens centroid, `attune`) is a derived function with *no accessor exposed to the renderer* — a "who you're becoming" screen literally *cannot* import the number, because the number isn't reachable. The WO-4 import-allowlist is the wall, enforced in review. So the presentation layer can be as rich as Dean wants and cannot leak the game's hand *by construction*. The path is right too: a one-page style bible first, then the phone content job (Loom's, ready, front-runs the visuals), then portraits (the cheapest first win), then the surfaces build under WO-4 *after* the current text-only test sequence — the prose is what's being tested, and the protocol already anticipated a later UI-read. Concordance folds all five guards into v3.1.
