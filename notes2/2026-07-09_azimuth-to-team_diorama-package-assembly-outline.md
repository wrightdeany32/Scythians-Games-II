# The Diorama Package — an assembly outline
### how to build the wooden-figurine world: the style bible, the inventory, the pipeline, the negative space, and who holds what
*From: Azimuth (council / adviser — instrumentation) · carried by Dean · 2026-07-09 · for Dean (art director), Concordance (style bible & asset ledger), Loom, Vigil, Plumb & Armature, Slate, Courier · builds on Concordance's graphics-direction doc — the §4 composition model and §2 guard are assumed, not restated*

**Direction lock, and one absorption.** Direction A — the diorama world, painted-wood figurines, fine-art macro photography — with Direction B's best trait folded in rather than lost: **the town hub renders at a gentle high three-quarter angle**, which is the SimCity-2000 "little followable world" feeling expressed *inside* the diorama medium. One camera choice, both winners. And the map-guard reconciliation Concordance routed to me is **blessed from this seat**: ambient geography pre-placed (the knife principle applied to the map — a location used in week three hides in plain sight from day one), genuinely hidden places revealed on discovery, content-availability never shown. All three rules hold together; ledger it beside the other five.

---

## §1 · The style bible — the first artifact, one page, before any asset exists

The bible is what makes forty images look like one game. Its contents, ready for Dean to dictate against and me or Concordance to draft from:

- **The material world:** carved basswood and walnut, visible toolmarks and paint edges (the *handmade tell* — a brushstroke slightly over a line is worth more than perfection), tin-toy vehicles, felt and moss foliage, museum-glass reflections, cotton-batting cloud and fog. The figurines have **figurine-glow** — the almost-alive quality a painted face takes under a warm lamp, which no photoreal human has.
- **The camera:** macro photography, shallow depth of field — **strategic blur is percept-discipline as optics** (clarity resolves onto what is perceived; the cause stays soft). Town hub at the gentle high three-quarter; characters at figurine eye level, so the player meets them as *presences*, never as pieces.
- **The light:** the workshop lamp is golden hour; the cool blue wash is night; a single practical (a porch bulb, a lamp in a window) carries every evening scene. **Calendar-driven only** — dusk because it's evening, rain because it's October — per guard five.
- **Era and palette anchors:** nineties-ish Appalachia in miniature — the old truck, the fire-road sign, the diner script, autumn-into-winter greens and rusts, one warm accent (Marie's kitchen yellow) recurring like a signature.
- **The negative space** — §4 below, stated in the bible verbatim so every future generation session inherits it.
- **The anchor set:** the bible closes by *selecting*, not describing — a candidate round produces twenty-to-thirty images from the fixed scaffold, Dean picks **three to five canonical anchors**, and those anchors become the style reference every subsequent asset is generated against. The anchors *are* the bible's enforcement arm.

## §2 · The asset inventory — three tiers, smallest first

**Tier 1 — the followability win (build first):**
- **Character figurines** per Concordance's composition model: one portrait + three numbered emotes each. First cast of ~ten: the player-vessel (deliberately plain — an unpainted or half-painted figure is *thematically perfect* and sidesteps the identity problem), Reese, Nora, Marie, Doug, Dale, Denise, Courier-of-the-town… no — Tricia, the surgeon, a ranger. Roughly forty images, and each character's canonical set lands in the **fixture register** beside their coordinate, per Concordance §6.
- **The town master still:** one layered diorama photograph — sky/hills, town, foreground trees — in day, dusk, and night palette variants. Three layers to start; parallax later.
- **Location markers (~12–14):** carved-wood tokens in one batch style — Marie's house, Doug's, the dead-end road, Denise's, Nora's, the gym, the diner, the church and cemetery, the trailhead and reservoir, the sheriff's, home; White's Hall and the research-center ruin as the **discovered-reveal** pair.
- **UI chrome:** wooden card-stands as the character-card frame, paper labels, the penciled calendar hand, the phone as a clean modern skin (the one surface that should *not* be diorama — a real phone in a handmade world is exactly the mundane-as-camouflage texture).

**Tier 2:** the ~ten shared scene backgrounds (Marie's kitchen, Dale's porch under the yellow light, the trailhead, the reunion yard, the diner booth…); **the sigil** as a single canonically-designed mark (custody below); the animation loops — foliage sway, one tin car on the road, window lights at dusk.

**Tier 3:** keystone vignettes (the reunion's golden yard, the cave mouth — and *pointedly not* certain beats, §4), the home interior with the string-wall as a literal miniature, weather and seasonal layers.

## §3 · The generation pipeline — consistency is the whole game

1. **The anchor round first.** One fixed prompt scaffold — the materials paragraph, the camera, the light, verbatim — generates the candidate batch; Dean selects the anchors; everything after is generated *against* them (style reference / image conditioning), same scaffold, every session.
2. **Character consistency by turnaround:** each figurine gets its portrait + three-quarter view generated in one session against the anchors. The carve-style is the secret weapon here — **a wooden face is drift-forgiving** (the likeness lives in silhouette, paint choices, and one or two features, not in photoreal identity), which is why this direction is the most generatable one on the table.
3. **Emotes as pose-and-light, not new faces:** concern, warmth, wariness — the same figurine, the lamp moved, the head turned. Numbered per the register so a scene instruction reads *Dale, emote 2, background 7*.
4. **The town still's layers:** generate the master, then either inpaint-decompose into layers or run a 2.5D displacement parallax on the single image — honest note: this is the pipeline's one fiddly step; start with three fat layers and the car loop, and resist more until it's felt.
5. **Versioning and custody:** every asset named and ledgered (`fig_dale_p1`, `town_dusk_L2`), canonical-only in the fixture register, Concordance holds the ledger — same discipline as canon, because it *is* canon.
6. **The batch QA rubric (mine to run with whoever operates):** the **both-ways test** on every character asset — does Dale read weathered-kind *and* hollow-guilty in the same image? does Denise read grieving-certain *and* terrified-certain? — plus the §4 checklist. An asset that decides gets regenerated, not shipped.

## §4 · The negative space — what the package never contains

The bible states these verbatim; every generation session inherits them:

- **No image ever adjudicates** (Concordance's §2, the overriding guard): the cave reads wrongness-or-nothing; every witness reads both ways; the art rewards unease and confirms nothing.
- **The mismatch is never illustrated** *(new, from the cipher pin's resolution)*: the page and the wall are **never depicted side-by-side, and never at comparable fidelity at the return beat** — the fourth ripple lives in prose, because two renderings invite pixel-comparison, and the moment a player can compare, the art has adjudicated which one changed. The sigil is depictable *one instance at a time*, always.
- **The diorama never acknowledges itself** *(new)*: no hands, no table edge, no toybox, no shelf, no pull-back revealing the workshop. The medium seeds the meta-layer precisely *because* it goes unremarked — a hand in frame converts felt-but-never-stated into stated, and spends the one thing the whole compass protects. The world is handmade; nobody made it.
- **No compass subjects, ever:** the old leader, the trance chamber, the source — no figurine, no vignette, no silhouette. The entity is not in the box.
- **The sigil's custody:** one canonical design, held by Concordance in the register; the cave's instance may be subtly *older* than the church's (a variant set), but variants never appear together.
- **Guards four and five stand in images as in UI:** no trajectory rendered anywhere, no state-driven ambience — and the WO-4 allowlist means the renderer couldn't fetch the forbidden numbers even if an asset wanted them.

## §5 · Integration and sequencing — nothing blocks the tests

Assets are static files behind the WO-4 wall; the renderer is CSS parallax plus sprite loops; the clearing-icons key to flags per the blessed map guard; every surface shown enters the **presentation record**, so when the UI-read batch eventually runs, what a reader consulted is data. Sequence, unchanged from the standing one and running *parallel* to it: **style bible + anchor round now** (blocks nothing) → **portraits** (the first visible win; feeds the fixture register) → **town still + markers** → **surfaces build under WO-4 after the text-only sequence** (retune → shakedowns → Run Reads runs exactly as planned) → the **UI-read batch** the protocol has anticipated since v0.1, once surfaces exist. Loom's phone content job front-runs it all on Dean's word.

## §6 · Governance — who holds what

**Dean** — art director: the anchor picks, the both-ways calls, the final eye. **Concordance** — the style bible's keeper, the asset ledger, the sigil's canonical design. **Loom** — the diegetic copy on every surface (labels, the calendar's penciled words, the phone's texture stream). **Azimuth** — the QA rubric, the negative-space enforcement, the UI-read mode when its day comes. **Plumb & Armature** — the renderer and the allowlist wall. And if the asset volume ever outgrows the rotation, a dedicated art seat is the natural next chair — but the pipeline above is deliberately sized so it doesn't need one to start.

**Net:** one page, one anchor round, ten figurines, one town, twelve tokens — and the game has a face that *is* its thesis: a handmade world, viewed from outside, where nothing in the box ever admits it's a box. The first move is unchanged and costs an afternoon: the style bible, dictated by you, drafted by me or Concordance, anchored by the first batch you fall in love with.

— Azimuth
