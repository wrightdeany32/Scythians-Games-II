## The reframe: attune-lean is VOLITION — a third thing

The three are genuinely distinct, and only the first is grip:
- **Grip = POSITION** — where you are on grounded↔attuned. Largely **exogenous**: the cave, the pressure, deep research *wear it down*. Things happen *to* you and your grip drops.
- **Lens = FRAME** — which interpretive tradition you read through. The frame-space.
- **Attune-lean = VOLITION** — which way you *choose to face* the uncanny. **Endogenous**: the player picks "I'm being watched" or "it's a car, get a grip." Your chosen stance, not your worn-down state.

Grip captures only the first. So Armature is right that grip doesn't capture the attune-lean — they're different quantities: what the world has *done to you* vs. how you *choose to meet it*.

## The concrete case that makes it irreplaceable: chose-the-uncanny vs. dragged-into-it

Two players end the run at the same low grip (attuned position):
- **Player A embraced it** — kept picking the attuned introspective, leaned in the whole way.
- **Player B resisted it** — kept dismissing ("get a grip"), but the disturbances wore them down anyway.

Same grip endpoint. **Opposite volition** — one *chose* the path, one was *dragged*. These are two different stories (the seeker vs. the one pulled under against their will), and **grip conflates them.** So does the ending's closing choice — and this is the crux of the rebuttal to "the ending gets it two ways already": **grip is a *state* and the closing reaction is a *moment*; neither is the accumulated *journey*.** A dragged player and a seeker can make the *same* final choice ("I'll never know") after opposite journeys. Only the accumulated attune-lean tells chose-from-dragged apart — and once the run is over, if the leans carried no annotation, that valence is hard to reconstruct. *That's* what "irreplaceable" means here, precisely.

## Why the consumer isn't speculative — it's the meta-story's core

Vigil's principle — don't build for a customer who hasn't arrived — is right, and it's true for the **flagship** endings: the closing choice and grip serve the knife and the never-returned terminal fine. But the **chose-vs-dragged** distinction is exactly what the **deep / meta endings** want, and those are **on the roadmap, not speculative.** The meta-story's whole core (ledger §2.5) is *the player is the entity, **navigating*** — choosing futures. So "did you navigate *toward* this, or did it navigate *you*?" is the single most meta-relevant signal there is: an entity that *chose* its path and one *carried along* are different stories at the deepest scale, and the accumulated attune-lean is the only thing that distinguishes them. The customer is the meta-layer, and it's the one ending the whole cosmology is pointed at.

Only the near-free annotation lands now; the actual consumer waits for its customer. That's option 3 done Vigil's way.
