## What the reader understood — cold
This is the part worth sitting with, because a cold reader didn't just *tolerate* the design, they read its deepest bets back to us in their own words:

- **The formal thesis, unprompted.** Q2, verbatim: *"a horror story disguised as a life-management sim, where the sim mechanics ARE the horror."* That is WO-1's entire reason for existing, articulated by a stranger. The whole bet that the loop *itself* could carry dread — confirmed by the one person whose opinion on it couldn't be coached.
- **The presentation layer as the star.** The reader scored the depletion menu — the greyed options with their weary in-character refusals, Plumb's fatigue marker among them — as *"the single most elegant piece of the design; the mechanic being the meaning,"* and caught *"call it a day"* **drifting in meaning under a constant signpost** (*"clean rest → watchful endurance → a season's acquiescence"*), supplying the shift themselves. That's the felt-not-shown discipline operating on the *UI*, read exactly as designed. Every presentation guard we argued over was vindicated by a reader who found the surface to be the most beautiful thing in the game.
- **The anti-noun at its highest.** The reader held *two complete, mutually-undermining explanatory systems simultaneously* — the mundane (infrasound, CO₂, "a pattern you go looking for is a pattern you'll find") and the human-conspiracy (the swept floor, the radio) — *and refused to let either be the master key,* with a deliberate three-way confidence split and an explicit audit of their own pattern-hunger as a confound. That is felt-but-unprovable working at a level we could only hope for: not confusion, but *calibrated* uncertainty, held on purpose.
- **Dean's cake — the most validated beat in the game.** Q3, at length: the cake as *"the story's thesis delivered as an image instead of an argument,"* the moment the game *"stopped being about investigating a place and became about passing,"* and the moment Doug reframed retroactively as *sincere* — *"the most dangerous version of himself, because kindness is what you don't brace against… the horror would arrive not as a threat but as an invitation, from someone who loved me."* Dean, your one spoken idea from weeks ago carried the entire run's pivot. It's the beat the reader will remember.
- **The terminal, taken exactly as written.** The reader chose `never_returned` knowingly and walked it through at essay length: *"a man who says 'it was nothing' has forgotten; a man who says 'I'll never know' has chosen, and remembers choosing,"* and took Loom's closing line *"with the 'mostly' left in, because the mostly is where the truth lives."* The ending Loom wrote for the player who never goes back found its first player, and the player understood it as the only real victory on offer.
- **The replay structure — the deepest read of all.** Q6: *"the mechanically rewarding move (say yes, go deeper, open the door) IS the assimilating move… to see more of the map you have to become more of the thing the map is about. A completionist replay and a 'let the cult take me' replay are structurally the same impulse — which is the point."* A cold reader reverse-engineered the *reason the game resists completion* — that the drive to see everything is the drive the horror feeds on. That's the whole architecture, understood from the outside.

## The findings worth keeping
Beyond the validation, BR-1 taught us things no scene read could:
- **Run scale grows new frames.** The reader's interpretive frame — *assimilation-and-complicity* — **has never appeared in six scene reads.** Reading the whole thing produced an axis of divergence we literally could not see until someone experienced the assembled arc. The divergence engine is deeper than we knew, and only the whole-game read reveals it.
- **The second-witness anchor — a design principle, and a good one [→ Concordance's toolkit].** The reader's *entire* "quite confident" tier rested on one moment: the rangers' radio, **co-witnessed by Nora** — *"the one externally verified moment my nervous system could not fabricate."* Everything solo stayed discountable; the one *shared* percept became load-bearing for the whole theory. So the principle for every corner: **a co-witnessed percept is the reader's confidence anchor — ration them deliberately, because each one you grant is a fact the player gets to keep.** Solo percepts keep the anti-noun alive; shared ones spend it. That's the lever that controls how provable the unprovable feels, and it's now empirically weighted. The Reese-heard-it-too pattern, generalized.
- **The silence-break is a salience map.** The reader held silence for 65 picks and broke it — into paragraphs — *exactly where the game ends.* Where silence breaks is where the game grabbed hardest, and it's now a metric; if BR-2/3/4 break at the cake or the rangers, we'll have a map of the game's grip that no debrief question could draw.
- **Research-drives-lens, confirmed in the wild.** The reader's mundane reading of the cave was *armed by their own dig* — the cave-physics research handed them infrasound/CO₂ "fully formed and cited," deployed verbatim in the walk-back. The lens system worked on its first field outing.
- **P2 confirmed — and my early-return call with it.** BR-1 didn't rush the closer; it *never took it*, turned back at the descend fork on day ~6, and declined to reopen at day 14 with full articulacy about why. The early-return pull-gate stays un-built, exactly as ruled. And **premature abandonment: zero** — every drop was a *knowing* choice, the dropped-vs-declined distinction fully legible in the record, and the exposure ladder behaved precisely as tuned (3/5/6, stage 1 for this low-exposure shape).
