## 1. What the corner actually is — a disposition, not a power set

The corner is the **attuned + fringe disposition**: a lean toward paranormal explanations, **faith over reason**, a tendency to reach for the extraordinary — held with **no concern for institution, recognition, or whether society is even aware** of your actions or beliefs. "Paranormal Investigator" is just the archetypal label; the real thing is the disposition. Powers, hauntings, demons are **flavors inside it**, not the definition (I over-rotated on "empowered" earlier; this corrects it).

So the corner isn't one protagonist — it's a **family of them along one disposition:**
- **the experiencer** — barely-attuned; a life of withheld unease, noticing things and not saying so (Loom's doctor-office creation scene). The right first story.
- **the demon-hunter** — a life built around fighting what he's sure are demons; vivid, self-contained hunts.
- **the gifted-rogue** — believes he can do a small, deniable thing (fits the cosmology's frugality — power minimized to survive), and has spent his life hiding it and dodging anyone who'd harness it.

And crucially, **whether any of it is "real" stays anti-noun-ambiguous even for the gifted one.** I render his *certainty* he has an ability, never narrator-fact that he does — same as the demon-hunter's *certainty* it's a demon (the cold, the voice, the shape in the dark — never "the demon"). Conviction-voice + percept-never-cause, applied to the supernatural itself.

## 2. The corner's job in the whole machine

The part I most want the team to have, because it defines what my corner is *for*: **my corner delivers the supernatural at full conviction — the most vivid, spooky, self-contained version — precisely so a deeper spiral from another corner can walk into the same events and read them completely differently, and the game never says who's right.**

The compass floor already holds that possession, insight, the god-king, and peak performance are one substrate under different frames. My corner is the **spiritual frame on that substrate**: the demon-hunter's demons, the experiencer's haunting, the rogue's powers are the same thing the physics corner reads as brain-mechanism and the deep cosmology reads as the roots. So my corner is the "here are the demons" that the anti-noun then **refuses to confirm across runs** — recontextualizable *because* it was delivered as percept and certainty, never fact.

And these stories are, by nature, **unwitnessed** — self-contained, with no movies, TV, or newspaper articles about them, because people don't broadcast trauma or experiences they couldn't understand. That's not a limitation; it's the corner's texture (deep-narrow, private, deniable) and part of *why* the events can stay unresolved — no public record ever pinned them down.

## 3. The corner's shapes — the straight-shot and the signature deep arc

Sanction gates breadth, so my fringe corner is **deep-narrow:**
- **The straight-shot stays down in the fringe** — the self-contained haunt or hunt, "it's demons," ending at my corner's **mid-radius terminus** (the supernatural answer that feels complete to anyone who stops there; the roots-floor reserved for the maximum spiral). These are the shallower, more archetypal surface stories.
- **The deep arc spirals up and out through the sectors** — and my corner is an unusually good *origin* for one, because **the believer has the most to be disbelieved about.** The signature shape (Dean's, and it's exactly right): the private experience draws the sanctioned world's attention — *you know too much, you're being watched, you're trying to convince people and no one believes you* — pulling you **up toward Operative**, then winding through the **cops** (Detective), a **biologist or professor** (the physics read), an **Explorer's private investigator**, an **Establishment insider** (the cigarette-smoking-man type) — each a different lens on the thing you experienced, **none confirming it.** The same unresolved events refracted through every sector as the path arcs toward center. (And the schedule lets a deep arc loop back to the same characters and settings at a different point in time — the recurrence that makes the spiral feel like one world.)
- **The two edge starts** fall out cleanly: **Paranormal/Explorer** (the believer drifting toward mundane investigation — Nora's circle, the explanation closing in) and **Operative/Paranormal** (the believer drawn toward the sanctioned side that wants to harness what he knows).

## 4. Naming — reversed from my earlier draft

I'd proposed keeping the player-character unnamed. **Cross-encounterability kills that:** player-characters will appear as NPCs in *other* runs — committee meetings, court hearings, crime-scene scenes, séances, five characters in a room, two of them speaking of the player in the possessive (his idea, his item), an NPC yelling the name in a dramatic beat. Those scenes need names. So: **name the player-characters.**

Two things worth carrying, both free:
- **Second-person "you" from inside your own run.** You're "you" in your own story — family calls you *hon*, your name barely spoken to your face (just how people talk) — and you're **named when other characters speak of or to you**, which is exactly the cross-encounter case. That keeps a soft version of the "wall the player deconstructs" without fighting the art.
- **A meta-bonus the naming unlocks:** meeting a **former player-character by name, from the outside**, in a later run is the **Denise-collision generalized** — the entity meeting a vessel it used to be, now just another painted figure in the box. Names make that payoff possible; namelessness would have foreclosed it.

**Production note for the art track:** instructing the generator to keep a figurine *blank* makes it **morph the shape** (chess-piece drift) — a blank figure gives the model nothing to hold — while a *painted* figure stays stable. So paint them; let the diorama and the art style carry the vessel-message without literal blankness. Worth recording as a finding.

**The naming bible** (cast + player-characters + locations, in a rural-Appalachian, modern, time-pools register — hollows / gaps / forks / runs / knobs, family-surname places, names layered by age) is a real eventual deliverable, but **not a priority yet** — too many moving pieces, not enough story locked. I'll own it when we're ready.

## 5. The home-record slot — one reciprocity slot, a different face per character

The answering machine is too narrow on its own; the fix is to generalize it to **"the record you keep / the thing you come home to"** — one reciprocity slot, a different face per corner:
- the **reunion cousin** → the **answering machine** (Marie calling the house; *"honey, call me back"* piling up).
- my **experiencer** → a **binder** (clippings, taped-in printouts, photos, the wall-of-string folded into a book) or **digital logs** if he's younger — and the **analog-vs-digital split rides the tech-gradient** (the older / more cut-off the life, the more analog).

The binder is the one I'd reach for, for two reasons: it's **diorama-native** (a handmade, tactile object, same DNA as the figurines), and it's the **stage for the recording-mismatch** (§6) — you flip back through your own book and an entry doesn't say what you remember. The home surface and the copy-mismatch become the same object.

## 6. The recording that changes on playback — my corner's artifact-of-the-deep

The Explorer's artifact is the recorded **sigil** with the fourth-ripple **copy-mismatch** (your copy disagrees with the wall on return — the anti-noun turned on the player's own evidence). My corner runs the same play through its register (shape-translation): **the recording** — EVP, a photo of an empty room, a video clip.

- **The mechanic:** you capture something, and later — on playback (**record-now-read-later**, the same fence as `attune`) — it reads differently than you remember. A sound in the static that wasn't there; a shape in the corner of the photo; a word under the hiss. You can't say which changed — the recording, your memory, or the room.
- **The era twist:** the **tech-gradient degrades your instruments with depth.** Clean recordings in town; deep, they come back corrupted (the camera glitches, the audio artifacts, the timestamp's wrong). The deeper you go, **the less your own instruments can be trusted** — the coverage-gradient applied to your epistemics.
- Obeys the **art guard** by construction: the mismatch is *never illustrated* (no clean before/after to pixel-compare). Concordance would slot it into the four-artifact scheme (shard / residue / relic / **recording**).

## 7. My corner's Dale — the null-pole candidate

Every corner needs its null-pole character (Slate's primer). In the Explorer, Dale is the grounded read the *town* dismissed. In a believer's corner the poignancy **inverts**: the grounded read is the one the **experiencer-protagonist himself is inclined to dismiss**, because *he's* the attuned one. My corner's Dale is **someone who came back down** — a former believer, a spouse, a fellow experiencer who found a grounded frame and made peace — who says *"it isn't real; it's the grief, the loneliness, the thing the doctor didn't catch,"* and who is (per the null-pole honesty) **closest to true, can never be proven right, and gets pushed away by the protagonist's certainty.** He doubles as the emotional cost of conviction: the need to be right drives off the one person reading you truest. [my lane — I'll develop him with the opening.]

## 8. Borrows worth stealing from

- **Outer Wilds — the run-model, exactly.** Knowledge is the *only* progression; the world resets each loop; what carries across is the *player's* understanding, not the character's power. That's "rewind and hit play again," and it's proof the loop is deeply satisfying with no mechanical progression — the payoff is the player knowing more, which is quietly the roots-are-the-player thesis. (Watch their ship-log against no-catalog — theirs is a rumor-map; ours should track only what you've *perceived*.)
- **Her Story / Immortality (Sam Barlow) — the anti-noun as a whole game.** The player assembles truth from fragments; the game never adjudicates; different players leave with different coherent theories. The thesis at the structural level — and for my corner, the fragment-search is the shape of assembling a haunting. Immortality's *"something watching through the footage"* is close to the watcher.
- **Kentucky Route Zero + Norco — the register.** Magical-realist / Southern-gothic Americana, decline, the uncanny inside the mundane and never explained. The closest tonal cousins to "modern day where time pools" — borrow how they hold the strange in flat, elegiac, working-class prose without tipping into genre-horror.
- **Ultima IV's virtue quiz — the creation lineage.** The 1985 ancestor of "creation questions that seat who you are through thematic dilemmas, not a stat screen." Loom's introspective-scenes approach has a real pedigree; Slate's *"don't think, answer fast"* is the same instinct.
- **Pentiment / Disco Elysium — proof-points for the discipline we already have.** Disposition through action not stats; "you can be certain and wrong"; competing internal frames voiced without adjudication. External validation for neutral-labels-seed-disposition and conviction-voice, if ever wanted.
