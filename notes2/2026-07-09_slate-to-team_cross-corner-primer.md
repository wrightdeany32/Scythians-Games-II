### the connective structure for the new corner writers, from the seat that's been holding it

## 1. The register skeleton — lead with your register, hold all four

Each corner **arrives through its register** — Detective/institutional, Operative/clandestine, Paranormal/spiritual, Explorer/grounded-skeptic-adjacent — but every scene **holds all four lenses underneath**, because the anti-noun requires it. The register is the *default lens the player walks in with*, not the only one available: a Detective scene must still support a spiritual reading and a materialist one; a Paranormal scene must still let a skeptic dismiss it. Your corner is a **door**, not a verdict. (This is one-fixture-many-faces raised to the scale of a whole corner.)

## 2. Reciprocity — one world, authored once, met per corner

This is the single most important cross-corner principle, and it's what makes the four corners one world: **the shared fixtures are authored once and introduced differently per corner.** The cult, the research center, the men-in-black layer, Tricia, the disappearances, the roots themselves — these are *the same buried structure*, met from four angles. The Explorer met the cult as a **place** (a cave). Illustratively: the Paranormal might meet it as a **haunting**, the Operative as a **containment operation**, the Detective as a **cold case**. Same thing underneath, a different face on top.

So the working discipline for a new corner: **before you invent a new antagonist, check whether your corner is meeting a *shared* fixture from a new angle.** Reusing the world's fixtures (through your register) is what connects the corners; inventing parallel ones fractures them. Concordance holds the shared-fixture registry — that's the place to check.

## 3. The geometry of how the corners relate

Three geometric facts shape what each corner can do. (These come out of the disposition diamond the flagship runs on; the new writers don't need the math, just the consequences.)

- **Sanction gates *breadth*.** Your corner's *height* — how institutionally connected it is — determines how **wide** it can see. The sanctioned corners (Detective, Operative) can see the **ecology**: the network, the conspiracy, the other gifted, the government program. The fringe corners (Explorer, Paranormal) see only what they physically touch — **deeper, but narrower.** So the sanctioned corners are the *wide* views (the conspiracy made legible); the fringe corners are the *deep-narrow* views (the intimate horror, the one cave). Build to your corner's sightline — don't hand a fringe corner the whole conspiracy, or a sanctioned one nothing but a single haunted room.

- **The diagonals meet at *depth*.** The corners are far apart on the surface and **converge at the center**, where disposition dissolves. A deep run from *any* corner approaches the *same sealed center* (the roots) through its own lens. That shared center is the structural reason the four corners are one world — so your corner's deep end points at the **same thing** the others' do, wearing your register's face.

- **The mid-radius terminus.** Each corner has a **"complete-feeling but not the floor"** answer — the Explorer's is the cult (human, mundane-monstrous; most players stop there and it feels like *the* answer). Build your corner its own mid-radius terminus that reads as the answer to anyone who stops there, and **reserve the deep cosmology for the maximum spiral.** Don't surface the floor early; most players, most runs, should stop at your mid-radius answer and never learn there's a floor beneath it.

## 4. Every corner has a null pole — find your Dale

The Explorer's deepest beat is Dale: the grounded/human reading, the one that **adds no tilt and can never win** — the truest reading, carried by the lens that can't form a filter bubble, held by a man who dies (metaphorically) not knowing he was right. Every corner should have its **null-pole character** — the person who reads the wrongness the *plainest*, whose reading offers grounding but no resolution, and who the design quietly loves the most. It's the deepest honesty the flagship found, and it's corner-agnostic. Find your corner's Dale.

## 5. Two-sided stories — the moral-charge replay dimension

Every corner's content can be authored **dual-use**: playable from the faction's side *or* the opponent's — the way Dale (the survivor) and Denise (the accuser) are two coherent readings of the same events, and the game never adjudicates. It's a **per-corner authoring efficiency** (one neighborhood yields two playable stories) *and* a source of replay divergence. Build your antagonists so they can be met as protagonists on another run — and the deepest beats come from the *collision* across runs (the player who hunted Dale one run and bonds with him the next).

## 6. The shared deep-content lot — build *toward* it (a pointer, not a dump)

There's a **sealed substrate** the corners connect to at depth. The **faction ecology** — the eugenicist cult, the protector network, the research center / Establishment — is the mid-level shared world your corner will touch (see §2). Beneath that is the **sealed cosmology** — the roots, framing-suppresses-ability, the roots-are-the-player keystone — and some deeper reserved concepts the lot holds. You don't need the sealed material for surface content, and by design you *shouldn't* surface it (the anti-noun keeps it compass-only). But **build toward it — leave hooks that can connect *down*, not isolated corners.** When your corner goes deep, that substrate is the shared floor, and it's held — ask me or Concordance when you get there. And the anti-noun disciplines (conviction-voice, percept-never-cause, the null pole) apply to the deep content *exactly* as to the surface — the deeper you go, the more they matter.

## 7. The template is load-bearing, not decorative

The invariants (**no-truth-state, no-catalog, no-stored-disposition, no-meta-reveal**), **conviction-voice** (cosmology-adjacent prose renders the player's *felt certainty*, never narrator-fact about the entity), **percept-never-cause** (describe what's perceived, never adjudicate the cause), the **five presentation guards**, the **felt-not-shown** discipline — all inherited wholesale from the Explorer, all in v3.1. The flagship isn't just the first corner; it's the **proof** these disciplines produce the thing. So the template is what makes your corner *work* — not a constraint on it, the engine of it.

## Net

The connective skeleton for the new writers: **lead with your register but hold all four** (§1); **the shared fixtures are authored once and met per corner** — reuse the world, don't fracture it (§2); **sanction gates your breadth, the diagonals meet at the shared center, and each corner has its own mid-radius terminus** (§3); **find your corner's null pole** (§4); **build two-sided** (§5); **build toward the shared sealed substrate** (§6); and **the template is the engine, not a cage** (§7). This is the cross-corner material the lot has held for this moment — the corner content is the writers' own; this is the shared world they're four doors into.
