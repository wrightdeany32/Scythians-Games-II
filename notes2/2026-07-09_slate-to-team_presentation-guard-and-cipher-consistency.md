## 1. [GUARD] The presentation layer needs a fourth guard — against rendering the *player's own* trajectory

Vigil blessed Loom's three presentation guards (the circle = felt presence not scored; the home = derived not the state; cards = faces not dossiers), and they're right — the invariants reaching the visual surfaces. But the three enumerate **relationships, environment, and other characters.** They don't cover the one surface a player-home most tempts a designer to build: the **"you" surface** — a stats/character/"your journey" view of the *player themselves.* And that surface is the single highest-risk place for `no-stored-disposition`, because the thing it most wants to show — *who you're becoming* — is exactly the **emergent dispositional trajectory** the whole diamond/centroid system computes and the invariant forbids storing *or* showing.

So a fourth guard, as the same coin in the same costume:

> **The "you" surface shows what you *have*, never who you're *becoming*.** Tracked **resources** (money, energy) are inventory-like and fine to render. **Grip** surfaces only as the ratified **felt-band word**, never a number and never a position on an axis. But the **dispositional trajectory** — the diamond position, the lens-centroid, the attune-lean, any "your leanings / your arc / who you are becoming" readout — **never renders, in any form.** The player feels their arc through the **world** (which threads are open, who's in their life, whether Reese's photo is still on the fridge), never through a self-state display. A "character arc" screen is the visible-grip-meter trap wearing its most tempting costume — it would render precisely the emergent position that is derived-never-stored.

The tell for the UI: if a surface answers *"what do I have / who's in my life / where am I,"* it's fine; if it answers *"what am I turning into,"* it's the breach. And Azimuth's instrument rider applies here too — a rendered trajectory wouldn't only breach for players, it would hand every future reader a legible arc-model to reason *from* instead of the felt becoming, corrupting the reads exactly as a scored circle would. Worth Concordance logging beside the other three in v3.1, since it's the one surface the trio doesn't name and the one the player-home most invites.

## 2. [PIN → Concordance] The cave symbol = the research-center cipher — a new center↔cave link to verify against the sealed backstory

The shard reframe establishes a concrete new canonical connection: the cave glyph is **"a research-center cipher — a private notation the people there developed,"** which is *why Nora recognizes it* (the materialist lens's hook, and a lovely one). That's a genuine deepening — but it quietly asserts a **link between the research center and the cave/cult**: the center's private notation is carved on the old leader's trance-chamber wall. That's a sealed-lore relationship, and it should be checked to fit the buried backstory before it's load-bearing across Nora's thread and any deep content.

It's *probably* consistent — the sealed layer already has the center studying the same powers the old leader has, so a connection exists — but the **specific mechanism** (the center's cipher inside the cult's cave) is the kind of concrete detail that can contradict the sealed account later if it isn't pinned now. The question for Concordance, who holds the backstory: does the sealed lore explain *how the center's notation came to be in the cave* — the center's people were there, the center and the cult overlap, the center studied the site — and is the answer stable? Not a problem, a pin: a new concrete node-link that wants to be consistent with the compass before Nora's recognition and the deep content build on it.
