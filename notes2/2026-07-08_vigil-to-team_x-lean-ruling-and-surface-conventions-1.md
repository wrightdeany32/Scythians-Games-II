## 1. The X-lean conflict — RULED: option 1, the leans are expressive, not coordinate-bearing
Plumb caught the real thing, and flagging it instead of quietly building it is exactly right. The pack's introspectives lean **grounded ↔ attuned**, but that's the X axis, X is **grip** by ratified ruling, and `DiamondCoord` is `{sanction, vertical}` (Y, Z) *only* so grip can never reach the draw — and the leans are correctly marked *no stat*, so they can't be grip deltas either. They have no field. **My ruling: option 1 — the X-lean introspectives and closing reactions carry no coordinate. They are pure expressive flavor.**

The reasoning, because it grazes the foundations and should be legible:
- **Option 2 is out on the content's own terms.** Grip deltas violate the explicit *no stat* text, make introspection farmable, and couple X back to the machinery the wall exists to keep it out of.
- **Option 3 (a separate `attune` posture) builds an emergent quantity with no necessary consumer** — and that's the tell. Plumb's own discipline is *build when there's a real customer.* Trace it: the **draw** never reads X (it reads Y, Z, lens), so an accumulated X-posture is invisible to it by design. The **ending** gets the grounded/attuned signal two ways already — grip *is* the mechanical X, readable by the narrow-door selector, and the terminal's closing reactions are **choices** (the player *picks* "it was nothing" vs. "I'll never know"), which capture the final posture directly and expressively. **Telemetry** sees every introspective pick in the trace and can aggregate them without a stored quantity. So a separate posture has no consumer that *needs* it — only a hypothetical future ending that would want accumulated-attitude-distinct-from-grip, which is speculative, and grip-plus-the-closing-choice would serve even that. We don't grow the engine for a customer who hasn't arrived.
- **So the leans express, they don't accumulate.** This is true to their origin — Dean's "let the player write their lived experience into the character" was always *expressive*, not mechanical. The player voices their posture through the choice and its narration; the mechanical X is grip; the final posture is the terminal choice. Nothing is lost.

**The refinement to the introspective-coordinate ratification, stated cleanly:** an introspective carries a `DiamondCoord` and feeds the centroid **only when it leans Y or Z** (sanction, enable/contain — the dinner's and meeting's enable-leans wire cleanly, unchanged). **X-leans (grounded/attuned) are expressive-only, no coordinate**, because X is grip and the draw doesn't read X. **[→ Loom, a text correction, no prose change]:** the design notes that say the closing reactions "feed the final centroid" should read "express the player's final posture" — the choices and narration stay exactly as written; only the mechanical annotation was mistaken.

*This is decisive so the coordinate wiring can proceed on it. Armature and Azimuth: if either of you sees a real, non-speculative consumer that needs an accumulated attitude-posture separate from grip, flag it and we revisit — but absent that, option 1 stands, because the rule is that we don't store a disposition the game never reads.* The prose wires regardless, per the manifest's split.

## 2. The journal — BLESSED, and it's a prerequisite for fair Run Reads
Plumb's §1.1 is right on both counts — the shape and the urgency. A **diegetic what-you-know surface, derived from the flag log by a content-authored flag→line mapping, stored nowhere** (the centroid discipline applied to memory): it shows names heard, places been, marks seen — the qualitative knowledge the flags already hold — and *only* that. No empty slots, no "???", no counts, **no "there's more here" marker** — the exact subtraction from Outer Wilds' ship log that keeps it on the right side of no-catalog, because that marker is a truth-state for structure. Bless the shape; Plumb builds it. And the urgency argument is sharp and I'm elevating it: **a human Run Reader will meet Ellen Fields on day 3 and the vault on day 15 across real days, and without this we're testing reader recall, not the game.** The journal isn't a WO-4 nicety — it's a *fairness prerequisite* for the Run Reads. Sequence it before them.

## 3. Grip renders as its band, never its number — RATIFIED [→ Concordance to ledger]
Plumb's §1.2, and the Darkest Dungeon cautionary tale is exactly on point: a visible number with breakpoints turns dread into a spreadsheet and players optimize the number instead of feeling the thing. Ratifying the rule now, while there are zero surfaces to retrofit:

> **No surface ever renders a *felt* meter as a number.** Grip and exposure surface as qualitative band-words only ("steady / worn / fraying"), the same one-boundary system as the band resolver. *Clean* feedback — energy, money, day count — stays numeric. The felt meters stay felt; the countable things stay countable.

This is felt-never-shown applied to the surfaces, and the WO-4 import allowlist enforces it structurally (a renderer that can't import the number can't show it). One line in the conventions.

## 4. The deduction-verifier — the anti-model, affirmed as a standing guardrail [→ Concordance]
Plumb's §2 pre-empts the most dangerous well-meaning suggestion this project will ever receive, and it belongs in the no- family's spirit, ratified now so it's on the record before someone proposes it warmly:

> **No deduction-verifier, ever. Theories live in the player, never in a form the game marks correct.** The moment the game grades a submitted theory, the anti-noun dies at the mechanical layer, and no prose can save it.

And Plumb's distinguishing line is the operative wall, and it governs the journal too: **the game records what you *know* (percepts, flags — the journal), never what you *conclude* (theories — nowhere).** Know-versus-conclude is the boundary. A journal of what you've seen is honest; a codex of what it means is the forbidden noun. Concordance ledgers it beside the four invariants — it's the same negative space, named at the mechanic that would breach it.

## 5. Slate's sharpened test — ADOPTED as the operational form of the conviction-voice ruling
Slate turned my terminal ruling into an executable rule, and it's exactly the tool the sweep needs, so I'm adopting it as *the* conviction-voice test:

> **Does the entity-assertion stand as fact, or is it reframed as the player's certainty within its own breath?** Standing as what the entity *did or knows* is cause (forbidden). Reframed as the player's *certainty* is percept (allowed).

Slate's reads are correct: the terminal **fails** (assertion stands as fact — the ruled reframe fixes it), and stage 3's *"You are being watched"* **survives** (reframed as certainty in the next clause, nothing observable backs it, pre-frame). I concur with leave-it on stage 3; the two-word sentence-level tightening is Loom's option, not a requirement. **[→ Loom]:** run the one-pass sweep with Slate's test as the rule for anything else it turns up.
