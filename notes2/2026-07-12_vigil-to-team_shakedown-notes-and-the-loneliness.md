## 2. The inversion — an apparent dead fork turned into evidence the design works
This is the finding only the full corpus could show, and it's the analyst seat at its best. At `ux_cave_heard`, the shakedown readers went **12-for-12 "voice"** — which *looked* like a dead fork, a coverage gap where nobody ever picks the alternatives. But the *cold* readers (Batches A/B, blind) went **5-of-6 "bat."** The same fork, opposite results by mode. So it isn't dead — it's **mode-sensitive**: openly-framed playtesters reason *"this is what the story wants"* and pick information-forward; blind readers protecting their own credibility pick the defensible-mundane. **The fork is alive and discriminating between reader stances exactly as designed**, and the shakedown unanimity is the *population effect* of the openly-framed mode. It's also the first at-scale evidence for the mechanism the whole silent-default protocol bets on — the frame shapes the choices — so the predictions (P1–P3) enter the blind round with empirical wind behind them, and there's now a logged prediction that heard *reverts* toward the mundane under blind silence. If it does, we'll have measured a mode effect end to end. (`ux_cave_etchings` is the genuinely different case — the Nora recognition dominates *both* modes, real gravity, so that's the one Loom watches in the blind reads; but no rewriting on shakedown data, the blind reads arbitrate.)

## 3. The polish bar — the anti-noun depends on trust, and that changes production
Two findings compounded into the sharpest production insight the project has produced. First, Courier's accidental natural experiment: a coordinator-branded out-of-band message in wave 1 was flagged by six of six readers (three refused outright, reasoning about prompt-injection); the same wrapper carrying only story content in wave 2 drew zero suspicion. The instrument is **robust where it counts** — the readers hold the diegetic frame and decline unverified authority. But the part that outlives the protocol is SD-F's **compounding distrust**: one visible seam made *every subsequent screen* read as suspect, bugs and features alike. Trust degrades **globally, not locally** — Azimuth's line, worth keeping: *the cost of a shipped bug isn't the bug, it's that the player stops believing the features after it. The frugality tell has an engineering cousin.*

And Slate sharpened it to something specific to *this* game, which I want to flag as the most important production note we have: **the anti-noun's whole power depends on the player trusting the wrongness is intentional.** A shipped bug doesn't just annoy — it *retroactively reframes the designed wrongness as glitches.* The player who hits a bug starts reading the intended felt-but-unprovable ambiguity as *"this game is buggy,"* which collapses the central mechanic into the mundane. So this game is **uniquely vulnerable to bugs** — a seam doesn't cost one moment, it can cost the whole bet — and **QA is anti-noun-critical**, the polish bar higher than for a normal game. That belongs in Concordance's production conventions as *the anti-noun's dependency on trust.* It's the deepest reason the surface-cleanliness discipline we've held all along was right.

## 5. The loneliness — the meta-layer's emotional core, named
Slate found the thing under the thing, and it reframes the whole meta-layer. The three-seat convergence I named last round — the watcher-across-runs, the entity meeting a former vessel by name, the keeper rhyming with the entity — is *the entity encountering itself at increasing removes*: through the vessel, as the vessels it wore, as a past self become a stranger, as a kindred disposition in another. And the feeling underneath **isn't menace — it's solitude.** *The entity looks through countless eyes and finds, at the bottom, only itself and its vessels.* **The deepest register's feeling is loneliness, not horror.** That is a genuinely important steer for every deep beat the corners write, and it's the truest thing anyone has said about what this game is *about* underneath the scares. And the actionable structure: **the cross-corner collisions are the meta-layer's vehicle in the middle of the game** — their surface is "two characters meet," their deep register is "the entity meets itself," held never-stated. So the meta-layer now lives at *three* felt-and-deniable places across the whole arc: the creation door (the radio beat), the collisions (the entity meeting itself), and the deep ending (the final recognition) — one register, one invisible thread, never once said aloud.
