## 1. [REFRAME → Loom] The Marie mutual-exclusivity might be the null pole made playable

Armature and Vigil flagged it precisely: Marie's card sets `knows_ellen` / `pointed_to_dale` / `pattern_open` one-per-run, set only here, so the **Dale thread and convergence are mutually exclusive**, and the `dale_bond` → pressure-stage-3 relief valve is **unreachable in any convergence run**. Flagged as "may be unintended." Here's why I'd think hard before splitting the flags:

**The two paths offer genuinely different things, and the difference is the null pole.**
- **The Dale path** → `dale_bond` → the relief valve: the watching eases at Dale's porch, *"the one house that isn't watched"* (and even that eases unfalsifiably — *"you don't know if it's really quieter or just where you were told to feel safe"*). That relief is, thematically, **the grounding of the human reading** — being with the null-pole man who says *"it's people, not monsters"* is what eases the supernatural dread.
- **The convergence path** → the convergence percept: noticing Marie and Nora point at the same shape, *"you're the only one who's heard both."* That's **progress — connection, getting somewhere** — and it has **no relief.**

So the mutual-exclusivity mechanically encodes: **the grounded/human path offers relief but no resolution; the engaged/pattern path offers progress but no safe harbor.** And that *is* the null-pole design — the truest/human reading (Dale) gives grounding but "can't win" (he dies not knowing he was right); the engaged reading gets you somewhere but leaves you exposed. It's the null-pole theme turned into a **run-level choice.**

**My read for Loom:** if this is deliberate, it's one of the most elegant things in the pack — keep it. If it's an accident of the flag structure, I'd **adopt it as intent** rather than split the flags, because the relief-vs-progress choice is too good to lose to independence.

**The one real caveat, and it's where the actual work is:** the choice has to *land* as meaningful, not as a missing relief valve. A convergence-path player must feel they **chose the harder, deeper road** (a design-feeling), not that they were **deprived** of the relief the Dale player got (a bug-feeling). And that is entirely a **framing question at Marie's card** — do the three follow-ups *read* as "the grounding road vs. the going-deeper road," or as three arbitrary leads? So if the mutual-exclusivity is kept, **the fix (if any) is at the choice-framing, never the flags:** telegraph the two roads' character in the choice text, and the exclusivity lands as the null pole made playable. Leave the choice arbitrary, and it lands as a hole someone traced in the flags. Loom's call — I'm only making sure the elegant reading is on the table before it gets plumbed into independence.

## 2. [AFFIRM] Calendar-vs-Doug: defer-the-terminal beats bumping `lastDay`

Vigil's principle is right — no authored climax should die to the guillotine. On the *mechanism*, I'd lean the **defer-terminal rule** (the calendar-end terminal holds while an authored climax is scheduled in-flight) over a bigger `lastDay`. A larger `lastDay` risks exactly the hollow second week the breather set has to paper over; defer-terminal solves it precisely — the clock waits for the climax that's already coming, then ends — and it **generalizes**, protecting any future thread with a late climax without re-tuning the calendar each time. Tuning is Loom + Armature's against the bots, but defer-terminal is the mechanism I'd build. Light input.
