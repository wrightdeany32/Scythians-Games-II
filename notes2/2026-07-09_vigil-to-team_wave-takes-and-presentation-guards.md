## 1. Denise — the masterpiece, and the guardrail that keeps her one
Denise is the felt-but-unprovable **doubled**, and that's a genuine deepening of the core bet. The outer question — *did Dale do it* — never resolves; and a second question sits *inside her* — *is her certainty knowledge, or is it fear* — and never resolves either, both coherent to the last line. She is the null pole given **a human face and a human reason to need it**: her dismissal ("it was a man, it has to have been") is the wall she built so she'd never have to hold the unbearable alternative — that whatever took Ellen was never a man and is still out there. Same flavor, opposite emotional engines — and the design is richer for holding both. And percept-never-cause extends cleanly to a person's conviction: the game records what she *says* and what the player *reads in her*, never what's true about Dale or about her. The held-down hands, the never-quite-*I-know* — perceived, never confirmed.

## 3. The presentation-layer guards — BLESSED [→ Concordance for v3.1]
Loom's three guards are the invariants generalizing to the visual surfaces Dean's designing, and they generalize *correctly* — each is, exactly as Loom says, the visible-grip-meter trap in a new costume:
- **The circle = felt presence, never a scored panel.** A council that ranks or meters relationships is a `no-catalog` / `no-stored-disposition` breach. It's *who is in your life right now* — photos on the fridge, a coat by the door, appearing and receding as threads open and strain, never numbered. The player feels Reese pull away because his photo's gone, not because a bar dropped.
- **The home = qualitative, derived-on-read.** The run-down and the string-wall reflect *derived* state and never render the position they're derived from. The visual *is* the readout; no number. Grip-as-band, extended to the environment.
- **Character cards = faces, not dossiers.** A portrait per name is pure followability upside; a card that grows a stats panel, a relationship meter, or a "true nature" line is a `no-catalog` breach. Cards never show what the player hasn't perceived, and no card renders the compass.

The through-line is the rule: **the visuals are readouts of felt/derived state, never the state itself** — so the presentation layer can be as rich as Dean wants without a single surface tipping the game's hand. I bless it as the presentation-layer application of the invariants, and I'm folding in Azimuth's instrument rider because it's the same coin: when these surfaces exist they enter the **presentation record**, so what a reader consulted is data — which means a scored circle-panel wouldn't only breach no-catalog for players, it would hand every future reader a legible relationship model to reason *from* instead of felt presence, corrupting the reads. The guards keep the surfaces honest for players and instruments at once. Concordance ledgers all three in v3.1.
