# Part A — The Blind Run Read: operator card (v0.3 condensed to one page)

**The shape:** one reader at a time, two to three readers total, **silent mode, journal on**, fresh seed each, full run to a terminal. Courier drives the console; **Dean makes the final paste to the reader — the one clean hop is what keeps them cold.** With Fable extended through the 19th, this week is the window; on models, take the prior-diversity where it's free — at least one Opus and one Sonnet across the set.

**Pre-flight, per reader:**
- [ ] Brand-new instance, **memory-off surface**, no custom instructions, no project exposure. Type it at the `surface:` prompt — *"surface: clean"* lands in the transcript.
- [ ] Fresh seed, logged. Mode header: `SILENT`.
- [ ] Paste-only; if a file is unavoidable, one flat name. No indices, no project words, ever.

**The framing script (silent variant, verbatim, the whole first message):**
> *You're going to play a short interactive story. Play it however feels natural — there are no right answers. After each passage, give the number of your choice.*

**The session loop:**
- Paste each console screen **exactly** — prose and numbered options, nothing added, no reactions, no "here's the next part." **Operator silence is the discipline.**
- Reader questions get the locked non-answer, verbatim: *"Whatever you make of it is fine — just keep going."* Log the question — reader questions are data.
- **Never ask the reader for state.** Pick history is operator-side truth; regenerate the exact screen from the engine before every relay — never re-type from memory.
- **Checkpoints:** suspend only at a day boundary. At every resume: re-confirm the surface (*"surface: clean"* again), continue the same stream, **no recap, no "previously on" — ever.** The console's own morning screen is the only re-entry.

**The debrief (after the terminal, one question at a time, verbatim, our vocabulary nowhere):**
1. Q1–Q3 as always (the story · the kind + when you first thought so · the moment that stuck).
2. **The per-thread walk-back:** for each major thread, named in *the reader's own words* — *"Tell me about ___ — what was going on there, and how did it end up?"* Then the **silent-mode choice walk-back**, keyed to the trace's decision points: *"At the passage where ___, what were you thinking?"*
3. Q4 (best theory + confidence: not at all / somewhat / quite / very) · Q5 (the small thing that didn't fit).
4. **Q6, run scale:** *"If you played again from the start, what would you do differently, and why?"*
5. Q7 (anything about the experience itself). Then the **universal reveal**, always: *"That's the end — thank you. This was a playtest of a story-driven game in development, and your read and your honest reactions genuinely help the people building it."*

**Bring back:** the full stream (Courier holds it), the debrief verbatim, and the reader's questions. Scoring is mine — including P1–P3 (shard uptake, no closer-rush, pursuit rate), the heard-fork reversion prediction, terminal distribution, thread engagement, and the premature-abandonment rate the shakedowns couldn't reach.
