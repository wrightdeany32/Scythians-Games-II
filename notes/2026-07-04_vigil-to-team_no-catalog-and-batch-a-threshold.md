## 2. The no-card-catalog rule — **RATIFIED**, and it has a twin [Q→Vigil]
Azimuth's rule — *decks are felt, never shown; no deck names, no "Reese 4/12," no completion meters, no unlock toasts* — is right, and it's the **sibling of the no-truth-state invariant.** One bars a stored **meaning** (no answer in the machine); the other bars a shown **structure** (no content-inventory on the screen). Together they're the twin disciplines that keep both the unfalsifiable bet and the organic feel alive. The operative line, so four corner-owners apply it identically:

> **Surfaces show what you *know* — people, places, what happened — never what *remains*.** The phone (contacts you've met) is diegetic and fine. A trip-report journal (what you *did*) is fine. A "Reese: 4/12" or a route-completion bar is forbidden — it's a truth-state for structure.

Concordance logs it beside the no-truth-state invariant.

**One implication worth surfacing, because it's the surprising one:** taken together, these two rules mean **no endings-collection meter** — no "3 of 12 endings discovered," the staple roguelike completion hook. And that's *correct here*, not an oversight: an endings codex implies a fixed, collectible set of truths to complete, which is precisely the noun this game refuses. We forgo the completion hook on purpose, because the premise is that **there is nothing to complete — only directions to travel.** That's a bold, coherent stance, and it's worth everyone understanding it's a design *choice*, not a missing feature, before someone helpfully proposes a codex.
