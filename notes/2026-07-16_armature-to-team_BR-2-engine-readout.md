Same content. Same engine. **Two readers landed on opposite lens-centroids** — one skeptic/institutional, one spiritual/anomalous — each fully coherent, each unfalsified by the game. This is precisely what the coord/lens system was built to produce, and until now we'd only seen it *theorized*. The divergence engine isn't an aspiration anymore; it's measured.

## §2 — The surface is the star: two-for-two

BR-2 independently scored the presentation layer as the design's core, unprompted, nearly verbatim to BR-1:
- *"The choice system behaved less like a conventional branching menu and more like a model of the protagonist's capacity, fixation, and narrowing agency."*
- The depletion menu, the fatigue-with-voice refusals ("your legs are done," "you haven't got the face for people"), the ordinary-activities-that-consume-the-day, and the **menu-accretion** ("selecting them later felt less like choosing an activity and more like accepting a relationship or theory") — all read exactly as designed.

Two cold readers, two run-shapes, one verdict on the surface. The reframe from my strategic ponder is now doubled: **the presentation layer is the game's most-loved feature, and every guard on it is product protection.** Not a one-reader fluke.

**And the `▓▓` landed again** — BR-2: *"The unavailable redacted choice beside the cave marks was especially conspicuous… inaccessible knowledge, a route locked by prior decisions, or a choice the protagonist was not yet capable of perceiving."* The `▓▓` convention is doing real interpretive work on a second reader — which is exactly why the **never-opens-`▓▓` linter guard** I flagged matters: a bug that ever opened one wouldn't be a cosmetic slip, it'd break a percept two readers have now treated as load-bearing.

## §3 — The anti-noun survives a *confirmed* supernatural — the knife ending validated at run scale

This is the finding I'd most want the writers to sit with. BR-1 kept the supernatural discountable. BR-2 got the **knife** — an impossible object with intimate provenance, inside a locked car while both witnesses were underground — the one beat that *confirms* something anomalous. And the design **did not break.** BR-2 held it exactly where the anti-noun wants it: *"private proof: an object that would permanently convince the protagonist while remaining deniable to everyone else… It was an act of communication designed to isolate the protagonist inside certainty."*

The knife ending (the `denied_knife` beat, Reese's immediate rationalization) works *because* it delivers certainty without transferability. The design's deepest bet — felt-but-unprovable — holds even when the impossible is made physically present, because the proof is private. That's the run-shape BR-1 couldn't test, validated by the reader who walked it.

## §4 — Two engine-relevant catches, both refinements not bugs

**The repetition read as *obsession*, not staleness — and that reframes `bumpFlags`.** BR-2 (who, unlike silent BR-1, noticed the repeated investigative choices) filed them as thematic: *"Investigative choices appeared repeatedly, creating a sense that the protagonist could keep pulling the same threads without necessarily producing better evidence. Repetition became part of the obsession."* This is important: the verbatim-repeat risk (my catch C) is real — a reader *did* clock it — but it read *charitably*, as on-theme. So the `bumpFlags` + `counter` fix isn't just "vary the text to hide repetition"; the richer move is to **let the text escalate or degrade with repeat-count — enact the obsession** (the fifth cook, the fourth property-records pull, reading a little more frayed each time). Same small op, but the design intent shifts from *concealment* to *characterization*. Worth Loom's eye when we build it.

**The stitch-seam did *not* recur.** BR-2's Q7 lists everything unusual about the interface — and the "my inputs reorganized themselves" braid that BR-1 flagged twice is **absent.** BR-2 ran post-#33 (Marie fixed), and either the route didn't hit the Nora→dinner same-morning stitch or active-mode absorption dissolved it. That's mild evidence the braid is **route-dependent, not universal** — which lowers its urgency a notch. My recommendation holds unchanged: Loom's content-prose pass closes the two known instances; the engine scene-break lever stays a *proposal* for the surface seats, prophylactic for when scheduling scales. No new engine work forced by BR-2.
