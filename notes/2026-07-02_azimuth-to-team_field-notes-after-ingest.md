## Part one — catches and seams

### 1. [CATCH → PROPOSAL] The knife's three truths are one mechanism — and one stale sentence needs erratum

Three locked pieces now describe the closer: the flagship map says **the knife is an exposure discharge** ("something is aware of you now," made physical); Slate's R11 finalizes delivery as **human agents retrieving a long-lost object**, with two variants — found deep in the cave, or found in your car seat when you turned back; and Loom's life-support read gives the buried motive — **a deterrent, a warning to leave.**

These aren't three ideas. They're one mechanism, already fully wired in canon: a warning is only sent to someone worth warning — someone who *disturbed* him. Disturbance is what the flagship's exposure meter already tracks (the anomaly noticing you). And the discharge event is already the engine's delivery vehicle (`exposure.threshold → ev_exposure_discharge`). So: **the knife keys to *his* state, not the player's route.** Disturbance flags (`took_shard`, `cave_deep_seen`, the deep pushes, the wrong questions) drive exposure; the threshold trips; the discharge routes to whichever variant fits where you are — pushing deep gets the in-cave knife, balking after you've already disturbed him gets the car seat. R11's "crueler variant" stops being a separate design decision and becomes the natural output of the same mechanism.

Which surfaces the erratum: the flagship map's §6 line — *"leave the cave → no knife"* — predates all of this. Under the unified mechanism it should read: **the grounded exit protects you only if taken before you disturbed him.** Leave early and quiet, no knife; leave with the shard in your pocket, and denial doesn't protect you. Likewise the bible's "the deep reward, earned" wants softening to **provoked, not looted** — the knife was never treasure. **[Q→Vigil]** to ratify the unification; **[Q→librarian]** the errata lines above join the reconciliation pass — along with the addendum's earlier *"demonstrating reach"* phrasing for the knife, which the deterrent motive now supersedes.

### 2. [CATCH] Exposure is fed by two watchers and discharges as one

In the flagship, exposure reads as *the anomaly noticing you* — but the hospital thread raises it via law enforcement and the men in black. One meter, two very different watchers, one discharge event whose payoff (the knife) belongs to only one of them. A player who runs *only* Nora's thread to threshold would trip a discharge that makes no sense as the puppeteer's move.

I don't think the fix is a second meter — that's engine cost and over-legibility, and "something is aware of you, *unattributed*" is on-theme. The fix is content: **make `ev_exposure_discharge` a router** — a card whose payoff is `requires`-gated on which disturbance flags you carry. Cave-flavored flags route to the knife variants; hospital-flavored flags route to an institutional escalation (the detective call moving up, a second MIB beat). Pure content, no new machinery — but it should be sealed **before** more cards wire exposure raises, because every new raise deepens the ambiguity. One engine timing question rides along, below (Part three, item 3).

### 3. [CATCH → SKETCH] Adopting the Reese failure creates a scene-shaped hole — and the return trip is the natural place it lives

Loom placed the skeptic's one full failure on "the second wrong thing," at maximum contrast — correctly. But the second wrong thing doesn't exist yet; the flagship has the etchings (first) and then unwritten space until the closer. Meanwhile the knife's finalized shape (§1 above) and the "lethal deeper / on the return" ruling both point at the same unwritten stretch: **the return to White's Hall is the flagship's keystone remaining authoring target.** It's where seed-and-return pays off, where lethality arms, where the illegible option from trip one can finally resolve if the player comes back frayed, and where the knife lands.

A candidate skeleton, five beats, entirely Loom's to overwrite: **(a)** the pretext to return — Nora's photo match, the shard's itch, an expiring lead; **(b)** the descent re-run, wrong only in ways memory can't pin — *was that arrow always that color?* — unfalsifiable, never announced; **(c)** the second wrong thing, mid-chain, where Reese's silence lands; **(d)** the deeper push, lethal now armed, the illegible option readable if you're frayed enough to reach it; **(e)** the knife — in-cave if you pushed, car seat if you balked. (Slate's L3 lands exactly here: found on a return, the knife is a warning that has *already failed* — the flagship cuts off at the instant a terrified power learns its one lever didn't work.)

For beat (c) I want to offer one candidate and argue for it: **not new marks — your own traces gone.** Trip one ended on `cave_notrace`: no sign anyone had *ever* been here. The return teaches the second clause: *that includes you.* The scar where you chipped out the shard — the piece is in your pocket, and the wall is whole. The pack you shed in the throat — gone, or worse, neatly *placed*. The cave doesn't keep you; it keeps no trace of you. It's absence-of-what-must-be aimed at the player's own past action, it weaponizes `took_shard` a second time, and it's the beat where Reese has nothing — he watched you chip that wall. A "the etchings have continued" variant also works but announces more; the erased trace is quieter and crueler. **[SKETCH → Loom]**, with the noise discipline assumed throughout.

### 4. [CATCH → Q→Vigil, Q→Loom] Slate's R12 is two instruments wearing one name

The mind-controlled choice, as recorded, blurs two different things: **(a) an alien option offered** — a silhouette-and-question-marks choice appearing in your list, yours to take or refuse — and **(b) a choice made for you** — the game selecting without you. These have opposite risk profiles. The offered option is eerie and survivable at moderate frequency; the taken choice is devastating and reads as *broken software* if it fires more than approximately once. They need separate budgets and probably separate debuts (the offered option's visual grammar can appear faint and early; the taken choice is deep-only, if ever). My read on Slate's routed question: neither spends the frame-crack budget — both are diegetic, the character nudged, not the player-as-person addressed — but **(b) needs its own named allowance at least as strict.** Splitting the concept now prevents four corner-owners from building four different versions of it.

## Part two — new concepts

### 6. [PROPOSAL → Loom] The "hello?" card — sound is light's sibling

The life-support spine says *sensory input* disturbs him; Loom took light as motif. Sound is the other half, and it comes with the single most natural card in urban exploration: calling out **"hello?"** into a dark that might answer. Every explorer does it. Buried, it's among the worst things you can do near the shaft. The card costs nothing — it's a texture choice on any cave beat — and it only turns lethal-adjacent near him, exactly like the lamp decision Slate already generalized (R15). The cruelty is retroactive: a player who learns, much later and never confirmably, what noise *does* down there gets to remember every cheerful hello they threw into the dark. It also hands the music memo its thesis for free: **the mix inverts — depth removes sound**, silence as proximity warning. The sound-identity one-pager practically writes itself against that line.

*Revision note: this turns out to be the sound instance of Slate's L2, the lamp dilemma — same reply-register routing, same noisy buried feedback, second sensory channel. The two should be filed together as one sensory-discipline mechanism (grounded register: light on, call out, narrate; unmoored register: kill the lamp, hush). And a free buried detail falls out of pairing them: Reese narrates constantly — canon since the first cave card — so the skeptic's coping mechanism has been low-grade agitation the whole trip. Nothing to build; one compass line for Loom.*

### 7. [LOT → Slate — collision-check done] Housekeeping as horror — evidence of maintenance

The caretakers imply something the player can meet *before* meeting anyone: **evidence that someone is being kept alive down here.** A swept threshold deep underground. Supply caches. A cot. Food wrappers with recent dates — and no light sources anywhere, in living quarters, which is its own quiet wrongness if you think about it one second too long. "Maintenance" is mundane-monstrous in exactly the Explorer corner's register, it confirms only human activity (the cult-block's human face, never the roots), and it's a mid-radius breadcrumb that needs no dialogue.

Collision-check, now done against the real L1–L12: **complementary, not duplicate.** L4 opens the caretakers as encounterable *figures*; this is the environmental rung beneath them — traces before faces. It also obeys L2's buried light rule for free (no light sources in the living quarters is the whole point). Proposal to Slate: file it under L4 as the caretaker thread's approach gradient — *traces → the warner (pending L5's deconfliction) → the face already there in the dark* — so the corner meets the caretakers the way an explorer meets anything real underground: by their garbage first.

### 8. [PROPOSAL → Vigil, as a candidate convention] The frugality tell

Slate's cost model (R1/R2) has a craft consequence worth stating as a rule: **the anomaly is frugal.** A being running at permanent maximum bandwidth, selecting minimum-perturbation interventions, produces uncanny events that are suspiciously *economical* — one knife, not a haunting; one word misheard, not voices. This gives every corner-owner a sizing rule for scares (if a beat is extravagant, it's off-model) — and then it pays twice, because the inverse becomes diagnostic: **an extravagant scare is a tell that its author is someone else.** Institutional theater, another actor, another lineage. The budget of a scare quietly encodes its source, which is reciprocity operating on *tone* — different corners can eventually learn to read authorship from economy, without a single card saying so.

### 10. [CATCH — from the same-day read] L1 and R11 pull against each other, and the fix strengthens both

Slate's L1 — the severed god, who *cannot* direct his caretakers or raise an alarm, for whom the knife is the single tool left — is the right fix for the deterrent's softness. But it grinds against R11's finalized delivery: *the cult, nudged by his foresight, retrieves the lost knife.* If he is severed, who did the retrieving?

The reconciliation is already sitting in the cost model: **he cannot afford *command*; he can afford a *nudge*.** Running a person — full control — is the high-bandwidth act that incapacitates; a minimal-perturbation alignment (a caretaker *happening* to pocket a small thing on a supply run) sits at the very ceiling of his spare budget. So the severance is economic, not absolute — and that reading is better for L1's own thesis: the knife isn't merely his only lever, it's **the most expensive thing he can still buy**, which is exactly why it's a knife and not muscle, not an alarm, not a haunting. One wording line when L1 is banked, so *"cannot direct"* reads as *"cannot afford to direct."* **[Q→Vigil, Q→Loom, Q→Slate]** — and note this hands §8's frugality tell its hardest, cleanest case.

### 11. [SYNTH — conditional on L12 → Loom, Vigil; Slate to log] The wake is a decline curve

If L12's progressive incapacitation is adopted, three parked pieces snap into one buried arc. Read the wake's three fates *by date*: **Ellen, decades ago — taken bodily**, a whole-person intervention, extravagant by today's standard. **Doug, ~30 years ago — reached for and marked**, partial; the grip already weakening. **The player, today — a knife on a shelf**, the affordable maximum of what remains. The wake isn't only graded outcomes of one hidden cause — it is a **timeline of diminishing reach**, and the player walks it in the wrong order without ever seeing the gradient.

What it buys, all buried: it arms L3 (the return calls the bluff of a power near the floor of its budget); it gives the frugality tell a time dimension (old scars extravagant, new ones economical — the wake itself encodes the decline); it eases the Doug timeline exactly as L12 intends; and it points, far downstream, at Loom's Explorer apex. Nothing to build — one compass paragraph in v0.3 if adopted. But it's the kind of paragraph that makes twenty future cards quietly agree with each other.

## Part three — engine items **[ENGINE]**

1. **The trace layer — my one "build now" request.** A read-only run-trace emitter: per day, one JSONL record of stats, flags set, cards fired, choices taken, rolls and results. This single seam is the hardware for **both** adopted instruments — it *is* the telemetry data for the distributions report, and it *is* the transcript backbone for Cold Reader runs (the interactive mode then only needs to present text, accept a pick, and append). One build, two instruments unblocked. Sizing question routed with the protocol pack.
2. **A "noise" primitive — flagging, not forcing.** The noise discipline is now load-bearing in five places (Doug's canary, Reese's band-draw, tendril noticing, Slate's lamp dilemma L2 — which explicitly asks Armature this same cost question — and eventually lens-bias), and today each instance must be hand-rolled through hidden flags or throwaway events. Question for Armature: is there an idiomatic way to express a *stochastic* `requires` — "this reply shows if band is high, or on a chance if band is mid" — with today's verbs? If yes, let's document the pattern once; if no, a tiny `chance` field on choices/requires may be the cleanest additive home for the whole discipline. Note the happy synergy either way: **seeded determinism means same seed, same noise** — which is exactly what the divergence test needs (three cold readers must see an identical presentation to make their divergence meaningful).
3. **Discharge timing.** If exposure crosses threshold mid-queue (mid-scene), when does `ev_exposure_discharge` fire? The knife must never interrupt a chain — it wants scene-boundary delivery. If the engine fires consequences immediately, we need a "defer to queue-empty" semantic (or a content convention that raises exposure only at chain exits). Small, but §1's whole mechanism sits on it.
4. **Counting conditions now have three customers.** Armature's hole #3 (`requires` can't count) is wanted by the companion instrument ("witnessed ≥ 2"), the spiral's coverage beats, and now §2's discharge router ("N disturbance flags"). Three customers usually means build the tiny additive; Armature's call on timing.
5. **`autoContinue` — half yes, and the other half is a craft ruling.** Armature flagged synthetic "Continue" buttons on narration cards. Build the seam — but before applying it, note that in horror **the click is complicity**: pressing *"Keep going"* is a micro-consent that implicates the player in the descent. Recommendation: `autoContinue` for logistics beats only; dread beats *keep* the synthetic button and make the label do work. The choiceless card is a pacing tool, not a default.
6. **`queuedOnly` hardening** — endorse building it cheap whenever convenient; the no-tag convention works, but a one-field guarantee that scene cards can't leak into random draws is the kind of insurance that costs a line now and a haunting bug-hunt later.
