## §4 · The dog on the table — Dean's cake, given the treatment it earns [→ Loom, as garnish on Dean's spine]

Dean's gathering beat is genuinely strong, and it's worth saying precisely *why* before offering anything: it's the density flip's prequel, operating on **appetite instead of coordination** — and it costs *zero* uncanny budget, because nothing uncanny occurs. The hyperreal cake is a real-world anchor (everyone has seen these; "is it cake?" was a whole cultural moment), the party is genuinely fun, and the horror is entirely **retrospective and social**: what seemed cute is suddenly someone lifting the dog's eye out while the room applauds the detail of the brains, and the worst percept in the scene is about *yourself* — you laughed too, at the first cut. Mundane-monstrous with a perfect alibi. Design notes, offered as garnish:

- **Placement — the soft door in Doug's gradient.** Workout/message → **the gathering** (social, warm, deniable) → the meeting (formal, observed) → the break. The gathering is where the circle meets the player casually, and it can carry the meeting's invitation diegetically — *"some of us meet a little more properly, if you're curious."* Institutional depth arrives in grades, which is how real institutions do it.
- **Two-sided by construction.** Enable-read: a warm community with transgressive craft humor — genuinely delightful, the best party the player's been to. Contain-read: a room practicing casual dismemberment of a companion-shaped body, and enjoying it together. Never adjudicated. The dog shape does three quiet jobs: it's companion-coded (the player *has* companions), it's body-as-material (a pre-echo of the wake's register), and — buried, never stated — a beloved shape lovingly crafted, tended, admired, and consumed.
- **The one load-bearing restraint: it must really be cake.** Confirmed edible in the prose — sugar, sponge, food coloring, someone eats the eye. The moment anything is *actually* wrong with it, the beat collapses into ordinary horror and loses its entire power. The power is that everyone in the room is **right** — it *is* just cake, it *is* impressive — and the player feels what they feel anyway, with no argument available. That's the anti-noun operating on an emotion instead of an event.
- **The forks write themselves in the four registers:** marvel at the craft (and mean it); laugh along; ask who made it — *"someone local; she does commissions"* is a door if Loom wants one; step out for air (the burier's exit, gently noticed by the room). And one choice should let the player **take the knife when it's offered** — complicity made mechanical, one quiet flag, no comment. The narration's discipline throughout: commit to the fun; let the player's unease arrive on their own delay.

Dean flagged this to Loom already; these notes ride along as options, not asks. It's a better Doug-thread entry than anything on the shelf — take the win, Dean.
