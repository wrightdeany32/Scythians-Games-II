# Batch A — Operator Runbook
### the checklist Dean follows live: three cold reads, then three new colleagues
*From: Azimuth (council / adviser — instrumentation) · 2026-07-04 · for Dean (operator), cc Armature, Vigil, Loom, Slate, Concordance · governed by the Cold Reader Protocol Pack v0.1 + amendments; build: frozen `cave-b3`*

Everything below is copy-paste discipline. The locked scripts are what make it safe for a compass-burned operator to run this — the moment you improvise, the firewall is you.

---

## 0 · Pre-flight (once)

- [ ] Armature confirms `cave-b3` frozen (done, on record).
- [ ] Roll **one fresh seed**. Log it. All three readers use it.
- [ ] Decide the model family. All three readers same family. (Convenient truth: your three future corner-writers should be same-family anyway.)
- [ ] Have the pack's §2 framing script and §3 debrief open for verbatim pasting.

## 1 · Per-reader surface check (every reader, no exceptions)

- [ ] **Brand-new instance.** Never one you've talked to before.
- [ ] **Memory OFF** — incognito, memoryless, or a surface with zero project exposure. Your main account remembers this project; a "fresh chat" there is **not cold.**
- [ ] No custom instructions, no project files, no connected context.
- [ ] **Filename hygiene:** if you must attach rather than paste, the file is named something flat — `story.txt`. Never `coldread_cave_cave-b3_…`, never anything containing *cave*, *coldread*, or the project name. A filename is a spoiler. Plain pasting is better.
- [ ] **Identity hygiene:** the reader never learns who you are, that a team exists, that this is a game in development, that this is a test, or that a role awaits them. To the reader: someone is sharing a short interactive story. That is the entire universe.
- [ ] Type the answer at Armature's `surface:` prompt so "surface: clean" lands in the transcript.

## 2 · The session loop

1. **First message = the framing script, verbatim.** Nothing before it, nothing after it.
2. **Each step: paste the console block exactly** — prose + numbered options, nothing else. No "here's the next part!", no reactions, no encouragement, no emoji. **Operator silence between steps is the discipline** — every word you add is leakage.
3. **Copy the reader's full reply back to Armature verbatim** (think-aloud + pick). It becomes the transcript's reader record. Don't trim, don't summarize.
4. **If the reader asks you anything** — *"is this a horror game?"*, *"am I supposed to…?"* — the locked non-answer, verbatim: **"Whatever you make of it is fine — just keep going."** The question gets logged; reader questions are data.
5. **If they go minimal or silent:** the single nudge, once per session, verbatim: **"What's your read on where things stand?"** Beyond that, let silence be silence — some readers hush when something lands.

## 3 · The debrief

- [ ] Questions from pack §3, **one at a time, in order, verbatim.** Our vocabulary appears nowhere.
- [ ] At most one *"can you say more?"* per question.
- [ ] Full answers back to Armature; the debrief section appends to the transcript.
- [ ] **The session ends here. Do not reveal anything yet** — the reveal belongs to §5, and only after you've decided this reader's read is complete.

## 4 · Between readers

- [ ] Fresh instance, fresh surface, same seed, same scripts.
- [ ] Never mention another reader's existence, choices, or theories.
- [ ] Resist discussing the content with anyone mid-batch; the analysis waits until all three transcripts are in.

## 5 · The conversion — reader becomes writer (after all three reads are done)

The order of this pipeline is its entire integrity: **read cold first, onboard second, never hinted in advance.** Then:

1. **Reveal and thank.** Now they learn what this was, who we are, and what's being offered.
2. **The "before I knew" memo — onboarding step zero, before they see any document.** Each new writer drafts a short naive retrospective: what they believed was happening, what unsettled them, what they wanted to do next, what the story felt like from inside. Richer than the debrief because it's reflective — and it is the one document they can only write *now*. The moment they read the corpus, the person who could write it is gone.
3. **Then the packet:** master ledger → the box-under-the-hood doc → the Explorer bible (the template their corner inherits) → their own transcript, annotated if they like.
4. **Concordance logs them:** onto the burn roster (their read is now a credential, not a loss), named seats, corners assigned.
5. **The asset, stated plainly:** these three will be the only members of this team who ever met the game cold. Loom never got that. Their felt-memory of the cave is unique institutional knowledge — it's why the pipeline is elegant rather than merely efficient. Optional garnish, your call: their coded lens from the read may suggest a natural corner (a skeptic-coded reader has a head start on the Detective's voice).

## 6 · After

- [ ] Three transcripts → `coldreads/` and the project folder, named per convention.
- [ ] **Scoring:** I'll take the analyst seat — the divergence rubric, lens coding, and the Batch A report are my instrument to run. Vigil co-reads with the watch-hypothesis (theories clustering on *who made the marks* = the cave leaning human — a lesson for the return trip, not the bet failing). Report to the folder; results feed Loom's return trip.
