## Part 6 — Dean's dome-cluster, and the card-coordinate system
This is the freshest idea in the batch and it's a real one — it's the **composition layer** the map has been implying, and it slots onto a seam Armature already built. Let me lay out what it is, what it connects to, and where the discipline goes.

**What it is.** In *Surviving Mars*, any dome can be the center of a cluster, and the ring around it supplies whatever that dome lacks. Translated to narrative space: **at any point in the volume, the mix of cards a player draws is a dominant-flavor core plus a complementary neighborhood** — the current sector at high density, the two adjacent corners at medium, the opposite corner rare (never zero). That single recipe buys three things at once: each corner reads *distinct* (an Explorer run is mostly Explorer beats), the world stays *continuous* (you still brush the neighbors), and no run goes *monotone* — which is exactly the gap you named in the cave, where a linear all-Explorer stretch wants a lawyer-with-a-case or a witnessed accident dropped in to give it texture.

**What it connects to — and this is the satisfying part:**
- It **is** the mechanism behind R5's "arcing earns breadth." Arcing through an adjacent corner *means* drawing more of that corner's beats, which *is* accumulating its lens. Your dome-cluster and Slate's three-currency map are the same insight from two ends — the draw-recipe and the epistemic outcome.
- It rides the **`drawWeight` seam Armature already built** — the same chokepoint lens-bias uses. Proximity-weighting is a coordinate-distance multiplier at that one site. So the engine hook already exists; the dome-cluster is the *design principle* that drives it.
- It gives the **coordinate system a second job.** Loom's authoring-map reads coordinates to find gaps and clumps (production management); the dome-cluster reads the *same* coordinates to weight the draw (the mixing recipe). One coordinate schema, two uses — authoring and composition.

**"Assigning numbers to the cards."** Each card carries an **(x, y, z) coordinate** — grip-lean, sanction/breadth, enable↔contain — plus its **depth ring** and its **tags** (sector, deck, texture). Those numbers place the card in the volume, and *two* systems read them: the production-map (where are my gaps) and the dome-cluster draw (what's near the player). Crucial, per the Part-3 ruling: **these numbers are authoring geometry, not player stats.** The only mechanical state stays grip (+ tier). So "the numbers inform what belongs around a card" resolves to: the coordinate says where the card sits; the recipe weights its neighbors; nobody tracks a sanction score.

**One refinement to your framing.** A dome's ring provides what the dome *lacks* — so the neighborhood's job isn't just "less of the same," it's *complementary variety*: the breather, the tonal shift, the cross-thread tendril the core can't supply itself. Proximity-weighting is the mechanical floor; the authoring judgment of *which* neighbor-beats give the right variety is the craft on top. The recipe picks the density; the author picks the flavor.

**The discipline (same as always).** This is the composition layer, and it's the **same deferred family as lens-bias and coordinate-draw** — spec it now, build it on `drawWeight` when there's enough content across sectors to tune against. The flagship does *not* need it (it's deliberately one linear thread, proving the pieces). And the load-bearing rule it shares with lens-bias: **weighting, never gating.** Every sector's beats stay drawable everywhere — rarer far from home, never impossible — or the corners become sealed silos and both "any ending from any start" and reciprocity quietly break. Your "opposite sector least common" already has this right; I'm just naming that *least* must never become *never*.

So: **adopt the dome-cluster as the composition principle,** file it with the coordinate-draw/lens-bias specs (Azimuth's lane, or a fresh one-pager), build it on the existing seam post-content. It's not new engine — it's the design that finally gives the tag axis something rich to do.
