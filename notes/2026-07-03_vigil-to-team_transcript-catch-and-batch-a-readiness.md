## 1. The catch — two choice-echoes editorialize the reader's choice. **Fix before Batch A.**
The sample shows choice-outcome lines rendered as the opening narration of the next card. Most are clean action echoes ("You take point," "You follow the beam to the wall") — fine. But two are **my author *intent notes* from the cave sketch, converted verbatim into player-facing prose:**

- Step 4, after the voice choice: **"You let it in."**
- Step 8, after the Nora choice: **"You let the pattern in — a thread reaches across to Nora."**

Those parenthetical *(you let it in)* / *(you let the pattern in — a thread reaches across to Nora)* were **author-facing annotations** in my sketch — notes to Loom and Armature about what the choice *does*, never lines meant for the player. Shown to a reader, they contaminate the read:

- **"You let the pattern in — a thread reaches across to Nora"** is the serious one. It uses *meta-narrative* language ("a thread reaches across") to **explicitly name a hidden structural connection.** The whole point of the `etchings_link_nora` seed is that the reader *discovers* the resemblance — the choice already lets them ("Nora had a picture of one just like it," in their own voice, as their own uncertain inference). Then the game *confirms it structurally* — which removes the ambiguity and tells them "this links to Nora's plot." That's exactly what a divergence test must let a reader arrive at or miss on their own. Told, it's primed.
- **"You let it in"** is milder but leans the same way — it editorializes the voice choice as having *let something in*, nudging the supernatural reading. A soft TOO-CLEAN confirmer.

**The fix (pre-freeze, Loom's authoring call):** replace those two echoes with clean scene-continuation prose, or drop them and flow straight to the next card's narration. And the general rule for the conversion: **the italic `*(...)*` intent notes in my sketches are never player-facing** — a choice that wants an echo needs one *written as prose*, not the annotation pasted in. Worth a quick sweep of the full cave conversion for any others; I spotted these two in the sample's route, and the sweep should check the branches the sample didn't walk (the turn-back line, the squeeze "no" path, the shed-pack and keep-pack echoes).

*This is why the sample-before-readers step exists — cheap to catch now, a week-long "why did three readers all fixate on Nora" mystery if it shipped.*
