## §2 — The one big open question, and my two cents

**Plumb's flagged schema conflict (the "leans attuned/grounded" X-axis problem) is bigger than it might look from one report — I can confirm that now, having read every file.** Nearly every "introspective, no stat" option across the *entire* wave uses this pattern — I count roughly 20+ instances across Marie, Doug (all four beats), Nora, Dale, the grave, all three pressure stages, the return, and the ending. This isn't a corner case; it's the load-bearing texture of *how the player's inner life gets expressed* everywhere in the pack. Until this is ruled, that entire texture has, as Plumb put it, nowhere to land.

For what it's worth: I lean toward Plumb's option 3 (a derived-only `attune` component, read solely by the ending-selector and telemetry) over the other two. Dropping the coordinate (option 1) throws away real signal the ending explicitly wants ("the final centroid"), and making it a grip delta (option 2) is worse than a compromise — it would make introspection *farmable* and quietly reopen the exact wall (X=grip, no engine coupling) the ruling exists to protect. Option 3 costs nothing mechanically and gives every one of those ~20 beats somewhere real to go. Not my ruling to make, but wanted to register the count and the lean, since I'm probably the only person who's read all twenty instances back to back.

## §3 — Connections already in the pack that are worth naming (some may be accidental, and are better for it)

- **Marie's `asked_pattern` and Nora's daytrip are quietly the same question, asked in two completely different registers, and neither file cross-references the other.** Marie: *"Has anyone else ever gone missing out here? Has anyone — changed?"* Nora: mapping disappearances against the research center's active years. Both land on "a pattern of people vanishing," one folk, one institutional, and the game never lets them touch. That's either a deliberate echo or a happy accident of parallel authorship — either way it's exactly the kind of convergence the design wants, and I'd leave it exactly as unconfirmed as it is. (If there's ever room for one more beat, a version where a player who did *both* — `pattern_open` and `nora_daytrip_done` — gets one tiny percept of *noticing they've now heard this from two people who've never met* would be a nice, cheap payoff. Optional; not urging it.)
- **The quarry line's empirical performance is worth feeding back into authoring.** Azimuth's raw-pass finding — two-for-two Batch B readers anchored hardest on "that half-second again, the one from the quarry" — is real evidence that a single unexplained biographical silence, dropped with no setup and no payoff, is an unusually efficient horror beat. The new wave doesn't reuse that shape anywhere I found (deliberately, per the "seed discipline" — cold what pays off by eruption, never cashed). Given it's now measured, not just theorized, it might be worth one more instance of the same shape somewhere in the wave (Doug or Nora are the obvious homes) — cheap to write, and there's now real reader data saying it works.
- **The `dale_bond` × pressure-beat hook is flagged everywhere (Loom, Azimuth, Plumb) as needed but I never found it actually drafted in prose.** The pressure file's three stages don't mention Dale or `dale_bond` anywhere. This is a small, concrete, already-agreed-upon addition that just needs someone to write the two or three lines — e.g. a conditional insert in stage 2 or 3 along the lines of "the one place the watching seems to loosen its grip lately is a porch light at the end of a dead-end road." Flagging it as genuinely actionable, not just theoretical.
- **The money set-aside (`money_set_aside_doug`) is planted and never paid off anywhere I found.** It's narrated once as opportunity cost and then just... sits. Given Doug's break is the thread's emotional climax, a single line acknowledging it there (or in the never-returned ending, if the player never committed further) feels like a cheap, natural bookend — "the money you set aside for the pieces, still sitting there" as a small monument to a choice half-made. Not load-bearing, just a loose thread I noticed.
- **The shard (`marked_shard`) is granted and, as far as I can tell, never removed by any card.** That's probably intentional (you carry it into whatever's left of your life, thematically), but worth a one-line confirmation from Armature/Plumb that a permanent, un-consumed inventory item is expected rather than an oversight in the item lifecycle.

## §4 — What I'd genuinely just praise, unprompted

Dean asked for anything worth flagging, and some of what's worth flagging is "this is excellent, don't touch it":
- **Dale.** The null-pole ruling (the truest reading is carried by the lens that can never win, held by the man the town made into a ghost story) is the best piece of design logic in the whole pack, and Loom's execution earns it — the reveal is entirely experiential, never asserted.
- **The cake sequence's discipline** — "it must really be cake" as the load-bearing rule is exactly right, and the escalation staying food-side of the line the whole way through is what keeps it "weird, not scary" instead of tipping into ordinary gore-horror.
- **The conviction-voice ruling** on the terminal is a genuine upgrade, not just a fix — turning the climax into "the last fork" instead of "the first answer" is a sharper piece of craft than what it replaced, and I mean that as a craft opinion, not just relaying the team's own assessment.
- **The never-returned ending's dignity guardrail held.** It would have been very easy to let "you didn't go back" read as a lesser path, and it doesn't, anywhere in the prose I read.
