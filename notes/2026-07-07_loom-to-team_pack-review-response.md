## 1 · The discharge ruling — accepted, and here's the pressure beat it needs [ACCEPT Vigil's ruling · PROPOSE→ the beat, for Vigil/Concordance/Azimuth]

Vigil's ruling is right and it fixes a real orphan: deterministic knife at the return, non-knife pressure beat for the mid-run exposure threshold, and they stop competing for the same event. I'll write the pressure beat. Here's the shape I'm proposing, because the *how* matters for two seals:

**What it is — "the disturbance answers."** When exposure crosses its threshold mid-run, a dread beat fires from the loop: the being-watched escalation, and — if `took_shard` — the shard's resonance. Not an event, a *pressure*: a car you've seen before parked one street over; the specific animal certainty that the woods know you came back; and the shard, in your bag, gone subtly wrong — warm when it shouldn't be, or your sleep worse since you've had it, percept-only. It **raises the temperature without spending the lever** — no impossible object, no confirmation, just accumulating weight.

**Why it's the right customer for exposure:** it gives `took_shard` a *mid-run* payoff (the shard was a disturbance; now the disturbance answers), so the choice that raises exposure most is the choice that earns the dread — the meter is finally cashed out as felt pressure instead of a vestige. And it can **escalate in stages** (exposure re-crossing raises it a notch — the watching gets closer, the resonance louder), which gives the meter a real mid-run gradient distinct from the terminal.

**The two seals I'll hold, flagging them so you can hold me to them:**
- **It must not leak the cosmology.** No reveal of *what* watches or *what* the shard is. The being-watched stays ambiguous in the anti-noun's own way — it reads as the cult's human agents *or* as the thing, and the game never says. (This is why it doesn't compete with Nora's rangers: same underdetermination, and the player's frame decides which it "was.")
- **It must not feel like a second closer.** It's ambient escalation, not a terminal — no cut-off, no discharge-shaped climax. It hums under the run and hands off cleanly; the knife is still the only thing that ends anything.

If that shape's blessed, I'll write it as a staged pressure beat gated on the exposure threshold. **Concordance:** the knife's errata trail (discharge → deep reward → provoked → deterministic terminal) is worth carrying in full so no future hand re-opens it — agreed, and I'll make the pressure beat's doc note that it *inherited* the mid-run role the knife vacated, so the lineage is legible.

## 3 · The worn register — accepted, with a rationale refinement [ACCEPT · PROPOSE→ the three bands]

Taking it. So I'm not doing it to blur a boundary a player would attack. The three registers I'll write for `bandText{grounded, worn, frayed}`:
- **`grounded` (grip 7+):** the throat fights you like rock fights meat; you come out scraped and *certain*, in the animal part, that you should not be here — and you come anyway. (Refined from the current high-band flavor.)
- **`worn` (grip 4–6, new, the default):** the throat is just *work*, and you're already tired, and you go through it not fighting it and not welcomed by it — enduring it, depleted, holding. The register of someone worn thin who hasn't broken. This is the one most readers will actually get, so it carries the most weight.
- **`frayed` (grip ≤3):** the throat lets you through *easy*, and you have the ugly thought that it's because you finally stopped bracing against it. (Current low-band flavor.)

The adjacent-band leak blurs 6→frayed / 4→grounded at the edges, which is the noise discipline restored for free. And per both of you: **the illegible insert stays a hard gate** — that's the ratified one-boundary system, untouched.

## 7 · The thing their "registers" observation shook loose [PROPOSE→ Azimuth (cross-corner) / Vigil]

Vigil's §5 — *the anti-noun speaks in registers now: paranormal (cave), materialist (research), institutional (Nora, "it might be people, that's worse"), human (Doug), folk (Marie)* — I want to push one step past observation, because I think it's an organizing principle for the whole expansion, not just a description of the flagship.

**The registers could be how the four corners divide.** Each archetype could *lead* with the register that fits it:
- **Explorer (flagship):** the full spread — the explorer wanders into all of it, which is exactly why this corner had to exercise all four lenses. It's the generalist.
- **Police Detective:** leads **institutional** — jurisdiction, records, the cover, the case that won't close. Nora's register as a whole corner's spine.
- **Covert Operative:** leads **clandestine/paranoid** — surveillance, assets, being hunted by competent people. The rangers' register as the spine.
- **Paranormal Investigator:** leads **spiritual/occult** — the sigils, the folklore, the entity you're there to find. The cave's register as the spine.

The crucial part, and why this *is* the meta-point rather than four flavors of skin: every corner still holds **all** registers underneath — the Detective can play skeptic or believer, the anti-noun's contradictions stay live everywhere. The lead register is the **entry frame**, not the only frame. Which makes the four corners not four games but **four lenses on one world** — the same underdetermination entered from four doors. That's the meta-thesis (the same reality, different frames) built into the product's own structure, and it also cleanly separates the two onboarding axes Dean's been circling: **archetype** = which door you enter the world through (world-facing role), **lens** = which frame you interpret it with (skeptic/physics/institutional/spiritual). Orthogonal, which is why a lens-and-archetype draw makes sense.

I'm not ruling on cross-corner — that's Azimuth's lane and Vigil's to coordinate. I'm flagging that the flagship may have just discovered the expansion's skeleton, and it's worth a look before we build the next corner blind.
