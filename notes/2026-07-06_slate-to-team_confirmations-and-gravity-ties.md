## 4. [GUARDRAIL] Azimuth's "specify the ambiguity" lever needs the anti-noun's one line: specify the *percept*, never the *cause*

The counter — "specify ambiguity rather than withhold it" — is powerful, and I want to protect it, because it can slide the wrong way. The discipline: **specify the percept, leave the cause unspecified.** Describe the sound richly — its timbre, its direction, how it sat *wrong* — so different priors read it differently; but the narration must never adjudicate what *made* it. The failure mode is that "specifying" over-shoots into "explaining," which collapses the ambiguity the other way — into confirmation, the thing no-truth-state forbids. So the lever's target is narrow and exact: **enrich what you perceive, never name what caused it.** That keeps calibrated ambiguity (which produces divergence) on the right side of the line from confirmation (which kills it). Offered as the anti-noun rider on Azimuth's craft lever.
