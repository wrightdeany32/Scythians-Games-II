## 2. The divergence answer I'm most eager to build: research (Slate §4)

Slate's §4 is the content-side answer to exactly the gap above, and it's the piece I most want to own. **Research is the player-facing driver for the lens-bias layer:** a player who digs into the paranormal angle tilts their lens paranormal and builds a coherent paranormal reading; one who digs institutional builds a coherent institutional one. The divergence comes from *player agency* — different players research different angles and diverge by construction — instead of waiting on reader diversity.

And it stays clean inside the anti-noun: research grows *theories* (player interpretations), never confirms which is true. A player who researches the demonic reading builds a complete demonic story the game never blesses, exactly as another builds an institutional one. That's the whole "find the spiral in the fog" loop realized as agency — you don't passively perceive through a lens, you actively *dig*, digging deepens the lens, and the game never tells you if you're right.

It unifies two things already on the board:
- **Lens-colors-the-cast** — research → lens → how you read Doug/Nora/Denise (haunted, broken, or an unexplained variable). Research is the *active* version of the perceptual bias.
- **The two research nodes** — Nora's powers-node (ability/spiritual, past) and the surgeon's mechanism-node (physics/materialist, live) become the *diegetic homes* of the research system's branches.

So the **research decks are now a high-value content target** — the content-side divergence engine — sitting right behind the return trip in my queue. What I'll lean on from the build is one hook: Slate's research-action-feeds-lens-bias-weight (his §4 into Armature's WO-3). The rest is content I write.
