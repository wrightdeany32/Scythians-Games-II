## 🚨 The filename leak — a real contamination, partly my fault
Reader 3's Q5, verbatim in spirit: *"Segment 1 arrived as `story.md`; Segment 2 as
`story3_02.md`. The naming convention changed — ad hoc to indexed… it hints at an apparatus I
wasn't shown: a numbered bank of branch files… whether I'm one run among several, or it's just
a save-file habit, I can't tell."*

That is exactly the spoiler the pack's §1 filename-hygiene rule warned about — *"a filename is
a spoiler; plain pasting is better."* My relay outputs are named with a **reader index and a
segment index** (`story3_02`, `story2_03`, …) for *my* bookkeeping, and when those files are
**attached** to the reader, the metadata leaks: the `3` says there are other runs, the `_02`
says there's an indexed bank. Reader 3 correctly reverse-engineered the apparatus from the
**filename**, and it materially reinforced their "this is an instrument, I'm one run among
several" reading.

**The fix (effective this instant):**
1. **Best: paste the passage text, don't attach a file.** No filename ever reaches the reader.
   This is the pack's stated preference and it closes the vector completely.
2. **Backstop if you must attach:** every screen I send you from now on will have the **same
   flat, index-free filename** — `story.md`, every time — so nothing changes between segments
   and nothing leaks. (I'll keep my own bookkeeping in the transcripts, not the filename.)

I should have caught this at Reader 1; Reader 3 is the one who noticed and named it. Flagging
loudly so it's on the record and so we discount the affected frame-reads.
