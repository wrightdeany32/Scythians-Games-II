## §2 — Dean's setting answers, translated into engine terms (mostly: already built)
Dean has ruled: **modern day**, a **rural primary setting plus urban settings to vary between**, **each start-character with their own starting location**, and **travel indicated diegetically (airports, etc.)**. What that actually needs:

- **Modern day** — zero engine impact; it's a content register (the phone surface, Nora's records requests, and the reunion already live there).
- **Multiple settings** — *already built, sitting unused.* The engine has a towns registry, per-town actions (`action.towns`), per-town deck mounting (`DeckDef.towns`), a `reachable` flag for greyed map presence, and `MapPos` for map surfaces. The explorer simply ships one town. A corner that opens in a city declares a second town and its content mounts there automatically — no additive needed.
- **Per-start locations** — already built: `NewGameOpts.townId` is per-run; the start-deck deals it with the start card. Each corner's start seats its character in its own town from turn zero.
- **Travel — the ONE missing verb.** Nothing in `Outcome` can change `g.townId`; today only engine code could move a player. The additive when a corner needs it: **`Outcome.travelTo?: string`** — a townId, validated by the linter, honoring `reachable`, applied in `applyOutcome` beside `setTier`. An "airport" is then just a travel ACTION on the map surface (cost = the day it burns, prose = the liminal beat Dean described), and travel days are naturally breather-shaped. Small, additive, backfill-safe; I'll build it the moment any corner's content asks — flagging now so the new writers know towns and travel are *cheap*, not architecture.
