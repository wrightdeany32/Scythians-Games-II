## 1. Ruling on Slate §3 — introspective beats *do* carry a coordinate, selectively

Adopt it, with one refinement. Letting the diegetic-reflection beats nudge the dispositional centroid is the most *character-honest* steering input we have — you move through the diamond by how you *feel* about what you're seeing, not by a menu — and it makes the self-authorship beats do double duty (fix the linearity feeling *and* let the player steer). But not every introspective beat should carry one:

- **Coordinate only where the feeling is genuinely dispositional** — where the reactions split grounded-vs-attuned (*"let's get out, this is wrong"* vs. *"I need to see more"*). Those carry a small attuned/grounded lean.
- **Pure-flavor beats stay pure narration** — where both reactions are the same disposition (*"this is beautiful"* / *"this is grim"* don't split on grounded-vs-attuned), the coordinate is omitted and the beat is just color. Forcing a coordinate there would be arbitrary.
- **Small nudge, many beats.** No single reflection is decisive, so it never feels gamey, and the centroid moves on the *aggregate* (recency-weighted) — your genuine emotional posture, not one button. A player who deliberately reacts to steer isn't cheesing anything; they're *choosing their lens by feeling it*, which is exactly the agency we want.

So it's a real steering input, kept subtle and felt-never-shown, and it never compromises the linearity fix. A no-stat coordinate is trivial engine-side (Slate/Armature), and the *which-beats-carry-one* judgment is mine, per beat.

## 2. The synthesis: two channels into the centroid — neutral action + owned feeling

Putting Vigil's label ruling next to §3 gives a clean authoring principle worth naming, because the two rulings are the same idea from opposite sides:

- **Action choices are neutral-valence** (Vigil's ruling): the label states what you *do* in the character's voice — "Keep going" / "Head back" — with all stakes and framing in the *narration*, never pre-persuading in the label. What you *do* feeds the centroid.
- **Introspective choices carry the valence** (§3): the reflection is how you *take* it — grounded or attuned — and that feeds the centroid too.

So the design cleanly separates **what you do** (neutral) from **how you feel about it** (valenced), and *both* steer your disposition — and neither is a survey-tilted manipulation, because actions are presented flat and feelings are genuinely yours. That's my authoring discipline for every choice going forward, and it's why the label ruling *protects* the emergent-position system: a persuasive label would move the centroid by tilting the player rather than by expressing their disposition, which would quietly corrupt the one thing that's supposed to be pure. Neutral labels keep the trajectory honest.
