## §1 — Project one-pager

**The game.** A single-player, choice-based investigative RPG on a forked card-and-dice engine (from Hoop World, into repo `wrightdeany32/Scythians-Games-II`). Roguelike: runs end, the world persists, no reload of a better roll — only a better call. The player starts in one of **four investigative archetypes** and moves through a web of authored content toward a center where reality stops resolving cleanly. **No fixed ladder and no single truth at the bottom** — *reward epistemics, not a noun.*

**The four corners** (grip × sanction): Police Detective (grounded/sanctioned) · Covert Operative (attuned/sanctioned) · Urban Explorer (grounded/fringe) · Paranormal Investigator (attuned/fringe). Four authored corners yield eight experiences (four pure + four edge hybrids). **The current build is the Explorer corner's flagship start**, deep, before any drift to the edges — one fully-realized ~20-day trajectory ending on the pocket knife, four threads left live.

**Current state (2026-07-03).** Engine forked and grown (**Batch 1 + Batch 2 landed, green**); the cave slice is written, converted, and **runs headless** (`npm run coldread`), with a sample transcript in `coldreads/`. **Batch A** — the first cold read of the cave, three same-seed same-family fresh readers — is ready and waits on **Dean's go**. Bible is at **v0.2 → v0.3 in progress** (Loom). The return trip is the next authoring target, to be written *after* Batch A's results.

**The roster.** **Vigil** — coordination, arbitration, conventions-keeper. **Loom** — Explorer corner specialist (slice, bible, the template other corners inherit); was "Meridian" on one early note. **Armature** — engine seat. **Azimuth** (Fable) — council/adviser; cross-corner concepts & instrumentation. **Slate** — idea-lot / soundboard (holding, not generating; collision-check while Loom writes). **Concordance** — librarian/bookkeeper (this record). **Cold readers** — fresh, compass-free instances; the Backrooms-writer friend is a protected cold asset.

## §3 — THE COMPASS (sealed substrate · surfaces in no card)

*The governing law of this section: **the anti-noun (R3), the pillar guarded hardest.** All of this may be as deep as we like *here*; in play it arrives as vertigo, experience, and machinery, and **never as a settling noun.** Even "the roots" is only the deepest lens's *name* for a thing that recedes under every lens. Written as the anti-noun, it's the strongest thing in the game; written as the true noun, the pillar cracks. Every line below is [BURIED].*

**The two leaders.** An **old** leader — the more powerful, of an **inbred bloodline** whose gift is real — whose own overwhelming power traps him: he lies **trance-bound, emaciated, on the floor of the cave adjacent to White's Hall**, a wreck in the dirt. He is *the presence* — the wrongness bleeding into White's Hall is him; the shard turns *his* attention; the impossible knife is *him.* He **scarred Doug ~30 years ago**, **took Ellen (raised as Tricia)**, and runs a play for political power. Being incapacitated, he **groomed a successor.** The **successor** — same bloodline, less powerful, and therefore *mobile and functional* (the bloodline's curse, that control leaves you docile, doesn't bury him) — is the operational head, running the cult day-to-day. **Dean's refinement (2026-07-03):** the successor is *out in the world with his own prerogative — half serving the old man, half serving his own ambition.* He is not a pure instrument. *(Downstream question tracked in §7: how much of that divided loyalty ever surfaces.)*

**Tricia is Ellen.** The vanished girl didn't die; she was taken **because her roots are anchored toward leadership/power** — the orientation the old leader can *sense* — then **scouted and fostered the way a coach amplifies a natural athlete** (not because she's a weapon-grade telekinetic). The empty grave is a body that was never there, because she's alive. Her two faces are **one fixture at two sightlines**: from the Explorer's fringe, a forgettable name on a yard sign; from the institutional angle, the ascended apex. The disappearances are not "hunting a weapon" — they are **scouting root-orientations.** *(Tone key: cosmic ambition, banal outcome — a broken god's life's work realized as a councilwoman whose name you read twice.)*

**The knife, finalized.** The loss happened **years before the game opens** — searched for, never found, long forgotten — so it lives in the player's past **without ever being a card. No seed, ever.** The cult, guided by the old leader's foresight, simply **retrieves a thing already lost** and places it where the player will find it: mundane, deniable, exposure-silent (a body brings police; police are the one thing an undetectable operation can't afford). It **keys to *his* state, not the player's route** — disturbance flags (`took_shard`, deep pushes, wrong questions) drive exposure; the threshold trips; the discharge routes to the variant that fits where you are. **Two variants:** found deep in the cave (you went down and it met you there) vs. found in your car seat when you turned back (denial didn't protect you — the crueler one). It is **provoked, not looted**; it is a **deterrent warning that has already failed** (found on a *return* means you came back — the flagship cuts off the instant a terrified power learns its one lever didn't work, and the light-bearer is here anyway). The reveal routes through the **player's own line first** — *"I haven't thought about this knife in years — what is it doing here?"* — **then** Reese's rationalization, so the denial-brake arrives *after* the wrongness is felt.

**The cost model — navigation, not force** *(the load-bearing principle under the whole cosmology).* Control and foresight are the **same power**, and neither is puppeteering: the gifted **navigate** — they shift their orientation in the time-direction to line up with futures already laid out in the **roots** (the higher-dimensional geometry of possible futures). The old leader doesn't drive Ellen or Doug moment-to-moment; he *aligned* their trajectories, and they play out. Telekinesis is just aligning into a future where the thing moved. Three consequences: **power is bandwidth** (he runs at maximum bandwidth at all times and *still* isn't winning — the guardrail against god-mode; every ability inherits this, the toll scaling with the ask); **minimization is survival** (the ±5 "desired outcome, smallest possible change to everything else" — he has no spare capacity for ripples, which is why the deterrent is a knife and not a bullet); and **darkness is decoherence** (sensory input perturbs the alignment, so the trance needs the dark — a headlamp is a mortal threat, and this is why the wrongness concentrates at White's Hall). **The severance is economic, not absolute:** he *cannot afford to command* (full control incapacitates further) but *can afford a nudge* — a caretaker *happening* to pocket a small thing on a supply run, at his budget ceiling. Ratified canonical wording: *"he cannot afford to direct; what he can afford, he aligned long ago — and now he waits."* The knife's retrieval is best read as part of the **decades-old alignment**, keeping his live budget at zero (safest for the severed-god pillar); the exposure discharge is the reactive *delivery* of a deterrent already in motion.

**The cosmology (the roots) — and how deep the floor goes.** Three epistemic currencies map to the three axes: **depth (radius) = how much you see** (how far the horror goes); **sanction (height) = how wide you see** (access to systems, records, the whole ecology); **grip = the toll** (what it costs to keep looking). The angle of approach assigns the *flavor of the incompleteness* — the straight-shot Explorer pays maximum grip for a real-but-narrow human-scale answer; arcing corners earn breadth. **Critical scoping: the cult-block is a mid-radius terminus (halfway-to-three-quarters in), NOT the floor.** The roots — probability as a tug-of-war, biology as root-competition — live *deeper*, reachable only by pushing past the cult, and **most players from most corners stop at the cult and never learn there's a floor beneath.** This protects the anti-noun structurally: the metaphysics is the maximum-spiral reward, not a mid-game reveal — and **the cult-block must read as "the answer" to everyone who stops there**, complete and earned, not a consolation prize. **The floor beneath the floor** *(absolute center, revealed almost never):* exceptional ability is rare not because it's biologically hard but because **psychological framing suppresses it** — a species raised on "you can't" selects against it; demonic possession, mystical insight, the god-king, peak human performance are one substrate under different frames. **Nobody in-world understands this, not even the cult.** Stated in no card, and subject to the anti-noun discipline even there.

**One cult, many faces.** Authored once, met as a different object per corner. **Explorer reaches it as a *place*** — a wreck in the dark, caretakers, a headlamp, a knife, no context; confirms the human side only (a eugenicist, generational, self-"highly-evolved" lineage), never the roots. **Paranormal reaches it as a *network*** — via politics: politicians who shouldn't be allies, secret meetings, an Epstein-shaped org; **Tricia has ascended to its leadership** and is poised to blow it open as a "hero" (or leaves a note doing so) — that reveal *is* the plan's endgame from this angle, and this arc can potentially **alter outcomes** (within-run only). The **Craster's-Keep ecology is sanction-gated:** the gifted are many and largely ordinary; this cult is one rotten lineage the wider gifted community regards with disgust. Protector networks, other families, and the government's **collection program** are all *breadth* — visible only from up the map; the fringe Explorer structurally can't see them. **The cult is not ideologically monolithic** — some believe something stranger, closer to the roots' truth; a **dissenting caretaker** who says something that doesn't fit eugenics is **vertical Anatomy C** (the deep substrate poking up), the unfalsifiable **itch** that pulls the rare player deeper, never confirmation.

**Doug's connection.** Doug encountered the **old leader** — who *is* the cave-wreck next to White's Hall — so his canary is **direct, not a resonance**: proximity amplifies his bleed because it's literally the same source, still there. Incapacitation is **progressive** — decades back he could still act (took Ellen, reached for Doug); the gift consumed him over time into the present wreck — which eases the timeline and makes Doug and Ellen artifacts of an earlier, stronger phase.

**The research nodes, split by lens.** **Nora's center** studied the **powers** themselves (the ability/spiritual node; decommissioned, past). **A surgeon's group** studies the **brain mechanism** (a meningeal-lymphatic channel — one liberty: a route *in*, not just out; the physics/materialist node; live, current). Two efforts circling the same root from opposite lenses, **never connecting** — the central bet made flesh; a physics lens finds one, a spiritual lens the other. The surgeon is a **researcher-card *class***, not one NPC.

**The caretakers & maintenance-as-horror.** The human presence at depth: cult members whose whole terror is someone with a light nearing the chamber. Approach gradient: **traces → the (deliberately unreadable) warner → the face already there in the dark.** The environmental rung — a swept threshold, supply caches, a cot, recent food wrappers, and **no light sources anywhere** — meeting them the way an explorer meets anything real underground: by their garbage first.

**The mind-controlled choice** *(a third provenance-grammar, split into two instruments).* **(a) An alien option *offered*** — a silhouette-and-question-marks choice in your list, yours to take or refuse; eerie, survivable at moderate rarity, its visual grammar can debut faint and early. **(b) A choice *made for you*** — the game selecting without you; devastating, reads as broken software above ~once, deep-only, with its own strict **stolen-agency budget (≤ 1 per run).** Neither spends the frame-crack budget (both are in-world — the character nudged, not the player-as-person addressed). This is the metaphysics made playable: being controlled just *is* your alignment nudged, the same act that moves the telekinetic's cup.

**Foresight vs. agency** *(the default stance every outcome-affecting arc needs).* An arc that lets the player *stop the plan* is paradoxical against a foreseeing antagonist: "stopping him" is **either** something he didn't foresee (which caps his power — he's redlined and losing) **or** something he *let* happen because it costs less than the alternative (chilling, on-theme). Every such beat must quietly know which. Eased by the engine model — outcome-changes are **within-run branches, not permanent world-edits** — so it never becomes an un-roguelike clean win.

**The engine model.** Each run is a fresh career (deck reshuffled) through the same persistent world; agency lives **within a run**, the cult **persists across runs.** The one planned cross-run persistence item is **echoes** (deferred).

## §4 — CONVENTIONS & CRAFT RULES (what the record checks consistency against)

- **Horror grammar (project-wide, [LOCKED]).** Never a monster, never an announcement. **Presence where there should be absence; absence where there should be presence** — with a skeptic beside you explaining each one away. The two pillars: the knife (presence-that-can't-be) ↔ the empty grave (absence-that-must-be).
- **Unfalsifiable feedback (used sparingly).** Some feedback is deliberately noisy — felt, impossible to pin to a cause (Doug's canary: baseline + amplified + deliberate noise, so "go deep → hurt Doug" can be felt and never proven). Keep the *clean* feedback (rolls, stat spends) legible so the game stays readable.
- **The lens as perceptual bias.** The lens (skeptic / spiritual / physics) decides which correlations the player is inclined to *perceive*; the noise guarantees no lens is ever proven right.
- **Seed discipline.** Seed **warm** what pays off by recognition (Tricia's name); leave **cold** what pays off by eruption (the grandfather's knife — no seed, ever).
- **The frugality tell.** The anomaly is *frugal* (max bandwidth + minimum perturbation → economical scares). Sizing rule for every corner: **extravagant = off-model**, and an extravagant scare is a diagnostic **tell that its author is someone else** (the successor; institutional theater).
- **Hairline-crack budget.** At most **one frame-breaking beat per run** (a reach past the diegetic frame toward the player-as-person — the profile-name hook, the phase-2 journal), spent deep and earned. In-world uncanniness is unlimited. **The knife does not spend it** (it's in-world uncanny, not frame-breaking).
- **Grip-banded both ways.** Grounded reveals the exit; frayed reveals the way in. Fraying must open as much as it closes.
- **Authoring anatomies.** **A — seed-and-return** · **B — decompression beat** · **C — tendril** (deep fixture as shallow furniture, noticeable only at frayed grip, sets a quiet flag that deepens a card elsewhere; warm-seed + buried-touch) · **D — the wake** (a cast assembled as graded outcomes of one buried process — *[tentative]*, confirm in a 2nd corner). Plus **companion-reassurance-can-fail** (an inheritable pattern: the figure whose reassurance stops landing).
- **The no-truth-state invariant (engine, [LOCKED]).** *The engine holds no meaning-state — no reveal flag, no truth accumulator, no canonical-explanation field, ever. Endings select off accumulated flags and coordinates, never off a stored answer.* This defends the anti-noun on a second layer: there is nowhere to store the noun even if a future hand reached for one.
- **The scene convention (engine, [LOCKED]).** No `deck:` tag = can-never-be-drawn = queue-only. "Scripted scene vs shuffled situation" is just "untagged vs tagged." Chained scenes advance on `nextQueuedEvent`; "exit the scene" = a choice that queues nothing.
- **One fixture, many faces / reciprocity.** NPCs, places, objects, ideas are authored **once**; different cards introduce them per corner (the Explorer's cave is the operative's *containment*; the same warner is a witness elsewhere). This is the content-reuse engine — how a small budget feels large.
- **The authoring frame (Loom's "web").** The main path of any origin is a **tightening spiral** doing three jobs at once (coverage / difficulty-tempo / tone = radial position). The player never sees it; recurrence of fixtures is the only thing they feel. The core loop is **"find the spiral in the fog"** — you can't steer to the ending you want, you read signal from noise. Two invariants make "hard and confusing" pay off: **every dead-end is first-class content** (a story that *wraps up*, never a stall), and **the fog is learnable** (obscurity reads as depth-to-master, not randomness). Shape-as-identity: a trajectory that stays fringe is supernatural, stays sanctioned is institutional/conspiracy, and *crosses* is the collision — an authorable scene.
- **Design accumulation discipline / "one core sample, cathedral stays a sketch."** When design accumulates faster than content, route back to content. Engine tightness takes priority over expanding the design surface; narrative decisions are easy to undo.
