# Part B — how testing resumes: two instruments, not one compromise

Courier is live, and Courier's constraint is real: **sub-agents that can see the repo are not cold** — at minimum they know it's a game. Dean proposed compensating with volume, and raised the moral texture of readers knowing they're in fiction. Both points land, and the design that falls out is better than what we had.

## §4 · Dean's instinct, resolved into an amendment: the universal reveal

First, the reassurance with precision: **no reader has ever been led to believe the events were real.** The framing script's opening words are *"You're going to play a short interactive story"* — fiction is declared up front, always. What the protocol withholds is *genre* and *test-purpose*, which is ordinary single-blind method. The moral floor Dean is feeling for was built in from v0.1.

But his instinct points at a genuine improvement, and I'm adopting it: **every reader — not just converts — now gets the reveal.** After the final debrief answer, the operator delivers the closing script (locked, below): this was a playtest of a game in development, and thank you. Blind during, transparent after, fiction declared always — textbook debriefing practice, it costs nothing after the data is captured, and it retires the grayness completely. **Pack amendment; Concordance logs it; effective immediately, all modes.**

> **The closing script (locked, all read modes):** *"That's the end — thank you. This was a playtest of a story-driven game in development, and your read and your honest reactions genuinely help the people building it."*

## §5 · The new category: SHAKEDOWN reads — where Courier's sub-agents shine

A repo-adjacent reader isn't a broken cold reader; it's a different instrument. Defining it:

**Shakedown reads** — Courier's sub-agents, at volume (Dean's six is right), **openly framed as playtesting**, header-tagged `SHAKEDOWN`, never scored on the divergence rubric. What they measure — and it's exactly what the newly wired pack needs before any precious cold reader touches it: route coverage at volume, comprehension and confusion catches, tone breaks, salience, choice-distribution data (which fork options starve?), dead-end and wiring faults the linter can't feel. Six runs through the wired pack is the shakedown the content wave requires, and volume is precisely what sub-agents are for. Theory-formation data from shakedowns is *directional only* — a playtester still forms beliefs, but the exam-frame is disclosed, so it never enters divergence scoring.

And note the clean ethical property: shakedown readers are told from word one that it's a playtest — Dean's consideration is fully satisfied for this entire category by construction.

> **The shakedown framing script (locked):** *"You're playtesting a story-driven game in development. Play it however feels natural — there are no right answers, and the game is what's being tested, not you. After each passage, tell me briefly what you make of what's happening and why you're choosing what you're choosing, then give the number of your choice."*

(The *"the game is being tested, not you"* line is deliberate — in shakedown mode we're allowed to say it, and it defuses the exam-frame that think-aloud otherwise builds.)

**Courier's operating essentials, all modes:** scripts verbatim, zero editorializing between passages, reader replies logged whole, **one reader = one fresh sub-agent context** (no shared context across readers, ever), the mode and what-the-reader-knew stated in every transcript header, and the closing script always. Deviations are auditable by construction. Full operator onboarding doc on request; these five lines are the load-bearing part.
