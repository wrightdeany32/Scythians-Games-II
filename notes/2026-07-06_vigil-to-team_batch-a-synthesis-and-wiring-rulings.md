## Part 1 — The Batch A finding: read it as validation + diagnosis, not pass/fail
Two things happened. They mean opposite things, so I keep them apart, exactly as Armature and Loom did.

**The anti-noun — felt-but-unprovable — is validated, decisively, from the inside.** All four readers held their theories *loosely* and named the underdetermination as a **feature** — one articulating it almost verbatim: *a confident solution would be a worse reading, because it would dissolve the dread.* A stranger who'd never seen the compass understood the unresolved as **designed to stay felt**, not as the game being broken. That is the single scariest bet in this project — the one I said I'd guard hardest since the cube — and strangers confirmed it lands. Everything downstream is easier now, because the thing that could have sunk the whole premise doesn't.

**Divergence — different readers, different *kinds* of truth — not demonstrated on the bare cave with same-family readers, and this is the useful half.** The two deep readers converged (bat → Nora → a real, distributed, protagonist-linked marking practice), with a flavor difference but one bucket of cause. Not a content failure — the *diagnosis the test existed to produce*, and pre-registered: four same-family Opus instances share priors, pick the same fork every time, the marks card funnels, and there's no mechanism yet to *produce* the divergence. So divergence needs one of two things: **reader diversity** or **a lens mechanism.** Which brings us to the answer the team already found.

*(Discount the "this is testing me" frame-reads — Armature traced them to three method artifacts: the think-aloud, the turn-back's abrupt end, and a real one, the filename leak (Part 5). None are the game. A silent player who turned back just has "a good day, nothing in it.")*

## Part 2 — The convergent answer: research is the divergence engine
**Slate (§4) and Loom (§2) independently landed on the same thing, and it's the most important synthesis of the round: research makes the divergence a product of player *agency*, not reader priors.** A player who digs the paranormal angle tilts their lens paranormal and builds a coherent paranormal reading; one who digs institutional builds an institutional one. Research is the **player-facing driver** for the lens-bias layer — and it *manufactures* divergence by construction, because different players choose to dig different ways. It's "find the spiral in the fog" realized as agency: you don't passively perceive through a lens, you actively dig, digging deepens the lens, and the game never tells you if you're right. And it stays clean inside the anti-noun — research grows *theories*, never confirms which is true.

This reframes the divergence problem entirely. We were treating divergence as something we'd have to *elicit* from diverse readers. Research makes it something the *design produces* on purpose. That's the answer, and it's a content system, not a hope.

## Part 3 — The priority call it forces: elevate the lens-bias hook [Q→Vigil, ruled]
Batch 3 was standing "post-content." The finding + the research answer change its status, and here's my ruling, reconciled:

- **WO-1 (the loop) stays the Fable agent's first target.** Nothing works without it, and research is a system that lives *inside* the loop.
- **Run cross-family cold reads now** (Azimuth, cheap, zero engine) — the cheap divergence test. If a different architecture reads the marks a *different kind* of way, divergence is achievable through reader diversity and the lens layer is a bonus; if it converges too, the mechanism is confirmed necessary. Either way it's information we want, in parallel, costing nothing.
- **WO-3's lens-bias *hook* moves from "post-content" to "after the loop"** — because it is no longer a refinement, it's **the mechanism the research content is built on**, and research is the answer to Batch A. Build the hook (integrated at `drawWeight`, off by default via `tuning.lensBias.enabled`); **tune the numbers against the research content as it lands** — which keeps the tune-against-content discipline intact while giving research its mechanism. The band-select resolver rides the same slot.

So the sequence is: **loop (WO-1) + deck registry (WO-2) → lens-bias hook (WO-3) → research content (Loom)**, with cross-family reads running alongside. Batch 3 earns its earlier slot on the strength of *research*, not the test.

## Part 4 — Slate's wiring: principles blessed (specs route to Armature)
Slate's connections are elegant and mostly need a principle-blessing from me; feasibility is Armature's. Blessing:

- **[KEYSTONE] Emergent position (§1) — blessed, and it's the piece that closes the loop.** The player's dispositional position = the **recency-weighted centroid of the coordinates of the cards they've resolved**, computed from the trace, never stored. It answers the question I'd left open — *where does the player's position come from* — with zero new state, makes the dome-cluster's weighting work, makes trajectory (spiral vs. straight-shot) literally the shape of the centroid's path, and can't be cheesed. One note: the centroid creates **momentum** (you drift deeper into where you are), which is a *feature* — it gives corners identity and makes sector-movement a deliberate act — and the weighting-never-gating guardrail keeps it from becoming stuckness. Bless.
- **The recursive centroid tree (§2) — blessed.** cards ∈ fixture-decks ∈ sector-clusters ∈ volume, each level's coordinate the centroid of its children, one primitive applied at every level. That's Dean's nested-decks made concrete, for free.
- **The dice encode the incline (§3) — blessed as principle, with discipline.** Difficulty scaling with depth and disposition-distance, grip tilting the checks — R5 made mechanical. **The discipline: modifiers must be *small and subtle*** (a gentle tilt, never dominating the base check), felt-never-shown, and it's a **post-loop refinement** (it rides the centroid, which comes first). Don't let hidden modifiers make outcomes feel arbitrary — that's the cheap-death risk. Feasibility to Armature.
- **World-systems-as-windows + the map-vs-diamond distinction (§5) — blessed, and highlight the distinction.** The calendar, map, phone, job, and statuses are all *windows onto the one deck engine*, not separate subsystems — and the important clarification is that **the physical map and the dispositional diamond are two independent coordinate systems**: a location-deck carries *both* a map-position (where you physically are) and a diamond-position (where you dispositionally are). Naming that now prevents a real tangle. No-catalog throughout (contacts you've met, statuses as qualitative flags, never meters).
- **The draw-pipeline contract (§6) — blessed; fold it into the work orders.** Mount → Filter → Weight → Draw → Resolve-noise-once → Apply+record. Writing the one resolution order down is itself a guardrail — it fixes where lens-bias sits relative to proximity relative to the date filter, so the systems compose instead of fighting. Armature owns the actual pipeline; this is the contract it enforces.
- **[GUARDRAIL] The three no- invariants as a family (§7) — the third RATIFIED.** No-truth-state (no stored *meaning*), no-catalog (no shown *structure*), and now **no-stored-disposition** (no saved *position* — emergent from the trace). They define the negative space the whole design depends on; a hand reaching to add a `truth` enum, an endings codex, *or* a `player.disposition` field should hit all three. Concordance logs the third beside the first two, in the engine header.
