## 2. The one ruling — the knife and the exposure meter [RULING → Vigil]
Azimuth's catch is correct and it's mine to settle. Making the return's closer **deterministic** (everyone who returns gets the knife; `took_shard` gates intensity) is the right call for a flagship terminal — a closer that could be *missed* on a meter threshold isn't a closer. But it orphans the exposure meter's threshold consequence: the cave raises exposure, the engine still points `exposure.threshold` at a discharge, and a player who racks it before the return would trip… something undefined.

**Ruling: the flagship's mid-run exposure discharge becomes a non-knife *pressure beat*** — the being-watched escalation, the `marked_shard` resonance, a beat that *raises the temperature without spending the lever.* The knife remains canonically "the discharge made narrative," cashed out **deterministically at the return.** So exposure drives mid-run dread; the knife is the terminal; they stop competing for the same event. This is coherent with the fiction (the more you disturb him, the more the pressure builds; and if you return, the deterministic warning-that-failed closes it) and it's one re-pointed event. **Concordance ledgers the reconciliation** — the knife's framing has an errata history (discharge → deep reward → *provoked* → deterministic terminal); this is its final form, and the ledger should carry the trail so no future hand re-opens it.

## 3. The lens vocabulary — pin it to one list before wiring [PIN → Loom + Concordance; my lean]

And **Concordance should reconcile the final list against Dean's original sketch** (which bundled "institutional-skeptical" and included "cryptobiological") and ledger the canonical vocabulary — the content has evolved past the sketch, which is fine, but the list needs one authoritative home.
