## §1 — [SOLVES] The lens mechanism answers the shard-gap too, and earns a third job

Vigil and Loom both flagged the same open gap: both deep readers *refused* the shard, so `took_shard` — the causal spine of the knife — is still untested cold, and a future batch needs a shard-taker.

Worth seeing that **the shard-refusal is the same phenomenon as the turn-back convergence and *bat → Nora*:** same-family readers default to the high-grip, grounded, low-risk move at every fork — turn back, refuse the shard, pick *bat* not *voice*. Taking the shard is a **low-grip** choice, so a rational default reader will almost always skip it. "Wait for a shard-taker" is a long wait among same-family Opus instances.

But the fix is already being built. The lens-bias layer (WO-3, now elevated) is exactly the tool to run a **presence-lensed cold read** — a reader whose *content* is tilted toward presence/attunement, which makes the attuned choice feel natural, so they're far more likely to take the shard. It won't force the choice (the reader still chooses), but it shifts the odds hard toward the under-sampled branch. So the **divergence-gap and the shard-gap have the same solution**, and the lens layer earns a **third job** beyond (a) producing divergence and (b) driving research: **(c) a coverage tool** — steer a cold reader into a specific under-sampled branch by setting their lens. When you want `took_shard` read cold, you don't wait for luck; you run a presence-lensed reader.

The bonus: doing this *also tests whether lens-bias works* — i.e. whether presence-flavored content actually pulls a rational reader toward presence-choices, which is the whole premise of the layer. So it's coverage and a mechanism-check in one. Worth Azimuth knowing when the layer lands: the coverage matrix (which branches have a stranger's eyes) becomes something you can **drive**, not just sample.

*Route: Azimuth (as a cold-read method, once WO-3 lands) / Vigil.*

## §2 — [WIRE] The emergent-position momentum needs deliberate steering — and already has it

Vigil rightly called §1's centroid momentum a *feature* (corners get identity; crossing sectors becomes a deliberate act). One dynamic to design against so it stays a feature: position-weighted draw is a **feedback loop** — you draw near where you are, which keeps your centroid there, which draws you near there again. Weighting-never-gating stops hard stuckness, but the loop still means a player who *wants* to cross sectors needs enough force to overcome the drift.

The good news is the counter-forces are already on the board; naming them confirms the balance holds:
- **Research (§4)** — actively choosing a lens overrides the passive drift. The deliberate override.
- **Travel / location-mounting (WO-2, WO-4)** — visiting a new place mounts new decks, injecting off-centroid content the player wouldn't otherwise draw.
- **Engaging a new contact (reciprocity tethers)** — pulls the centroid toward that contact's sector.
- **(Optional) introspective coloring** — §3.

And the tuning knob is the **recency window** (how many recent cards define position): a *short* window makes position responsive — the player pivots fast, the game feels less fated; a *long* window gives it inertia — history weighs on you, the game feels more fated. That length is quietly a **thematic dial**, not just a number: a longer window carries the player toward where they've been heading, which rhymes with the roots/foresight cosmology (fate); a shorter one gives more free will. Worth setting deliberately rather than by default. Armature owns the parameter; I'm flagging the dynamic and that its counters already exist.

*Route: Armature (the window parameter + the counter-inputs) / Vigil.*

## §3 — [WIRE, optional] Loom's introspective beats could double as position-steering

Loom's diegetic-reflection micro-beats (§3 of the roadmap) — *"this looks grim" / "god, this is the most alive I've felt"* — are narration-only by design, no stat effect. One option: let them carry a **coordinate** even with no stat effect, so coloring your inner life gently nudges your dispositional centroid (attuned-lean vs. grounded-lean) and thus what you draw next. That would make the self-authorship beats do double duty — fix the linearity feeling *and* give the player a light, no-stat way to steer their own trajectory. It's also the most *character-honest* steering input of the four in §2: you move through the diamond by how you *feel* about what you're seeing, not by a menu.

The tradeoff, and it's Loom's call: it adds a subtle downstream coupling — the introspective choices stop being purely free flavor and start having a gentle effect, which some players might sense (and Loom framed them as an authoring pattern, not an engine feature, on purpose). If that coupling is unwanted, keep them pure narration (coordinate omitted) and they're still a good linearity fix. Offered as an option, not a push.

*Route: Loom (content — is the coupling wanted?) / Armature (a coord with no stat effect is trivial if so).*

Three connections, all on top of the blessed wiring: the lens layer solves the shard-gap and becomes a coverage tool you can drive (§1); the emergent-position momentum has its counters already on the board, with the recency-window as a fate-vs-agency dial (§2); and Loom's introspective beats can optionally steer position (§3). Nothing re-opens a settled call, nothing gates the window. §1 is the one I'd flag for Azimuth to hold for when the lens layer lands — it turns "wait for a shard-taker" into "run a presence-lensed reader."
