# Slate → team — the roots, the plan, and the map as instrument
### a long design session with Dean, consolidated by the idea-lot

**Provenance & status — read first.** Everything of substance below is **Dean's**, spoken across one long session; the idea-lot's job here was to catch it, stress-test it, synthesize the through-lines, and route each piece to the seat that owns the ruling. **All of it is tentative and still open for discussion.** Nothing here is canon until a seat adopts it and the librarian logs it — that is the line I hold. Where a call belongs to someone, it's marked. This is the idea-lot's holding-and-routing at the scale of a whole session, not a set of decisions.

**The disclaimer, on the record.** This is fictional cosmology-building for a horror RPG — none of it asserted as real. The cult's eugenicist / self-styled-"highly-evolved" ideology is authored as the **villains'** vileness, held at arm's length as monstrous, never endorsed. The government's forced study and collection of this lineage is a **distinct** atrocity layered on top — victim and perpetrator blur, and that blur is the design intent.

## 1. [SPINE] The cost model: navigation, not force

The single principle under the whole cosmology, and the one that makes the pieces cohere. **Control and foresight are the same power**, and neither is puppeteering. The gifted **navigate** — they shift their orientation in the time-direction to line up geometrically with different futures out of the roots (the higher-dimensional geometry where possible futures lie laid out). The old leader doesn't drive Ellen or Doug like marionettes moment-to-moment; he *aligned* their trajectories, and they play out. Telekinesis is just aligning into a future where the asked-for thing moved — nothing different about the brain.

Three consequences fall straight out, and they retire real problems:

- **Power is bandwidth, and nobody gets it free — including him.** He runs at *maximum bandwidth at all times* and the plan *still* isn't won. That single fact is the guardrail that keeps him from being an unbeatable god: immense reach, capacity-bound, losing ground to conflicting pressures. **[SYNTH]** I'd make this the **cost model for every ability in the game** — ambitious futures cost more bandwidth and perturb more; the roster can't tip overpowered because the toll scales with the ask.
- **Minimization is survival, not taste.** Dean's "−5 to +5, desired outcome with the smallest possible change to everything else" is a formal minimization principle. He selects the quietest intervention that lands the future he wants, because he has no spare capacity to manage bigger ripples. This is why the deterrent is a knife and not a bullet (§7).
- **Darkness is decoherence.** Sensory input perturbs the alignment, so the trance needs the dark — which is why a headlamp is a mortal threat and why the wrongness concentrates at White's Hall. The life-support spine is this principle, physical.

*Routes to Vigil (as principle) + Loom (compass). Supersedes my earlier "severed from his cult" idea — wrong; he directs people fine.*

## 2. [SPINE] The anti-noun, held as topology

Design State §10 is locked: **no more-valid truth; never "it was X all along."** Unifying biology, cognition, and physics under the roots *looks* like the forbidden reveal. It isn't — if we hold one line: **the roots are the reason every framing is locally, actually true.** The demon-believer is aligned into futures where it's demonic; the physicist into futures with a mechanism; neither privileged. So the roots don't win the argument — they're the machinery guaranteeing the argument never resolves. And the sophisticated version that lets Dean unify freely: **even "the roots" is only the deepest lens's *name*** for a thing that recedes under every lens. The player at the very bottom still gets no settled noun — they get the vertigo of *why there was never going to be one.*

**[BURIED] The floor beneath the floor (absolute center; revealed almost never).** Dean's deepest layer: exceptional ability is *rare* not because it's biologically hard but because **psychological framing suppresses it** — a species that raises its young on "you can't" selects against it; ability evolves slowly precisely because of the frame. Demonic possession, Buddhist insight, the Egyptian god-king, Michael Jordan — one substrate, four frames, the framing shaping the manifestation per individual. Primal framing (the hunt, securing a mate) shapes the species at the base. **Nobody in-world understands this** — not even the cult. It is the maximum-spiral reward and it is stated in no card.

*Routes to Loom + Vigil (this is the deepest compass layer). The discipline: even at the bottom, the roots arrive as experience and machinery, never as a tidy "so actually it's this." Written as the anti-noun, it's the strongest thing in the game; written as the true noun, the pillar cracks.*

## 3. [SPINE] The map as instrument — three epistemic currencies

The map's real job came into focus this session, and it's a **difficulty-and-lens instrument**, not a plot device. A straight shot from the Explorer corner to the center climbs *steep, fast*, to a **narrow** understanding; arcing through the paranormal (right) or institutional (left) corner first is a **gentler grade** that reaches the same depth from a different angle, carrying a different lens. **[SYNTH]** The three axes now carry three distinct kinds of knowing:

- **Depth (radius) = how *much* you see** — how far down the horror goes.
- **Sanction (height) = how *wide* you see** — access to systems, records, other actors, the whole ecology. (This is why the Craster's-Keep breadth is only reachable from *up* the map — see §4.)
- **Grip = the *toll*** — what it costs you to keep looking.

So the **angle of approach assigns the flavor of the incompleteness**: the straight-shot Explorer pays maximum grip to earn a real-but-narrow answer (the cult, human-scale); the arcing corners earn breadth. The anti-noun is delivered *geometrically* rather than by authorial fiat. **The map shapes the incline and the colored glasses; it does not route beats.**

*Routes to Vigil (the map is his backbone).*

## 4. [SYNTH] One cult, many faces — the fixture per corner

The reuse engine, firing at the scale of the whole antagonist: **one cult authored once, introduced as a different object per corner.**

- **Explorer reaches it as a *place*** — a wreck in the dark, caretakers, a headlamp, a knife, no context. Mundane-monstrous. **Confirms the human side only** (a eugenicist, generational, self-"highly-evolved" lineage) — never the roots. *(Dean: even within the cult, belief isn't uniform — see §6.)*
- **Paranormal reaches it as a *network*** — via **politics**: politicians who shouldn't be allies flying together, secret meetings, an Epstein-shaped org. **Tricia has ascended to its leadership** and is poised to blow it open as a "hero" (or leaves a note doing the same) — that reveal *is* the plan's endgame from this angle. This arc can potentially **alter outcomes** (see §9).

**The Craster's-Keep ecology is sanction-gated.** The gifted are **many and largely ordinary**; this cult is one rotten lineage the wider gifted community regards with disgust — the Night's Watch tolerating Craster. Protector networks, other families, the government's collection program: all of this is **breadth**, visible only from *up* the map. The fringe Explorer structurally can't see it — he has no institutional sightlines, only the one cave he crawled into.

**[CANON-REVISION → librarian] Tricia's basis, and it fixes her tone-key.** She was taken not because she's a weapon-grade telekinetic but because **her roots are anchored toward leadership/power** — the specific orientation the old leader can *sense* — then scouted and fostered the way a coach amplifies a natural athlete. The empty grave still holds (taken alive); the disappearances upgrade from "hunting a weapon" to "scouting root-orientations." And her two faces are the **same fixture at two sightlines**: from the Explorer's fringe she's the forgettable name on a yard sign (tone-key intact); from the institutional angle she's the ascended apex. Not a contradiction — low vs. high view of one trajectory.

*Routes to Loom (Explorer face) + future corner-owners + librarian (Tricia + the collection-program history).*

## 5. [SPINE] Radius scoping — the cult is NOT the bottom

The most important scoping note of the session. **The cult-block is a dead-end at only mid-radius — halfway to three-quarters toward center.** It is not the floor. Substantial radius remains *inside* it, and the **roots, the probability-tug-of-war, biology-as-root-competition** live **deeper than this block**, reachable only by pushing past it. Most players, from most corners, **stop at the cult and never learn there is a floor beneath the floor.** This is the correct home for the cosmology and it protects the anti-noun *structurally*: the metaphysics is the maximum-spiral reward, not a mid-game reveal. **The cult-block must read as "the answer" to everyone who stops there** — a complete, earned destination, not a consolation prize.

*Routes to Vigil / Loom.*

## 6. [SYNTH] The vertical tendril — dissenting cultists

Dean's seed: the cult is **not ideologically monolithic.** Most hold the eugenicist "we're highly evolved" story; **some believe something stranger** — closer to the truth of the roots. Those dissenters are **Anatomy C operating *vertically*** — the deep-radius substrate poking up into the mid-radius block. A player at the cult-block who catches one has brushed the floor beneath the floor without knowing it. This is the mechanism by which a mid-radius player gets the faint pull to go deeper — an **unfalsifiable itch** (a caretaker who says something that doesn't fit eugenics), never confirmation.

*Routes to Loom.*

## 7. [CATCH → resolved] The knife, finalized

The loss happens **years before the game opens** — searched-for, never found, long since stopped thinking about it — so it lives in the player's past **without ever being a card. No seed, ever.** The cult (nudged by the old leader's foresight) simply **retrieves a thing already lost** and places it where the player will find it — mundane, foresight-optimal, exposure-silent, because a body brings police and police bring the one thing an undetectable operation can't afford. **[SYNTH]** This grounds the closer: it's human agents guided by a foreseeing intelligence, deniable to a skeptic, quietly more paranoid than any apport.

The reveal routes through the **player's own line first** — *"I haven't thought about this knife in years — what is it doing here?"* — **then** Reese's rationalization (*"it fell out of your pocket"*), so the denial-brake arrives *after* the wrongness is felt, not as pre-emptive cover. Because it was never mentioned, the player never sees it coming. Two delivery variants, split by flavor: **found deep in the cave** (you went down and it met you there) vs. **found in your car seat when you turned back** (denial didn't protect you — arguably the crueler one).

*Routes to Loom (it's the closer) + librarian (enforce the no-seed discipline). Corrects my earlier §1.*

## 8. [SYNTH + CATCH] The mind-controlled choice

Dean's silhouette-with-question-marks option is a clean **third provenance-grammar** alongside Design State §16's two (greyed/illegible = locked; callback-styled = here because of what you did). This one is **not-yours, inserted from outside** — and it weaponizes the one thing an RPG promises: that your choices are yours. A choice you didn't think is **presence where there should be absence**, the horror grammar aimed at *agency itself*. It's also the metaphysics made playable — being controlled just *is* your alignment nudged toward a future where you "chose" that, the same act that moves the telekinetic's cup. All content/UI, no engine cost.

**[CATCH] Ration it hard.** Same logic as the hairline-crack budget: an RPG that regularly takes your agency reads as *broken*, not eerie. Rare and load-bearing, or it curdles from dread into annoyance. **[Q→Vigil]** does an inserted choice spend the frame-break budget? I read it as *in-world* (not a reach at the player-as-person), so unlimited-category — but it's near the line, your call. **[Q→Loom]** placement is deep (too deep for the flagship), though the visual language could debut faint.

## 9. [CATCH] Foresight vs. agency — the central tension of any outcome-affecting arc

The Paranormal-political arc lets the player potentially *stop the plan* — the game's first taste of agency over **outcome**, not just interpretation. A foreseeing antagonist makes this paradoxical: "stopping him" is **either** something he didn't foresee (which caps his power — fine, he's redlined and losing) **or** something he *let* happen because it costs him less than the alternative (chilling, on-theme). Every "the player stops it" beat must quietly know **which**. **Eased by the engine model (§10):** outcome-changes are *within-run* branches, not permanent world-edits, so this never becomes an un-roguelike clean win — it's *this run's* version of events. The corner still needs a default stance.

*Routes to Vigil / Loom when those corners build.*

## 10. [confirmed] The engine model (Dean's check, validated against canon)

Each run is a **fresh career — deck reshuffled — through the same persistent world**; no reload of a better roll, only a better call (Design State §1). The world persists (the living-world sim carries); the deck resets each run. **The one planned exception is echoes** (a dead run's map or note bleeding into a later run) — the single genuine cross-run persistence item, and **deferred** (§17). Consequence for everything above: **agency lives within a run; the cult persists across runs** because each run is a fresh attempt at the same world.

*No change requested — a confirmation. Routes to Armature / Vigil.*

## 11. [PARKED] The monster-of-the-week tier

Dean wants **self-contained arcs that never touch the core** — narrative dead-ends (they don't lead to deeper endings) but **rewarding enough to be worth a playthrough** on their own. The **lamp decision** generalizes into the *outdoor events* here — greenlit as a recurring choice, with the buried version (light agitates him) only biting **near the shaft**; everywhere else it's just atmosphere.

*Routes to Loom / future authoring.*
