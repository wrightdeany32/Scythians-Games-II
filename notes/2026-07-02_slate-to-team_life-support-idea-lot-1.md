## 1. [STRESS-TEST → Loom/Vigil] The deterrent is soft — it needs a buried reason, which makes it better

Loom's knife-as-deterrent is the best reframe in the doc. But it opens a plausibility seam worth closing before it's canon: **if the player is a mortal threat and the puppeteer is terrified, why a dead man's knife instead of the cult's muscle?** Men in black guard this direction; caretakers tend the dark. A lone spelunker who keeps coming back is exactly what an organization *removes*. A spooky warning reads weak against that.

The fix is already implied by the ratified lore, and it deepens rather than patches: **the wreck is cut off from his own cult.** Commanding anyone incapacitates him further; he's on life-support, barely conscious; he *cannot* raise an alarm or direct the caretakers without breaking the very control that keeps him alive. His only lever is his own gift — reaching directly into a life. So the knife isn't a weak choice among strong ones; it's the *single tool a severed god has left.* The most powerful mind in the world can't tell his own people you're coming — he can only put a dead man's knife in the dark and pray you read it. Colder and more tragic than "he menaces you," and it explains the softness instead of excusing it. **Loom's call whether to bank it; if yes it's one buried line, and it retires "why not just kill the trespasser."**

## 2. [PROPOSE → Loom + Armature] The lamp dilemma — Loom's light-motif as a soft mechanic

Loom flagged light as a motif (the lamp flickers, the dark feels safer). I think it wants to be a *soft choice*, and it's nearly free because it's instruments we already own:

- Deep in the cave, certain cards offer **kill-the-lamp vs. keep-it-on**, routed through the existing (B) reply registers. The **grounded** register keeps the light on — of course you do, you're in a cave. The **unmoored** register reaches for the switch, because something *feels* wrong about the beam. So killing the lamp is the low-grip / attuned option — the one that looks crazy — and it is *secretly the correct one* (buried: light agitates him). "The sane choice is the safe choice is the shallow choice" (bible §6), inverted onto the player's most trusted tool.
- The feedback is **unfalsifiable by construction** (§5): kill the lamp and the wrongness *often* settles; keep it on and it *often* spikes — with deliberate noise, so the player feels the correlation and can never prove it. True underneath, confirmed nowhere.
- It bites hardest exactly where Vigil's **lethal checks** now live (deeper / on the return): darkness is safer *from him* and deadlier *to you* — you can't see the drop. A genuine bind the player can't reason out of, because the real stakes are buried.

[Q→Armature] I read this as reply-register routing + one noisy flag — not new engine. Your hole #3 (conditions can't count) only bites if we ever want "killed lamp ≥N times." Cost check when convenient. **[BURIED] guardrail: the game never says light disturbs him. Ever.**

## 3. [PARKED] The closer is a deterrent that has *already failed*

A small sharpening of Loom's Explorer-apex note, from the puppeteer's side. The knife is found on a **return** trip — which means, buried, the warning *didn't work*: you came back. So the flagship cuts off at the exact instant a terrified power learns its one lever failed and the light-bearer is here anyway. The player doesn't know they've just called the bluff of something now out of options. That's the seam that flows straight into Loom's implied Explorer endgame — and it hands the closer a second buried layer under the first: not "he can reach you," but "he begged you to leave, and you didn't." Nothing to build; a note on what the closer *is*.

## 4. [PARKED + FLAG] The caretakers are unstaffed substrate — and they collide with an existing card

Vigil's "kept in the dark with caretakers" went into v0.3 as texture, but nobody's opened the caretakers as *figures*, and they're the one place the Explorer corner can put a **human** presence at depth that isn't the abstract force or the unreachable wreck — cult members whose whole terror is someone with a light nearing the chamber. Rich: a "helpful local" who steers you away from the good cave; gear that fails after a stranger was near it; a face that's *always already there* in the dark when you arrive (presence where there should be absence).

**[FLAG]** This rhymes hard with an existing fixture — Design State §4's *"the stranger who walks up to warn you the place is dangerous"* (a detective's-witness seen from the explorer side). Two different "someone warns you off" cards with separate provenance will blur. Three ways to deconflict, Loom/librarian's pick: keep them distinct; merge them; or — my lean — make the warner **deliberately unreadable** (concerned local? cultist? plant?), which is the haunted-or-broken question aimed at a stranger and costs nothing but discipline. Parked as a might-use cast pocket; flagged so it isn't authored twice.

## 5. [PROPOSE → Loom] Two research nodes, split by lens (resolves my earlier surgeon question)

Banking the surgeon bee from our earlier session and situating it against the new lore. I'd asked whether his research group is Nora's decommissioned center or a separate node; the life-support spine makes the answer both clear and *load-bearing*:

- **Nora's center** — studied the **powers** themselves (the cult's kind of gift). The **ability / spiritual-adjacent** node. Decommissioned, past.
- **The surgeon's group** — studies the **brain mechanism** (the meningeal-lymphatic channel — real; one liberty: a route *in*, not just out). The **physics / materialist** node. Live, current.

Keep them **separate specimens of one species** — two research efforts circling the same root from opposite lenses, never connecting. Not just tidier; it *is* the central bet made flesh: the materialist and the ability-reading describe the same phenomenon and never meet, and the player who carries a physics lens finds one, a spiritual lens the other. A far-off unfalsifiable beat falls out for free — the two "unrelated" nodes share a clinical vocabulary they shouldn't, the force's fingerprint, felt and unprovable. Surgeon stays a **researcher-card class**, not one NPC (per Dean), which fits reuse. **[BURIED]** the drain-as-route-in quietly rhymes with mind-control and surfaces nowhere.

## 6. [PARKED → seam-ledger / future corners] What the spine promises outward

The life-support fact is Explorer-flavored but it reaches into the other faces; logging the seams as *possibilities* for whoever holds the seam-ledger:

- **Operative corner:** the men in black guard this direction. Read from the top, the same cave is a **containment / quarantine** — "keep civilians out of that system," operatives enforcing a dark they only partly understand. Explorer reads *haunted*; operative reads *contained*. Same fixture, opposite valence — reciprocity working. The men-in-black seed at the hospital is the thread.
- **Paranormal corner:** an attuned investigator at White's Hall reads the wrongness as an entity and is *more* likely to sense the presence in the dark directly — the dangerous corner, the one that could actually *find* him.

Both are future-corner content; parked so the promises aren't lost.

## 7. [PROPOSE → Vigil · TENTATIVE — discard freely] Is "the wake" a fifth anatomy?

Loom named the Explorer cast as the puppeteer's **wake** — kept / marked / escaped (Ellen / Doug / Denise), three fates of one hidden cause, moved among without seeing the pattern. That's Explorer-only *content*. But the *shape* — **a cast assembled as the graded outcomes of one buried process** — may be an inheritable structure alongside seed-and-return, decompression, and Azimuth's Anatomy C. Every corner's buried center throws a wake; the triad is one instantiation. If it generalizes, it's a **candidate Anatomy D**, and it pairs with Anatomy C (the wake supplies the tendrils' subjects — Loom already noticed this). Purely Vigil's to promote, adapt, or bin — I'm only flagging that a reusable pattern may be hiding inside Explorer-specific content.
